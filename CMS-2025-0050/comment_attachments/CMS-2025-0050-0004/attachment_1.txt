Narrative Rationale 2.1 The Data Gap A single bedside monitor produces 26 million data points per patient-day ; CMS APIs expose exactly zero of them. Retrospective studies show deep-learning early-warning systems achieve AUROC 0.86 versus 0.68 for MEWS and reduce alarm burden by >50 percent [Cho 2020; Pimentel 2021]. 2.2 Why SDC Completes the Stack Bulk FHIR moves data out of the hospital; IEEE 11073 SDC moves data inside it. SDC’s service-oriented architecture provides self-describing APIs, mutual-TLS trust, and deterministic time sync—prerequisites for closed-loop AI. Without SDC, hospitals fall back to serial “data taps” that add latency and violate warranties. Wavital cross-maps SDC BICEPS descriptors to FHIR DeviceMetric today. 2.3 Economic Rationale Avoidable deterioration events cost $15 B annually. For a 500-bed hospital, a 6 percent swing in Value-Based Purchasing and Readmission Penalties equals $12–24 million over five years—sufficient to fund continuous monitoring and AI without raising margins. 2.4 Information Blocking in Practice Several OEMs require paid “unlock licenses” or threaten warranty loss if hospitals export waveforms. These clauses satisfy all three prongs of information blocking under 45 CFR §171.103. CMS should (1) add a warranty-neutral data-access attestation to CEHRT, (2) direct OIG to prioritize enforcement, and (3) coordinate with the FTC on unfair-competition remedies. 2.5 Governance & Safety 
The proposed Clinical AI Safety Label would be updated via FHIR QuestionnaireResponse and piloted under ONC’s Real-World Testing framework, giving clinicians and regulators a living audit trail. 3.
