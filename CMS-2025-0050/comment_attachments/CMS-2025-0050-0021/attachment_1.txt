As a healthcare professional, I recognize the transformative potential of Clinical AI in improving
documentation integrity, care quality, and operational efficiency across our healthcare system.
AI-assisted Clinical Documentation Integrity tools can streamline chart reviews, identify
undocumented diagnoses, and improve the accuracy of diagnosis-related group assignment and
risk adjustment, which are critical for value-based care models. According to a 2023 JAMA
study, AI enhanced CDI programs led to a 22 percent increase in appropriate severity capture
and a 14 percent reduction in documentation omissions. However, AI systems must be used with
caution, as they can misinterpret clinical nuance, generate compliance risk queries, or contribute
to provider burnout through excessive alerts. Additionally, reliance on AI without human clinical
oversight may undermine professional judgment and introduce unintended bias. CMS should
encourage innovation in AI while ensuring transparency, clinical oversight, and strict adherence
to established CDI and query guidelines to make sure AI supports rather than compromises
patient centered, ethical, and data informed care.
