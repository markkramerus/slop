Agilian Government Solutions (AGS) welcomes this RFI and urges CMS to prioritize bold,
actionable policy steps that reduce administrative waste and improve beneficiary experience
particularly in the context of Medicaid and Medicare renewals.

As policymakers consider moving from annual to semi-annual redeterminations, we see an

urgent need to replace outdated, paper-based processes with automated, Al-enhanced, digital-
first solutions that leverage existing infrastructure and commercial innovations.

Policy Recommendations: Make Renewal Smarter, Not Just More Frequent

1. Fund automation and Al-driven renewal workflows

CMS should encourage and fund solutions that:

Auto-populate renewal forms with existing eligibility data (e.g., SNAP, TANF, SSA);
Use Al to guide users through complex renewal questions in plain language;

Deploy intelligent reminders via SMS, email, or app notification;
Flag incomplete renewals in real-time and nudge users with next steps.

Al-enabled digital assistants can reduce error rates, minimize friction, and support beneficiaries
with low literacy or limited tech experience—at scale.

Z, Leverage and extend existing infrastructure investments

CMS and states have already invested heavily in Medicaid Enterprise Systems (MES), the
Federal Data Services Hub, and Blue Button 2.0. CMS should:

« Provide integration standards and reusable APIs for digital renewals;
« Incentivize states to build renewal tools that plug into existing eligibility engines;
« Promote plattorm-agnostic architectures that can scale across states.

Let’s stop reinventing the wheel. Use what's already built—and make it smarter.
4. Promote interoperability with commercial digital products

Digital health shouldn't start from scratch. CMS should identify leading-edge commercial
products—think mobile apps, identity credentialing platforms, chatbot frameworks—and
encourage states to partner with them, not compete against them.

« These tools are already HIPAA-compliant, user-tested, and cloud-native.

« Open standards like FHIR and SMART on FHIR can facilitate secure data exchange and
integration.

« CMS can curate a list of pre-qualified vendors and reusable components to streamline
procurement.
