Comment from Agilian Government Solutions Re: CMS-0042-NC – RFI on the Health Technology Ecosystem Submitted via regulations.gov Agilian Government Solutions (AGS) welcomes this RFI and urges CMS to prioritize bold, actionable policy steps that reduce administrative waste and improve beneficiary experience— particularly in the context of Medicaid and Medicare renewals. As policymakers consider moving from annual to semi-annual redeterminations, we see an urgent need to replace outdated, paper-based processes with automated, AI-enhanced, digital- first solutions that leverage existing infrastructure and commercial innovations. Policy Recommendations: Make Renewal Smarter, Not Just More Frequent 1. Fund automation and AI-driven renewal workflows CMS should encourage and fund solutions that: •Auto-populate renewal forms with existing eligibility data (e.g., SNAP, TANF, SSA); •Use AI to guide users through complex renewal questions in plain language; •Deploy intelligent reminders via SMS, email, or app notification; •Flag incomplete renewals in real-time and nudge users with next steps. AI-enabled digital assistants can reduce error rates, minimize friction, and support beneficiaries with low literacy or limited tech experience—at scale. 2. Leverage and extend existing infrastructure investments CMS and states have already invested heavily in Medicaid Enterprise Systems (MES), the Federal Data Services Hub, and Blue Button 2.0. CMS should: •Provide integration standards and reusable APIs for digital renewals; •Incentivize states to build renewal tools that plug into existing eligibility engines; •Promote platform-agnostic architectures that can scale across states. Let’s stop reinventing the wheel. Use what’s already built—and make it smarter. 3. Promote interoperability with commercial digital products Digital health shouldn’t start from scratch. CMS should identify leading-edge commercial products—think mobile apps, identity credentialing platforms, chatbot frameworks—and encourage states to partner with them, not compete against them. •These tools are already HIPAA-compliant, user-tested, and cloud-native.
•Open standards like FHIR and SMART on FHIR can facilitate secure data exchange and integration. •CMS can curate a list of pre-qualified vendors and reusable components to streamline procurement. 4. Support equity with a "Digital Participation Fund" As we digitize, we must not leave vulnerable communities behind. CMS should create a competitive grant program for states to: •Provide mobile-friendly renewal support in multiple languages; •Ensure compatibility with assistive technologies; •Offer public-private partnerships for mobile data subsidies, broadband, and device access. Access isn’t just about the software—it’s about getting the software into people’s hands. 5. Quantify the cost of inaction To strengthen the policy case, CMS should analyze and publish the national cost of paper-based redeterminations—factoring in printing, postage, returned mail, call center volume, and avoidable churn. Model this against the cost of deploying scalable digital tools. We suspect the return on investment will be clear and compelling. Final Word Administrative churn is not an inevitable cost of doing business—it’s a fixable policy failure. CMS has the authority, tools, and partners to lead a transition from legacy mailing cycles to automated, intelligent, digital renewal systems that reduce waste and improve access to care. Let’s treat this not as a technology problem, but as an equity and efficiency opportunity. Sincerely, Agilian Government Solutions
