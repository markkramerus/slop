Subject: Public Comment on Digital Infrastructure Needs to Support Value-Based Care
To the CMS and ONC teams,
Thank you for the opportunity to respond to the Request for Information on modernizing the
Medicare health technology ecosystem. As a company working closely with independent
provider organizations—particularly Community Health Centers (CHCs), provider-led ACOs, and
regional CINs—we deeply support CMS’s vision of improving care coordination, transparency,
and equity through digital infrastructure.
We write today to respond specifically to the section of the RFI focused on Value-Based Care
(VBC) and the role that digital infrastructure plays in advancing its success.
Key Recommendations for VBC Enablement
1. Create enforceable data standards that ensure real-time, two-way data exchange
between payers and providers.
Providers in VBC arrangements often lack timely access to the very data they are measured
on—claims, attribution, quality results, and benchmark performance. One CHC partner of ours,
participating in both Medicaid MCO and MSSP programs, experienced a 90-day delay in

receiving performance data, only to learn they had missed an incentive opportunity due to
outdated rosters and unaccounted exclusions. These gaps aren’t just frustrating—they are
financially and operationally damaging.
We recommend CMS:
● Establish minimum standards for structured, real-time data exchange in VBC
programs
● Incentivize or require payers to share quality and financial performance data at
monthly or quarterly intervals
● Include provider-facing APIs and flat file formats that do not require EHR
customization or additional licenses
2. Prioritize CHC and safety-net provider needs in digital health infrastructure
investments.
CHCs serve over 30 million patients—yet they’re often excluded from interoperability
discussions due to limited resources or vendor support. Several of our CHC clients struggle with
inconsistent payer file formats, missing risk scores, or performance logic that can’t be
independently validated.
CMS should:
● Fund and promote shared data infrastructure at the regional or PCA/HCCN level
● Encourage open performance scoring methodologies to reduce dependence on
opaque vendor tools
● Promote “plug-and-play” tooling for small and mid-sized organizations to analyze and act
on data
Note: Syntax Health currently supports several provider organizations—including CHCs,
academic faculty practices, and ACO-aligned networks—with actuarial modeling, contract
performance tools, and actionable analytics that simplify VBC execution. This work has
demonstrated that when providers have access to timely, interpretable data, they can renegotiate
contracts, avoid missed incentives, and improve outcomes—all without increasing administrative
burden.
3. Reduce administrative burden by supporting integrated VBC tooling.

Digital infrastructure should not add to the reporting burden—it should reduce it. We see
provider organizations managing VBC contracts in dozens of spreadsheets and PDFs, leading
to errors and missed opportunities.
We recommend CMS:
● Recognize or certify tools that integrate contracting, performance measurement, and
financial reconciliation
● Encourage modular infrastructure that supports providers of all sizes—not just IDNs
with in-house tech teams
● Consider demonstration projects that validate how digital tools improve provider
experience and outcomes in VBC
4. Center equity and transparency in VBC data infrastructure.
Medicare’s digital health transformation must serve all providers and patients. This includes:
● Publishing benchmarks and scoring algorithms used to determine shared savings
● Ensuring attribution logic and risk adjustment are accessible, explainable, and
updatable
● Supporting VBC enablement for providers serving rural and underserved populations,
including CHCs, tribal clinics, and Medicaid-focused ACOs
Closing Thoughts
Radical change requires practical people. We urge CMS and ONC to design this modernization
effort with real-world providers in mind—especially those serving high-need populations. A more
transparent, timely, and equitable digital ecosystem is not just a technology issue—it’s
foundational to Medicare’s success in value-based care.
We are grateful for your leadership and would welcome future opportunities to collaborate.
