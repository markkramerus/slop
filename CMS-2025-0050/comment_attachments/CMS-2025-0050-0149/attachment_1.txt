Response to CMS Request for Information on Interoperability and Health IT Advancement Submitted by: Fredric Santiago, SAFI Clinical Informatics Group LLC Topic: Enabling FHIR Adoption through Clinician Education Thank you for the opportunity to respond to this important Request for Information concerning strategies to improve interoperability and health care quality , particularly in light of the 21st Century Cures Act and the activities of HL7 FHIR Accelerator groups. While the RFI’s questions appropriately center on technical implementation, scalability , and the alignment of standards like FHIR with policy goals, we believe a foundational issue is not being directly addressed: Does the clinical workforce have the necessary knowledge and training to support widespread FHIR implementation? We submit that the greatest barrier to the success of FHIR-enabled interoperability is not technical, but educational . As confirmed by the 2022 Global Survey by HL7 and Firely , the most frequently cited barrier to FHIR adoption is a lack of familiarity with the FHIR standard , particularly outside developer communities. This gap is especially acute among clinicians and clinical informatics professionals—the very individuals whose workflows FHIR-based solutions are designed to support. Without a workforce that understands the standard in context, implementation efforts risk being technically compliant but clinically misaligned. 
Since 2023, our organization has worked to bridge this gap by creating FHIR training tailored for clinicians, clinical informaticians, and health IT professionals . We do not train developers—we train those who must evaluate, guide, and adopt technology solutions within clinical settings. In our view, empowering this workforce with working knowledge of FHIR is the first step toward successful implementation and sustainable use. We respectfully propose that CMS, in partnership with ASTP/ONC and other stakeholders, consider the following: ● Fund or endorse educational programs that focus on FHIR training for clinicians and clinical informatics professionals. ● Incorporate workforce readiness metrics into evaluations of FHIR adoption and implementation progress. ● Encourage collaboration between FHIR Accelerator projects and clinical training organizations to ensure alignment between standards development and clinical workflows. By investing in the education and empowerment of the clinical workforce, CMS can ensure that FHIR is not only implemented, but implemented well—supporting safe, effective, and interoperable care. Thank you for your leadership in advancing interoperability . We welcome the opportunity to contribute to this effort.
