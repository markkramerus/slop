GENERAL COMMENTS High Quality Real -World Data The most clinically relevant evidence of safety , efficacy , and value for any medical product or protocol is to be found in high quality real world datasets. Such primary d atasets are in stark contrast to synthetic compendia of EMR, claims, prescription , revenue cycle management codes and 
 Response to Request For Information June 9, 2025 Page 4 of 21 Copyright © 2025 RegenMed, Inc. other “big data” solutions . High quality real-world datasets are define d by a single anatomical region, pathology, treatment protocol, and long terms outcome assessment. They are verifiable to primary sources – treating physicians and their patients -- and are inherently structured to deliver causal correlations among clinical hypothesis, treatments, and long -term outcomes for specific patient cohorts . There are over one billion patient physician interactions each year in the U .S. Each of these represents longitudinal real-world data f ar more relevant for clinical decision making and VBC than reimbursement codes , drug prescriptions, or insurance claims. Modern technology enables the generation of genuine real world evidence for any particular health/wellness approach, rivaling in quality and clinical relevance that of much more expensive clinical trials. High Quality RWE Represents Impactful Medical Research The large majority of clinical trials today can be afforded only by well -capitalized pharmaceutical and medical device companies. Also, many peer -reviewed published studies are coming under increasing scrutiny for alleged bias. Even when such bias does not exist, the underlying raw data is rarely available. Medical literature (despite its accelerating volume) fails to address many if not most health/wellness issues in a clinically relevant and timely manner. In addition, NIH and other governmental inst itutions are substantially reducing research grants. Meanwhile, physicians are -- as they have always been -- the most important source of clinical hypotheses which can lead to innovative products, diagnostic tools, and treatment protocols having a broad imp act for all Americans. Those hypotheses arise in every day medical practices. Critically, the data needed to test those hypotheses arise in those same settings. Impact of Recent Regulations and Rules. As indicated in this RFI, the past years have see n a number of well -intentioned legislative and rule - making attempts to ensure the deployment of “effective digital capabilities needed to make informed health decisions, and increasing data availability for all stakeholders contributing to health outcomes” . We respectfully submit that Circles can make a major difference in achieving those goals – with minimal cost and excellent patient and provider user experience.
