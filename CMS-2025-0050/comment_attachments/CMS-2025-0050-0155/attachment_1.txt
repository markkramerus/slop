Should CMS endorse non -CMS data sources and networks, and if so, what criteria or metrics should CMS consider? Yes, CMS should consider endorsing non- CMS data sources and networks , such as health information exchanges (HIEs), community care hubs, and other trusted data platforms , but only if these platforms meet clear standards for data quality, interoperability, privacy, and utility. H igh-performing, non- CMS data sources can expand access to timely, relevant health information, reduce duplication of effort, and improve care coordination for Medicare beneficiaries. CMS should consider the following criteria or metri cs for endorsement: • Interoperability Compliance: The data source should adhere to nationally recognized interoperability standards (e.g., HL7 FHIR, USCDI) and be able to exchange data effectively with CMS systems and other networks. • Data Quality and Completeness: The network should demonstrate consistent, high- quality data with minimal gaps, including timely updates, standardized terminologies, and accurate patient matching. • Privacy and Security Protections: The organization must comply with HIPAA and other federal data protection regulations, with robust safeguards to ensure data privacy and security. • Consumer Access and Transparency: The platform should enable patients and caregivers to access their data easily and support mechanisms for consent, data sharing, and correction of inaccuracies. • Governance and Accountability: CMS should evaluate whether the organization has transparent governance structures, clear data use policies, and processes for resolving disputes or data integrity issues. • Proven Impact and Use Cases: Preference could be given to data sources that have demonstrated real-world value, such as improving care coordination, reducing readmissions, supporting public health reporting, or enabling analytics for underserved populations. • Accurate Representation: CMS should consider whether the data source includes diverse populations and addresses disparities in access, care, and outcomes, particularly in rural, underserved, or underrepresented communities.
