1.2 Federated, Credential-Bound Identity for National Scale

● acr: assurance level
● auth_time: authentication timestamp
● trust_level: issuer-defined trust classification
● Signed FHIR Consent and OAuth tokens
● Homomorphic tokenization (512-float vectors, non-reversible) for deduplication across
ACA, CHIP, and Medicaid
Private Identity, as a CARIN Alliance Member, promotes federated credentials that are portable,
verifiable, and fully privacy-preserving.

2.3 Consent That Can Be Proven—and Revoked
We use FHIR R4 Consent resources as cryptographic enforcement tools. Each consent
record:
● Specifies actors (provision.actor), data (provision.data), and purpose
(provision.purpose)
● Is signed using JWS and linked to an OAuth session
● Is dynamically enforceable through token introspection and Consent.status
All data access is logged using FHIR AuditEvent and Provenance, producing CMS-readable
NDJSON exports.
When a patient revokes consent, the token becomes invalid in real time—enabling CMS to meet
the revocation and intent-binding mandates in CMS-9884-P and TEFCA.
As a CARIN Alliance Member, we believe consent must be computable and revocable—not
passive.

4.2 Detecting Synthetic Identity Without Biometrics or Central Storage
iTrust Identity offers fraud detection without sacrificing privacy:
● Homomorphic tokens: On-device generation of 512-float, irreversible identity vectors
● No biometric storage: No images or biometric templates ever leave the user’s device

● Similarity scoring: Approximate deduplication using cosine distance or ANN across
ACA, Medicaid, and CHIP
● Cross-program deduplication: Federated CredentialMatch scores computed in real
time
● Client-side cryptography: Reduces attack surfaces and enforces local compliance
This allows CMS to identify duplicate or suspicious identities without storing or exposing any
sensitive data.
As a CARIN Alliance Member, we lead with privacy-first design to earn trust while
detecting fraud.
