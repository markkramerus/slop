Introduction
Polimorphic builds artificial intelligence tools that empower government agencies to deliver
faster, more accessible, and more equitable services. In health and human services (HHS) and
department of social services (DSS) settings, we streamline constituent communications and
casework using multimodal AI voice, chat, and backend CRM/workflow systems.
Our submission addresses key barriers and opportunities across patient-facing and
provider-facing workflows, with a focus on Medicaid and SNAP administration — programs with
significant overlap in population and process with Medicare. The same infrastructure is directly
transferable to Medicare customer-service and the Qualified Medicare Beneficiary (QMB)
program.
Section B: Patients and Caregivers
PC-1 / PC-4 / PC-5: Health Management and Care Navigation Solutions
Polimorphic deploys AI voice agents to handle inbound calls to HHS/DSS offices. These
agents answer high-volume questions related to application status, eligibility, document
requirements, and timelines — dramatically reducing hold times and staff burnout. They can
also escalate complex queries to staff.
We also offer chatbot, SMS, search, and email assistants that respond to constituent
inquiries with consistent, policy-aligned answers across channels.
For outbound use cases, Polimorphic supports AI-driven voice interviews for Medicaid and
SNAP applications and recertifications. These interviews typically follow structured scripts, often
handled by human caseworkers spending ~25% of their time in this role. Our AI voice system
can complete these interviews in multiple languages and flag applications that need human
review. While currently deployed for Medicaid/SNAP, these same tools are directly applicable to
Medicare customer service centers or digital navigation platforms.

PC-6: Accessibility and Usability
Polimorphic emphasizes accessible language, mobile responsiveness, and multilingual
capabilities in all constituent-facing tools. Our systems are designed to work for users who have
no prior digital experience, rely on mobile devices, or prefer to interact via voice. In testing,
voice-based tools achieved an increase in successful self-service among non-English-speaking
users.
Section C: Providers
PR-2 / PR-4 / PR-7: Reducing Burden through Automation
Our CRM and case management system digitizes intake forms, classifies and routes requests,
and enables status visibility across agencies and departments. We integrate AI review tools to
surface errors, missing documents, or eligibility for secondary programs, increasing both speed
and accuracy of case decisions.
For providers, auto-filled forms and pre-screened data from constituent-facing AI tools reduce
manual data entry and make scheduling and documentation more efficient. By eliminating
repetitive tasks, our tools free up clinical staff for direct care — an essential aspect of CMS’s
provider burden reduction goals.
Section D: Payers
PA-2 / PA-5: Data Sharing and Quality Improvement
Polimorphic's tools would benefit from expanded access to Medicaid eligibility and benefit usage
data. This would allow our AI tools to pre-fill applications, recommend additional programs, and
eliminate redundant paperwork.
We encourage CMS to extend the Blue Button API model to Medicaid and other
HHS-administered programs, and to include social determinant indicators (e.g., public housing
status, income history, recent hospitalizations) as opt-in data streams. With Medicaid usage data,
we estimate a 20–30% reduction in incomplete applications and an increase in program uptake.

Section E: Technology Vendors
TD-1 / TD-2 / TD-4: Unlocking Innovation
Polimorphic supports CMS efforts to improve access to certified APIs and encourages:
● Expansion of FHIR-based APIs to cover state Medicaid systems.
● Incentives for county governments to provide case information to authorized apps.
● Simplified credentialing for accessing TEFCA or HIE-aligned services.
Developers need access to richer data and clearer rules on usage rights to build truly helpful
tools. CMS could accelerate innovation by publishing standard legal frameworks for API access and
data-sharing with third-party vendors.
TD-9: Certification and Standards
Polimorphic supports redefining certification to emphasize API-enabled capabilities. Access to
real-time case updates, document uploads, and interview status via API would significantly
increase automation and constituent engagement. Certification should cover real-time status
change notifications, enabling apps to alert users instantly of key eligibility decisions.
Section F: Value-Based Care Organizations
VB-1 / VB-2 / VB-3: Enabling AI in APMs
Polimorphic tools assist in:
● Outreach to non-responsive beneficiaries through voice AI reminders.
● Intake triage for urgent issues and referrals.
● Automated documentation and status updates that reduce lag in care planning.
Our tools can flag high-risk or eligible patients for wraparound services and increase first-time
application completion rates. In recent deployments, our AI-driven outreach can increase first-time
application completion rates by 10 up to 50% (most applications have a ~70% initial error rate),
accelerating time to care.

Conclusion
Polimorphic recommends CMS and ASTP/ONC take the following steps:
1. Expand Blue Button-style APIs to include Medicaid and DSS datasets.
2. Fund pilot programs that explore AI voice and digital assistant use in state and local
benefit administration.
3. Incentivize adoption of digital tools that reduce staff burden and increase accessibility
for limited-English and digitally underserved populations.
4. Adjust policy to allow for AI to conduct interviews for applications and recertifications
We would welcome the opportunity to demonstrate our platform, provide real-world workflows,
and collaborate on pilots or research.
