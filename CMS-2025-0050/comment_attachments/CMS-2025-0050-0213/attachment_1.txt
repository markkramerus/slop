 Driv e the development and adoption of digital health management and care navigation applications Glytec applauds CMS for examining policies to accelerate the adoption of SaMD across the health care system. These technologies have the potential to significantly improve patient care, reduce preventable harm, and lower costs by streamlining clinical workflows and enabling more precise, data -driven decision -making. SaMD has an increasingly important role in health care, and mechanisms to financially incentivize its adoption should be an important focus of policy change. Outcomes -based incentiv es that align financial rewards with patient outcomes are essential for the widespread adoption of digital health tools . For example, last August, CMS released its fiscal year (FY) 2025 Inpatient Prospective Payment System/Long -Term Care Hospital Prospective Payment System final rule , which required hospitals to report on both the Severe Hyperglycemia and Hypoglycemia electronic clinical quality measures (eCQMs) beginning with the calendar year 2026 Reporting Period/FY 2028 Payment Determination. CMS’ incl usion of the new eCQMs created a meaningful incentive for h ospitals to invest in tools that improve glycemic management. By tying performance on these measures to public reporting and potential financial rewards or penalties, CMS is driving the adoption of SaMD , which helps providers deliver safer, higher -quality care. Since the rule was finalized, d igital platforms like Glytec’s Glucommander are increasingly used by hospitals to standardize insulin therapy, reduce adverse glycemic events, and align with performance expectations. These tools support clinical teams by automating insulin dosing, reducing reliance on manual protocols, and enabling proactive intervention when patients are outside of target glucose ranges. In doing so, they improve patient outcomes , reduce health care costs, and help hospitals succeed u nder value -based reimbursement models where glycemic control is a key indicator of quality and safety. Tools like Glucommander are helping progress insulin management from simple insulin use to insulin dosing schedules to paper -based “sliding scale” syste ms, on to now tightly managed, algorithmically based systems that optimize care, minimize the chances of patient harm, and improve economic management. Our program tracks all values longitudinally, presents the results graphically, and is accompanied by mo nitored analytic metrics. This detailed support can be refined for hospital floor units , clinical committee s, and hospital medical executives for system -level quality initiatives. We encourage CMS to continue advancing outcomes -based models , particularly t hose that reward effective glycemic management. By aligning incentives with performance on measures like severe hyper and hypoglycemia, CMS can drive broader adoption of clinically validated SaMDs that support insulin safety, reduce preventable harm, and a lleviate provider burden. Sustained investment in these models will not only improve outcomes for patients with diabetes but also support hospitals in delivering more efficient, cost -effective care. 
 Reduce administrative burden while accelerating progress toward value -based, patient - centered care Administrative burden continues to drive provider burnout and inefficiency in care delivery. When properly implemented, d igital health tools can offer a powerful means of streamlining documentation and simplifying workflows to reduce the administrative burden on clinicians . For example, Glytec’s software significantly reduces provider burden by automating the complex, time-consuming process of insulin titration. Traditionally, insulin titration requires clinicians t o manually assess blood glucose trends, calculate insulin doses, and adjust therapy based on evolving patient needs, often using paper protocols or spreadsheets. Leveraging Glytec’s Glucommander system, clinicians receive real - time, personalized dosing rec ommendations based on each patient’s unique glucose trends and clinical status. The platform also generates alerts when a patient’s glucose levels fall outside the target range, giving clinicians confidence they will not miss critical events. These efficie ncies save valuable clinician time, reduce cognitive load, and support safer, more consistent glycemic management across the care team. By leveraging tools like Glucommander , CMS can reduce documentation fatigue, support safer clinical decision -making, and allow providers to focus more fully on patient -centered care.
