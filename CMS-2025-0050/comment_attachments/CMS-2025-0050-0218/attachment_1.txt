Dear Secretary Kennedy, Administrator Oz, and Director Sutton,
I am urgently requesting a brief video meeting to introduce a breakthrough that could close the
most persistent and costly blind spot in American healthcare: the inability to verify whether self-
managed medications are actually taken—and whether they work.
This breakthrough is Digitally Verifiable Adherence and Efficacy (DVAE)—a method that
uses smartphone-connected packaging, wearable biosensors, and digital dispensing to confirm, in
real time, both the adherence and the clinical impact of each medication dose taken by
chronically ill patients. I have patented one approach, but I believe this field is ripe for explosive
innovation—if CMS/CMMI leads.
Today, federal incentives unintentionally sustain a dangerous illusion. The Star Ratings’
“Proportion of Days Covered” and the ACA’s Medical Loss Ratio metric reward the
appearance of adherence, not the reality. As a result, we pour billions into treating preventable
disease progression caused by missed doses, undetected treatment failure, and medication
mismanagement.
DVAE changes everything.

It transforms adherence from an unmeasured hope into a verifiable reality. It empowers patients
with actionable feedback. It equips providers with real-world effectiveness data. And it gives
CMS the power to unlock enormous cost savings through tertiary prevention—right now.

Innovation Center’s Strategy to Make America Healthy Again. By verifying the most critical
variable in chronic care—whether patients are taking the right medications at the right time
and whether those medications are working—DVAE enables a future of radically improved
outcomes, lower costs, and smarter value-based care.
But this future won’t materialize unless CMS/CMMI acts.

Subject: Digitally Verifiable Adherence and Efficacy (DVAE) — A Pillar-Enabling
Innovation to Transform Chronic Care Management at Scale
The ability to digitally verify the adherence and efficacy (DVAE) of every self-managed
medication dose taken daily by chronically ill patients—using widely available smartphone
technology—offers CMS/CMMI the most direct, scalable, and cost-effective means to fulfill all
three strategic pillars outlined in its innovation strategy.
DVAE as a Catalyst for CMMI’s Strategic Objectives
1. Promote Evidence-Based Prevention – Tertiary Prevention at Scale
DVAE uniquely enables tertiary prevention—managing existing chronic conditions to prevent
complications, hospitalizations, and decline—by transforming the most consequential, yet least
monitored, element of chronic care: self-managed medication use.
 Precision Monitoring: DVAE directly confirms whether patients are taking the right
medication at the right time, and correlates this with biometric outcomes to assess real-
world efficacy.
 Immediate Cost Reduction: Improved adherence alone reduces preventable ER visits,
hospitalizations, and disease progression—generating rapid savings. Multiple studies
estimate the current 50%+ non-adherence results in up to $500B in avoidable costs.
 Evidence-Based Triage: DVAE data allows early identification of ineffective treatments
or deteriorating control, enabling proactive, evidence-based intervention.
2. Empower People to Achieve Their Health Goals
DVAE turns the smartphone into a real-time, personalized medication assistant—offering
patients immediate feedback, secure tracking, and the confidence that their efforts are medically
impactful.
 Transparency and Accountability: Patients and providers are empowered with
verifiable data about whether the medication regimen is working.
 Digital Literacy Inclusion: DVAE integrates with existing mobile platforms and
wearables, reducing friction and extending digital equity.
 Integrated Goal-Setting: Providers can tailor health goals and treatment plans based on
real-time, individualized adherence and outcome data.

3. Drive Choice and Competition
DVAE democratizes chronic care data, allowing any provider—especially independent practices
and community-based organizations—to participate in value-based care with high-fidelity
insights.
 Level Playing Field: Smaller providers can demonstrate performance with DVAE
metrics without relying on high-cost infrastructure.
 Marketplace Differentiation: Payers can offer better plans by incorporating DVAE-
powered adherence data, enabling more accurate risk adjustment and targeted
interventions.
 Alternative Sites of Care: DVAE supports at-home treatment with confidence,
promoting site-neutral reimbursement and reducing facility dependence.
Why CMS/CMMI Must Lead the Industry to Implement DVAE
DVAE fills a critical blind spot in the current healthcare infrastructure: the invisible gap between
medication prescription and outcome. CMS/CMMI must act as the motivating force to integrate
DVAE into the healthcare ecosystem for three urgent reasons:
1. Systemic Market Failure: No current regulatory or reimbursement model incentivizes
verification of self-managed medication, despite its impact on >90% of chronic disease
outcomes and costs.
2. Data Gap Undermines Value-Based Models: Without verified adherence and efficacy
data, value-based care, ACOs, MA plans, and capitation models cannot fully manage or
price chronic care accurately.
3. Rapid ROI: Unlike upstream prevention strategies that require years to show impact,
DVAE enables immediate tertiary prevention, which generates short-term cost savings
and improved outcomes—aligning perfectly with CMS’s mandate to protect taxpayer
dollars.
Call to Action: Policy Levers CMS/CMMI Can Use to Motivate Adoption
To accelerate national implementation of DVAE, CMS/CMMI should:

 Incentivize DVAE in Model Design: Include DVAE capabilities as a condition for
enhanced shared savings, quality scoring, or payment bonuses in ACOs, Medicare
Advantage, and other value-based models.
 Support Through Waivers: Offer waivers to reimburse DVAE-enabling devices or apps
as qualifying durable medical equipment (DME) under global risk models.
 Tie to Star Ratings and Quality Metrics: Require or reward DVAE-generated
adherence and efficacy data in drug management metrics for MA and Part D plans.
 Engage States and CBOs: Integrate DVAE into Medicaid and CHIP pilots to support
high-risk populations and promote digital inclusion.
 Engage Stakeholders and the Private Sector:
o Partner with technology developers to refine DVAE platforms, ensuring
accessibility for diverse populations (e.g., older adults, non-English speakers).
o Collaborate with private payers to align DVAE incentives across multi-payer
models, amplifying market demand and economies of scale.
 Publicly Report on DVAE Impact: Track and publicly report on the impact of DVAE
implementation on adherence rates, clinical outcomes, and cost savings within CMS
innovation models. This will build the evidence base and encourage wider adoption.
Conclusion
DVAE is not just an innovation—it is the missing infrastructure that allows CMS/CMMI to
operationalize its bold strategic vision today. By verifying whether medication regimens are
being followed and whether they work in real-world, self-managed environments, DVAE closes
the last major gap in chronic disease management.
With modest policy leadership, CMS/CMMI can enable a future where every medication dose
taken by every chronically ill American is digitally confirmed and clinically meaningful—
fulfilling prevention, empowerment, and choice while safeguarding taxpayer funds like never
before.
