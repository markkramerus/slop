Dear CMS Administrator Dr. Mehmet Oz and the Health IT Modernization Team,

Thank you for the Opportunity to provide input on the Request for Information (RFT) regarding
CMSs Health Information Technology Modemization initiative.

On behalf of the Uraali Refaluwasch Association (URAALD), a culturally rooted non-profit
organization based in the Commonwealth of the Northern Mariana Islands (CNMI), we write
to advocate for the inclusive engagement of U.S. territories in this national initiative. URAALI
is a 501(c)(3)-equivalent entity certified under CNMI Business License No. 27006, dedicated to
community empowerment, cultural preservation, and indigenous advocacy.

Key Recommendations:
!. Inclusion of CNMI Cross-Sector Partners:

CMS should actively engage with and support local agencics that serve as critical pillars
in healthcare delivery and coordination within the CNMI, including:

o The CNMI Office of Vocational Rehabilitation (OVR), which provides essential
support for individuals with disabilities to achieve independent living and
employment;

o The CNMI Office on Aging, housed under the Department of Community and
Cultural Affairs (DCCA), which serves a growing clder population and family
caregivers in need of integrated services:

o The CNMI Department of Corrections (DOC), which is responsible for the
health and welfare of incarcerated adults and juveniles. Including this population
in digital health strategies is crucial to ensuring continuity of care, behavioral health
access, and reintegration success.

2. Equity in Health IT Funding and Support:

CMS should ensure full access for U.S. territories to participate in funding, pilot
programs, and technical assistance under thic modernization initiative. The CNMIs

healtheare system, led by the Commonwealth Healthcare Corporation (CHCC), faces
unique resource and connectivity Constraints that require tailored support.

4. Territorial Listening Sessions and Representation:
