Organizational Overview Socially Determined integrates health and social care through social risk analytics. Our SocialScape® platform, social risk data, and solutions empower health systems, health plans, and other risk-bearing organizations to manage risk, improve outcomes, and advance healthcare for all. We quantify social risk—upstream nonmedical drivers of health such as housing instability, food insecurity, and transportation barriers — and measure its impact on health outcomes, utilization, and cost at both the community and person levels. We partner with Medicaid Managed Care Organizations, Medicare Advantage plans, ACOs, health systems, and federal agencies to embed social determinants of health (SDOH) insights into population health, care management, value-based payment, and digital transformation strategies. Socially Determined is transforming how healthcare organizations integrate social risk data into their operations, enabling them to take predictive, actionable, and measurable steps toward improved health outcomes and reduced costs. Section III. Responses to RFI Questions VB–1, VB–2, VB–3, VB–4: Digital Health Adoption in Value-Based Care Socially Determined positions SDOH as a strategic lever within value-based care (VBC) rather than a referral-driven initiative. Previous attempts to collect and utilize social data—via survey screenings, general population health surveillance tools, and Z-codes—have experienced slow adoption and typically yield limited data volumes, leading to siloed, reactive approaches to addressing upstream factors. To achieve better outcomes and lower costs by proactively intervening on social determinants of health, our data enables: • More precise risk stratification, surfacing risk not visible in clinical or claims data 
• Enhanced provider engagement and care coordination, especially for patients facing non-clinical barriers to adherence • Improved quality and population health performance through precise engagement and targeted interventions • Cost containment by reducing avoidable utilization rooted in social risk In CMS models such as MSSP , ACO REACH, D-SNPs, and Medicaid managed care, social risk must be a core element of design, payment, and measurement—not a siloed adjunct. TD–1, TD–2: Technology Ecosystem and Data Opportunities To catalyze innovation, CMS should: • Encourage API-based integration of social risk data into EHRs, care management tools, and payment platforms. • Expand CMS data availability to include non-clinical domains, such as food insecurity, housing instability, financial strain, and transportation, via Blue Button and FHIR interfaces. • Develop FHIR profiles for SDOH and community-based data to ensure data consistency across programs. The SocialScape® platform and social risk data are already interoperable with major platforms and complement CMS claims, EHR data, and care management workflows. VB–5, VB–6, VB–10: Certification and Program Requirements CMS should expand certification programs beyond clinical workflows to include: • Geospatial and community-level intelligence to drive targeted outreach. • Predictive models that are independently validated and inclusive of social risk. • Agentic AI applications that use social context to improve decision support and beneficiary engagement. Certification pathways must also address the realities of resource-constrained providers (e.g., FQHCs, rural practices), by prioritizing low-burden data use over high-burden data capture. PC–5, PC–7: Patient and Caregiver Engagement To improve patient and caregiver adoption of digital health products, CMS should: 
• Endorse socially intelligent digital companions that go beyond reminders to support behavior change and access. • Promote real-world effectiveness studies of these tools using integrated SDOH, claims, and outcome data. • Fund innovation models and pilots that explicitly measure the ROI of social risk-informed digital solutions. Section IV . Final Recommendations CMS has laid a strong digital foundation through initiatives like Blue Button, FHIR mandates, and TEFCA. To unlock the full potential of digital health, CMS must now treat social determinants of health as essential infrastructure, not peripheral data. We recommend CMS: 1. Embed social risk into payment and quality models, not just care delivery expectations. 2. Support public-private partnerships to expand the use of predictive social risk analytics. 3. Expand certification and data-sharing frameworks to include social, behavioral, and environmental data. 4. Invest in AI-driven tools that integrate social context to deliver personalization at scale. Social risk data has the power to change how we deliver care. Let’s bring it to the forefront—where it belongs.
