I. Introduction
OneSelf Technology appreciates the opportunity to respond to CMS’s Request for
Information (RFI) on the future of the health technology ecosystem. We commend CMS and
HHS for recognizing the critical need to engage consumers more directly in their care, while
also modernizing digital infrastructure to support equitable, cost-effective, and outcomes-
driven healthcare delivery.
Despite decades of investment in enterprise health IT systems, most patients—and their
families—remain disconnected from their own health data, care plans, and treatment
decisions. Digital tools that prioritize the needs of healthcare organizations often fail to
deliver meaningful value at the individual level, especially for populations managing
chronic conditions, navigating complex care pathways, or facing health disparities. This
persistent gap must be addressed.
OneSelf Technology developed OneSelf Path™ to meet this challenge directly. Our solution
is a consumer-directed, AI-powered health application that transforms clinical data into
personalized, actionable pathways—enabling individuals to engage in their own care using
the same data and insights available to providers.
II. The Problem: Consumer Health Tools Lack Function and Value
Despite decades of national investment in health IT infrastructure and federal initiatives
promoting interoperability, consumers remain marginalized in the digital health ecosystem.
While regulations such as the CMS Interoperability and Patient Access Rule have expanded
access to health records, most consumer-facing technologies have failed to deliver
meaningful utility, context, or empowerment.
Today’s consumer health applications predominantly serve as passive data storage tools.
They may allow individuals to view or download portions of their electronic health records,
but they rarely offer interpretation, decision support, or next-step guidance. For most

consumers, the data remains unstructured, unexplained, and unactionable, essentially
turning digital access into a viewing experience rather than an empowering one.
This disconnect reveals a critical shortfall in the current health technology ecosystem:
most, if not all, consumer health apps collect and store data, but offer little to no value to
the user. Without clinical context, guidance, or integration into care planning, the
consumer’s experience becomes one of frustration, ambiguity, and missed opportunity.
Individuals managing chronic conditions, caring for children or aging parents, or navigating
complex diagnoses are left with digital folders—not intelligent tools that support informed
action.
At the same time, the broader $38 billion care management market continues to focus on
institution-driven solutions, often relying on human-to-human interventions that are
expensive, fragmented, and inaccessible to many. These models are designed for
enterprise ROI—not individual agency—and they largely exclude consumers from
participating in their own care management in any meaningful or sustained way.
The result is a paradox: consumers have more access to their health data than ever before,
yet less guidance on how to use it effectively.
OneSelf Path™ was built to change this. It is not another portal or record-viewing tool. It is a
consumer-directed, AI-powered application that interprets personal health data in real
time, surfaces what matters, and actively routes individuals along their own personalized
path to better health.
III. OneSelf Path™: A Scalable, Consumer-Directed Solution
OneSelf Path™ is a unique platform that redefines care management from the bottom up
starting with the individual. Using on-demand health data APIs, the app retrieves a person’s
clinical records from the Nationwide Health Information Network (NHIN) and other health
information exchanges via Direct Messaging and HL7 standards (C-CDA, ADT).
Once data is received, OneSelf Path™ applies evidence-based rules, AI, and proprietary
ontologies to identify:
• Diagnosed medical conditions
• Unaddressed care gaps
• Preventive health opportunities
• Logical next steps on a consumer’s personal health roadmap

The result is a dynamic, self-updating care plan that mirrors the logic of provider-driven
care coordination—but in a consumer-facing format, accessible from a smartphone or web
portal.
Key Features:
• Personalized health “pathways” that change as conditions evolve or gaps are closed
• Secure, standards-based data exchange via existing national networks
• Low-cost, high-yield alternative to traditional care management workflows
• Supports HL7 v2/v3, Direct Messaging, C-CDA, and emerging FHIR APIs
• Tracks consumer engagement and behavior in real-time for outcomes analysis
Unlike most care management tools, OneSelf Path™ does not require continuous human
intervention, making it highly scalable and suitable for both commercial and public health
applications.

V. Integrating Consumer Preferences: Insights from Lisa Moon’s Research
Dr. Lisa Moon’s 2017 dissertation, Health Data Sharing Preferences of Consumers: Public
Policy and Legal Implications of Consumer-Mediated Data Management, underscores the
importance of consumer control over personal health information. Her research identifies
key factors influencing consumers' willingness to share health data, including:
• Trust and Transparency: Consumers prefer organizations that are transparent
about how data is collected, used, and shared.
• Perceived Benefits: Willingness to share data increases when consumers perceive
direct benefits, such as improved healthcare outcomes or personalized treatments.
• Privacy and Security Concerns: Fear of data breaches and misuse of sensitive
information can deter data sharing.
• Control Over Data: Consumers desire control over who accesses their data and
how it is used, reflecting a broader desire for autonomy in health-related decision-
making.
OneSelf Path™ aligns with these preferences by providing a platform that:
• Empowers Consumers: Offers tools for individuals to manage their health data
actively.
• Ensures Transparency: Clearly communicates how data is used within the
application.
• Enhances Privacy and Security: Implements robust security measures to protect
personal health information.
• Provides Control: Allows users to determine who can access their data and for
what purposes.
By integrating these principles, OneSelf Path™ fosters trust and encourages active
consumer participation in health data sharing.
VI. Recommendations for CMS
To modernize the health technology ecosystem in ways that truly center the consumer,
OneSelf Technology offers the following policy recommendations:
1. Incentivize Consumer-Facing Interoperability
Fund and promote applications that allow individuals to access, interpret, and act
on their health data using trusted networks (e.g., TEFCA, NHIN).
4

2. Recognize Consumer-Directed Tools in Care Management Models
Update CMS quality measures and payment models to acknowledge consumer-led
digital care management as valid, reportable activity.
3. Support Standards That Bridge Clinical and Consumer Use Cases
Expand FHIR and Direct Messaging certification criteria to include consumer
functionality and dual-sided (provider-consumer) use cases.
4. Invest in Low-Infrastructure Digital Health Solutions
Encourage states and Medicaid managed care organizations (MCOs) to deploy
mobile-first, low-cost applications like OneSelf Path™ that reduce PMPM costs while
increasing patient engagement.
VII. Conclusion
The next phase of interoperability must prioritize the individual—not just the institution.
CMS and HHS have laid the groundwork through standards, certification, and nationwide
exchange frameworks. Now is the time to extend those tools to consumers, enabling them
to navigate their health with clarity, autonomy, and confidence.
OneSelf Path™ offers a scalable, standards-aligned solution that directly advances CMS’s
goals across primary care, chronic disease management, and public health. By shifting
from institution-centric care management to consumer health intelligence™, OneSelf
Technology helps transform the digital health ecosystem into one where every person has a
map—and a voice—in their healthcare journey.
