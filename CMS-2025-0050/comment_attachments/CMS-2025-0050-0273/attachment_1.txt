Key Recommendations and RFI Question References 1. Accelerate Adoption of Care Coordination and Concurrent Review Platforms (PC-12, PR -1, VB -1, PA -2, PA -5) CMS should establish targeted financial incentives, such as per -member -per-month (PMPM) payments or enhanced care management fees, to support adoption of care coordination and concurrent review SaaS platforms by primary care providers, SNFs, behavioral hea lth organizations, MCOs, and organizations in downside risk arrangements. Universal access to these platforms is essential for all stakeholders —regardless of size or geography —to achieve real -time visibility, reduce errors, and deliver patient -centered car e. 
2. Mandate Open APIs and Integration Standards —With IP Protections (TD-4, PR -3, PA -2, PA -5) CMS should require all — EHRs, payer platforms, and care coordination solutions —to support open, standards -based APIs for seamless, secure data exchange between providers and payers. Integration requirements must not be interpreted to allow or require paye rs, providers, or third parties to demand access to proprietary source code or unique technical functionalities. Protecting intellectual property is essential to foster innovation and fair competition in the digital health market. 3. Reduce Administrative Burden and Support Value -Based Care (PR-7, VB -3, PA -5, TD -13) CMS should encourage use of digital platforms to automate quality reporting, care planning, and concurrent review. Policies should also enable aggregation of clinicals within care coordination platforms, empowering providers and payers to deliver holistic, patient -centered care. We urge CMS to invest in pilots and demonstration projects to measure the impact of care coordination and concurrent review platforms on outcomes, provider and payer experience, and cost savings. 4. Modernize SNF and Post -Acute Care Coordination (PC-12, PR -1, PR -7, VB -3) Olio Health’s platform is uniquely positioned to help SNFs meet new F -627 and F -628 requirements, which mandate robust processes for safe discharges, returns, and referral management. Our solution enables: ● Streamlined, digital referrals from SNFs to HHAs and hospice companies, with real -time status tracking and secure information exchange. ● Automated workflows to ensure compliance with federal regulations, reducing the risk of citations, financial penalties, and patient harm. ● Rapid, coordinated returns to SNFs when medically necessary, minimizing delays and ensuring patient safety. ● Comprehensive audit trails and documentation to support survey readiness and regulatory reporting. 5. Medication Reconciliation at Care Transitions (PC-12, PR -7, VB -3) Transitions between care settings are among the highest -risk moments for medication errors and adverse drug events. Olio Health’s platform allows for a pharmacist -driven medication reconciliation workflow at every patient transition. When a patient moves f rom one level of care to another (e.g., hospital to SNF), Olio enables the pharmacist to compare the previous Medication Administration Record (MAR) with new MAR orders in real time. The platform allows for notifications on discrepancies —such as omissions, duplications, or dosing inconsistencies —and prompts immediate resolution. Every 
reconciliation step, intervention, and outcome are logged, supporting compliance, quality improvement, and reporting for value -based care programs. 6. Support for Behavioral Health and Whole -Person Care (PC-12, PR -1, VB -3, PA -5) Olio Health’s solution with behavioral health providers enables seamless coordination between physical and mental health services. This is particularly valuable for Medicaid and managed care populations with complex, co -occurring conditions, supporting CMS ’s goals for equity, access, and whole -person care. Conclusion Olio Health urges CMS and ASTP/ONC to: ● Establish financial incentives for providers, SNFs, behavioral health organizations, and payers — including MCOs —to adopt and use secure, care coordination and concurrent review platforms. ● Mandate open, standards -based API integration —while explicitly protecting SaaS vendors from demands to disclose or surrender proprietary code or technology. 
● Reduce administrative burden by supporting automation and data exchange in care management and utilization review workflows. ● Incentivize digital medication reconciliation and robust referral management at care transitions to improve patient safety and outcomes. ● Prioritize equity by enabling all providers and payers —including those in behavioral health, rural, and underserved areas —to access and implement advanced digital care coordination tools.
