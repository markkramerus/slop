Comment from the Federation of State Medical Boards (FSMB) on CMS RFI: Health
Technology Ecosystem (CMS-0042-NC)
Docket Number: CMS-0042-NC
The Federation of State Medical Boards (FSMB) appreciates the opportunity to provide
comment in response to the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) Request for
Information (RFI) on leveraging data to improve patient care. As a national nonprofit
organization representing the 69 state medical and osteopathic boards of the United States
and its territories, FSMB shares CMS’ commitment to improving patient outcomes through
better access to actionable data, secure digital credentials, and interoperable solutions.
Founded in 1912, FSMB has long been a trusted source for physician licensure and
disciplinary data. Our Physician Data Center (PDC) aggregates and securely maintains
comprehensive, primary source-verified information on all U.S. licensed physicians and
physician assistants/associates, including licensure status, disciplinary actions, medical
education, and specialty certification. The PDC is used by state medical boards, federal
agencies, hospitals, credentialing organizations, and health plans to support workforce
mobility, quality assurance, and patient safety.
In addition, FSMB operates DocInfo (www.docinfo.org), a publicly accessible tool that
allows patients and other stakeholders to verify physician licensure and view disciplinary
history across jurisdictions. By promoting transparency, DocInfo supports CMS’ goals of
empowering patients with information and facilitating informed decision-making.
During the first Trump Administration, FSMB launched Provider Bridge
(www.providerbridge.org/) in order to address the growing need for flexible and safe
interstate practice. Provider Bridge is an online platform, developed with funding from the
Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA), to assist with emergency
telehealth deployments and expand provider mobility, especially in times of national or
regional crisis. Provider Bridge demonstrates FSMB’s experience in managing federated
credentialing systems and supporting digital solutions to meet emerging healthcare
demands. Currently, there are more than 320,000 physicians, PAs, and nurses registered
as well as many hospitals, health departments, and provider organizations.
FSMB strongly supports CMS’ efforts to expand digital health technologies via data
interoperability and the implementation of digital identity credentials. FSMB has researched
and implemented real-time digital identity credentials for providers in specific use cases.
FSMB has seen real benefits from the use of these credentials and believes their use would
