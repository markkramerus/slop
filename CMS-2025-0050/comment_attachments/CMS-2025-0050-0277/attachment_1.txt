Executive Summary:
Trilogy Innovations and Imagineeer, LLC welcome the opportunity to help CMS
modernize its digital health technology ecosystem. Our combined experience spans
identity-integrated data exchange, scalable FHIR implementation, AI-readiness, and
public health infrastructure delivery. This response provides actionable recommendations
grounded in Executive Orders 14028, 14058, and 14110, CMS Strategic Pillars 2 and 4,
and ONC’s HTI rulemaking. We are prepared to support CMS through pilots,
demonstrations, and ongoing implementation engagement.
I. Introduction
Trilogy Innovations and Imagineeer, LLC appreciate the opportunity to respond to
CMS’s Request for Information (CMS–0042–NC) on the Health Technology Ecosystem.
We strongly support CMS’s commitment to building a modern, equitable, and
interoperable digital health infrastructure that improves outcomes and reduces burden for
providers and patients alike.
Trilogy Innovations is an SBA 8(a) certified technology firm delivering secure, cloud-
native software engineering and digital modernization across DOJ, FBI, DoD, and
NOAA. We specialize in policy-aligned DevSecOps, container orchestration, identity-
integrated data exchange, and Zero Trust cybersecurity. Imagineeer, LLC—founded by
former HHS CIO/CDO Jose Arrieta—has a deep track record in aligning federal policy
with scalable, secure technology infrastructure, including standing up the HHS Protect
platform during the COVID-19 response.
Our combined expertise positions us to provide valuable insight into the design,
implementation, and governance of CMS’s future-state health IT ecosystem. Our
recommendations align with CMS Strategic Pillars 2 and 4, Executive Orders 14028,
14058, 14110, and 14094, and ONC’s HTI-1 and HTI-2 regulatory frameworks.
II. Recommendations and Comments by Theme
A. Patient & Provider Access Experience (PC–5 to PC–7, PR–9 to PR–10)
• Define standards for delegated access, proxy credentialing, and auditability.
• Provide open UX and UI templates, including mobile-friendly and multilingual
options, to improve application usability.
• Define resilience benchmarks for performance in low-bandwidth and
disconnected environments.
• Ecosystem Enablement: Support creation of a trusted developer ecosystem for
Medicare-facing tools, including third-party care navigation and scheduling apps.

B. Data Interoperability and API Enablement (PC–8 to PC–10, TD–1 to TD–3)
• Expand Blue Button 2.0 and DPC APIs to include SDOH, imaging metadata, and
device telemetry data, leveraging scalable, containerized API infrastructure.
• Standardize cross-payer APIs for attribution, eligibility, and prior authorization,
using group-level scoping and real-time authorization protocols.
• Adopt centralized, policy-based identity and token services using modern open
identity standards.
• Enforce Zero Trust Architecture, enabling contextual access based on workload
identity, purpose, and policy.
• AI Readiness: Define FHIR-linked metadata labeling conventions to support AI
development and real-world evidence (RWE) applications.
• Innovation Certification: Establish an alternative certification track for non-
CEHRT vendors that demonstrate operational, secure, standards-based
interoperability.
• Standards Alignment: FHIR-based data exchanges should align to the HL7 US
Core Implementation Guide and be evaluated against NIST-referenced identity
assurance protocols.
C. TEFCA and Future Exchange (PC–10, PR–6, TD–6)
• Clarify TEFCA’s relationship to existing exchange networks and define its role in
endpoint resolution and trust services.
• Require TEFCA participants to support FHIR APIs, CDS Hooks, and patient-
mediated exchange.
• Align TEFCA implementation with ONC HTI-1/HTI-2 milestones, particularly
around FHIR endpoints, identity verification, and consent.
• Demonstration Offer: We are preparing a demonstration highlighting directory
validation, identity-bound endpoints, and payer-to-provider transactions.
D. National Healthcare Directory (PC–12, TD–5, VB–15)
• CMS should normalize and reconcile inputs (PECOS, NPPES, plan networks) via
centralized data governance, leveraging real-time validation tools and automated
schema enforcement.

• Include endpoint metadata, network affiliations, identity-linked provider
credentials, and AI-readable markup such as schema.org to support patient search
and machine-based routing.
• Adopt a hierarchical attestation model to reduce provider burden, allowing
organizations to validate data on behalf of affiliated providers.
• Require NIST IAL2/AAL2-compliant digital identity credentials for directory
access and editing.
• Pilot Support: Expand the Oklahoma NDH pilot to test federated directory
validation, AI-enhanced record resolution, and integration with payer-provider
endpoints.
• Long-Term Governance and Sustainability: To ensure lasting data accuracy
and operational alignment, CMS should establish a governance framework that
includes version-controlled schema updates, automated change detection, and
structured collaboration with state Medicaid agencies and regional HIEs. These
shared governance models would reduce redundant effort, support data quality
enforcement, and improve long-term compliance across the ecosystem.
E. Value-Based Care Technology Alignment (VB–1 to VB–4, VB–10 to VB–13)
• Allow CEHRT-equivalent pathways based on API enablement and verified data
quality, especially for rural and specialty practices.
• Require Bulk FHIR and Group-level support for digital quality measurement.
• Create APIs for referral loop closure, care team updates, and patient panel
management.
• ROI Metrics: Track administrative savings linked to API adoption using metrics
similar to those used in federal modernization scoring.
• Rural Focus: Fund pilots for APM-enrolled providers in rural and VA-affiliated
care settings to validate scalable identity-based access and real-time event
notification.
III. Strategic Alignment and Partnership Offer
• Our recommendations support CMS Strategic Pillars 2 and 4, Executive Orders

IV. Conclusion
Trilogy Innovations and Imagineeer, LLC commend CMS and ONC for their leadership
in shaping the next generation of digital health infrastructure. We recognize the
complexity and urgency of creating a scalable, secure, and patient-centered ecosystem
that serves the diverse needs of Medicare and Medicaid beneficiaries.
Our combined experience — spanning federal regulatory modernization, Zero Trust
architectures, public health data governance, and scalable FHIR/API infrastructure —
uniquely positions us to help CMS translate policy vision into operational success. We
understand that identity, access, and data quality are not just technical challenges, but

We are eager to contribute through pilot participation, stakeholder working groups, and

delegated access workflows, or enabling real-time data interoperability, we stand ready to
assist CMS in implementing these priorities.
We appreciate the opportunity to submit this response and respectfully request a follow-
up conversation with CMS leadership to discuss alignment, next steps, and areas where
we can provide immediate value.
