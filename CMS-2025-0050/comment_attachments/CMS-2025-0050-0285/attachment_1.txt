HHS -CMS ASTP/ONC RIN 0938 -AV68 To address this, we propose an AI -powered Medicare Coverage Assistant that uses Medicare -specific coverage guidance in concert with personalized claims data from the CMS Blue Button project to provide highly tailored guidance to Medicare beneficiaries. This solution directly addresses the need identified for tools that help beneficiaries "understand and manage health needs" . As shown in Exhibit 1 , our organization has already developed and deployed an AI -powered chatbot specifically designed to answer Medicare Parts A and B coverage determination questions (e.g., "Will Medicare pay for a CPAP machine?"). It can help beneficiaries understand their coverage options through natural language conversations, eliminating the need to navigate complex Medicare documentation or spend hours on hold with customer service representatives. Building upon this proven foundation, we propose an enhanced version that would combine two powerful capabilities: • Generative AI with Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) : Our system will leverage advanced generative AI technology enhanced with RAG to provide accurate, contextually relevant information. Unlike basic chatbots that rely on pre -programmed responses, our solution will access an indexed copy of the official Medicare coverage database containing a ll National Coverage Determinations (NCDs) and Local Coverage Determinations (LCDs). This approach ensures that responses are grounded in official Medicare policies rather than general information. This architecture prevents hallucinations (when an AI creates false interpretations or made -up facts) and ensures that all coverage guidance provided is factually accurate , traceable to official Medicare documentation , and easy to update without retraining a large model . • Medicare Blue Button Data Integration : The innovative aspect of our proposal is the integration with the Blue Button 2.0 API, which will allow our system to incorporate the beneficiary's Part A and B claims history into the coverage determination process. By analyzing this personalized health data, the assistant can provide responses tailored to the individual's specific healthcare journey, including their diagnoses, procedures, and previous treatments. This persona lization transforms generic coverage infor mation into actionable guidance that accounts for the beneficiary's unique medical situation and Medicare utilization history. HHS -CMS ASTP/ONC RIN 0938 -AV68 Benefits Our Medicare Coverage Assistant offers three key benefits , shown in Exhibit 2, that directly address the challenges faced by Medicare beneficiaries and their caregivers . Exhibit 2: Medicare Coverage Assi stant Benefits Benefit Description Personalized Coverage Determinations The Medicare Coverage Assistant transforms generic coverage information into personalized guidance by analyzing the beneficiary's specific claims history and medical context. Rather than simply stating general Medicare policies, the system provides tailored responses that account for the individual's unique healthcare journey. For example, when asked about CPAP machine coverage, the assistant would first check the beneficiary's claims history to determine if they have already completed the required sleep study. If the study has been completed, the system would confirm this and expla in the next steps for obtaining coverage. If not, it would explain this prerequisite and provide guidance on how to schedule a sleep study with a Medicare -covered provider. This personalization eliminates confusion and provides clear, actionable informatio n specific to each beneficiary's situation. Reduced Administrative Burden Medicare beneficiaries and their caregivers currently spend hours annually navigating coverage questions, including lengthy phone calls with Medicare representatives (often with wait times exceeding 30 minutes), searching through complex coverage manuals, and visiting multiple websites to piece together information. Our Medicare Coverage Assistant dramatically reduces this burden by providing immediate, accurate answers to coverag e questions through a simple conversational interface. Users can get answers in seconds rather than hours, eliminating the frustration of being transferred between departments or receiving inconsistent information from different sources. This time savings is particularly valuable for caregivers who are often balancing their caregiving responsibilities with work and other family obligations . Accessibility for All Users Medicare beneficiaries represent a diverse population with varying levels of technical proficiency and health literacy. Our conversational interface makes complex Medicare coverage information accessible to beneficiaries of all technical skill levels, incl uding those with limited digital literacy or cognitive impairments. Users can interact with the system using natural language, asking questions in their own words without needing to understand Medicare terminology or navigate complicated menu systems. The system could also support voice interaction for users with visual impairments or mobility limitations that make typing difficult. Additionally, all responses are provided in clear, jargon -free language with definitions of technical terms when necessary, en suring that information is truly accessible to all users regardless of their background or familiarity with healthcare systems. Knowledge Base Development The knowledge base will serve as the foundation for accurate coverage determinations and will be developed through: • Creating an automated process to extract Medicare Coverage data : We will develop a crawler to retrieve and parse coverage information from the Medicare Coverage Database (https://www.cms.gov/medicare -coverage -database/search.aspx ). This automated process will run on a regular schedule to ensure our knowledge base remains current with the latest Medicare policies. • Indexing content and ensuring accuracy : After extraction, all content will be processed through our proprietary natural language processing (NLP) pipeline to identify key coverage criteria, prerequisites, and exclusions. We will implement a verification system to ensure only the latest Local C overage Determinations (LCDs) and National Coverage Determinations (NCDs) are included in the knowledge base, with automated detection of superseded policies. A team of Medicare policy experts will conduct regular qual ity assurance reviews to validate the accuracy of the indexed information. AI Assistant Enhancement Building upon our existing work , we will implement several key enhancements: • Leveraging our existing Medicare coverage chatbot : We will begin with our proven chatbot platform that has already demonstrated success in answering Medicare Parts A and B coverage questions. This provides a solid foundation of conversational AI capabilities specifically configured for Medicare terminology and policies. • Implementing a claims -aware search agent : When a beneficiary asks a coverage question, our system will automatically initiate a search agent that analyzes their claims history through our database . If necessary, t his agent will identify relevant prior procedures, diagnoses, and treatments that may impact coverage determinations for the current inquiry. For example, when asked about CPAP coverage, the system will search for sleep study records and related diagnoses of obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) . • Enhancing the RAG process with personalized data : We will extend our current retrieval augmented generation (RAG) architecture to incorporate three key data sources: the user's question, relevant Medicare coverage determinations from our knowledge base, and the beneficiary's personal Blue Button claims data. This enhanced RAG process will use a sophisticated prompt engineering approach to generate responses that accurately reflect both official Medicare policies and the beneficiary's specific healthcare con text. The system will clearly distinguish between general policy information and personalized guidance based on claims history.
