 CMS-0042-NC StrivePD Architecture StrivePD’s cloud-native data pipeline aligns with CMS and ONC’s vision for a patient-centered, standards-based digital ecosystem in three key ways: 1. Open ingestion (HIGHLY AVAILABLE & EXTENSIBLE) The front-end Ingest API accepts raw HealthKit, Apple Watch, and third-party sensor streams the moment they’re generated. Because it’s schema-driven and FHIR-aware, new data types (e.g., Epic FHIR bulk exports, Bluetooth wearables) drop in with minimal lift. This keeps patients at the center: wherever they collect data, it flows into a longitudinal record. 2. Security-ﬁrst, customizable processing (HIGHLY RELIABLE & SECURE) On AWS, each processing step is containerized behind role-based access controls and encryption at rest/in flight, satisfying HIPAA and CMS “zero trust” expectations. Modular transforms let us normalize to US Core FHIR proﬁles or specialized research schemas on the fly supporting both clinical care and value-based population analytics without duplicative pipelines. 3. Low-latency, developer-friendly access (HIGHLY PERFORMANT) The Query API and SDK expose indexed time-series and streaming endpoints that apps, EHR plug-ins, and analytics platforms can query in milliseconds. This lets care teams surface real-time symptom insights, payers run quality-measure dashboards, and researchers prototype adaptive-trial analytics all on the same governed dataset, reducing silos and analytic duplication. StrivePD turns dense, heterogeneous neurologic data into standards-compliant, readily computable FHIR artifacts that flow securely from patient devices to clinicians, payers, and researchers. By collapsing ingestion, normalization, and high-performance access into one AWS-hosted backbone, we cut the time and cost of integrating novel real-world evidence exactly the acceleration CMS and ONC are calling for. By funneling every watch-captured tremor episode, gait change, or missed-medication timestamp through StrivePD’s low-latency pipeline, we can generate personalized “nudge logic” in seconds: an evidence-based message to take a skipped dose, a hydration reminder, or a prompt to start a balance-focused exercise. These micro-interventions surface in the patient’s app exactly when they matter, while the same risk signals trigger a clinician alert only if thresholds are crossed keeping the care team focused on the moments that truly require 
 escalation. In practice, this turns continuous real-world data into a real-time coaching layer that keeps people with Parkinson’s on their care plan day-to-day and initiates early intervention as soon as risk is detected, delivering the proactive, patient-centric model CMS and ONC envision. Apple FHIR Mapping Apple’s Health Records feature, exposed to developers as FHIR-native clinical resources in HealthKit, directly advances CMS and ONC’s call for a patient-centered, standards-based digital ecosystem. By giving individuals on-device access to their AllergyIntolerance, Condition, Coverage, Immunization, Observation, Medication, Procedure, and DocumentReference data with no proprietary wrappers Apple eliminates format translation overhead and lets any FHIR-capable app plug in immediately. This lowers integration costs for innovators, encourages competition around value-add services (e.g., care-plan coaching, remote monitoring), and equips patients to share complete, computable records across providers, reducing duplicate tests and care gaps. In short, Apple’s out-of-the-box FHIR pipeline turns the smartphone into an interoperable health hub, accelerating better outcomes at lower systemwide cost..
