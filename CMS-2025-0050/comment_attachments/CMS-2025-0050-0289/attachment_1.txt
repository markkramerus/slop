Podimetrics thanks the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) for the opportunity to
submit comments on the “Request for Information; Health Technology Ecosystem (CMS-0041-NC).
Based in Somerville, Massachusetts, Podimetrics is a tech-enabled services company dedicated to
preventing diabetic foot ulcers (DFUs) and associated amputations, which are among the most costly
and debilitating complications of diabetes. The company’s innovative SmartMat™ digital health
technology uses evidence-based remote temperature monitoring (RTM)1 for early detection and
prevention of DFUs and associated amputations. Data from Podimetrics’ partnership with the
Veterans Health Administration – The Initiative to End Diabetic Limb Loss at the VA (TIEDLVV) 2 3 4 –
as well as with commercial health plans5 shows that use of the SmartMat™ leads to significant
reductions in potentially avoidable and costly hospitalizations, substantial declines in total healthcare
spending, and meaningful improvements in patient mortality.6 7

I. Example of High-Value Digital Health Technology: Evidence-
Based SmartMat™ Prevents Diabetic Foot Ulcers and
Associated Amputations
Background: According to the American Diabetes Association (ADA), 160,000 diabetes-related
amputations occur annually8 – a 75 percent increase over the past decade9 – and up to 85 percent of
those amputations are preventable but occur due to patient challenges accessing high-quality care.10
Diabetic foot ulcers and diabetes-related amputation are among the most costly and debilitating
complications of the disease:
● $3.5 billion in single-year Medicare spending on potentially avoidable hospitalizations
related to diabetes amputations: In 2020 alone, Medicare spent $3.5 billion on potentially
avoidable hospitalization costs from diabetes-related amputations.11 In that same year,
Medicare spent another nearly $1 billion on potentially avoidable hospitalization costs for
beneficiaries with diabetes experiencing both amputations and ketoacidosis12 – suggesting
that, if those costs continue, Medicare potentially could spend upwards of $40 billion or
more over 10 years on potentially unnecessary and avoidable care for diabetes-related
amputations. Indeed, the cost of a single amputation associated with a diabetic foot ulcer
can be more than $100,000 and the mean one-year cost from a public payor perspective for a
diabetic foot ulcer is $44,200 (2015 USD).13 14
● High risk of mortality for patients with diabetes-related amputation: Patients suffering
from diabetes-related amputation have higher five-year mortality rates than patients with
coronary artery disease, breast cancer, and colorectal cancer.15 They also are much more

Podimetrics’ innovative SmartMat™ technology employs remote temperature monitoring for early
detection and prevention of DFUs and associated amputations. The patient stands on the
SmartMat™ for 20 seconds per day from the comfort of home while the technology scans for
temperature changes in the feet that could indicate a developing foot ulcer. The data then is
transmitted securely via cellular network for analysis without the need for Wi-Fi or a mobile device,
allowing a clinician to intervene in time to prevent the ulcer before it appears in most cases. In peer-
reviewed research from Kaiser Permanente, benefits of the SmartMat™ included25:
● 71 percent reduction in diabetic amputations
● 52 percent reduction in all-cause hospitalizations
● 40 percent reduction in emergency department (ED) visits
● 26 percent reduction in all-cause outpatient visits
VA Program with SmartMat™ reduces DFUs and amputations for
veterans: Podimetrics and the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs entered into a formal
partnership in 2020 to provide at-risk veterans with diabetes access to the SmartMat™ RTM
technology. 26 27 28 29 The Initiative to End Diabetic Limb Loss at the VA (TIEDLVV) has been highly
successful:
● $100 million in healthcare savings in first 12 months: Data from 6,428 Veterans suggested
the VA saved roughly $100 million over the first 12 months of enrollment from avoided
hospitalizations and outpatient visits alone – translating to savings of nearly $16,000 per
veteran per year.30
● Mortality reduction: Veterans had a 37 percent reduction in one-year mortality over the first
12 months of enrollment in the program – equating to one veteran life saved for every 23
veteran participants who entered the program.31

II. Policy Recommendations to Advance Digital Health
Technology
Podimetrics strongly applauds the efforts of CMS and ASTP/ONC to facilitate widespread adoption
and access to digital health technology in federal health programs and the U.S. healthcare system
more broadly. To that end, Podimetrics respectfully offers the following policy recommendations:
1. Medicare coverage pathway for innovative digital health technology: In 2024, CMS
established a limited pathway for Medicare coverage of a small number of technologies and
devices called Transitional Coverage for Emerging Technologies (TCET).33 TCET replaced
the much more expansive medical device coverage pathway established in the first term of the
Trump Administration, Medicare Coverage of Innovative Technology (MCIT).34 CMS should
build on the work of the first term of the Trump Administration to significantly expand TCET
and establish a broad separate pathway for Medicare coverage of digital health technologies.
Unlike MCIT, the pathway should include digital health technologies beyond only those with
“Breakthrough” designation from the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) so that Medicare
beneficiaries have access to truly innovative devices and technologies that improve health
outcomes like the SmartMat™ RTM technology that prevents debilitating, costly and deadly
DFU and amputation complications from diabetes. As part of this expansive pathway,
Medicare should cover and recognize the work of healthcare providers to effectively treat and
manage patients benefitting from these novel technologies.
2. Medicare and Medicaid quality measures: Two Medicare Merit-based Incentive Payment
System (MIPS) quality measures address diabetes-related foot care, but do not assess
prevention of DFUs and associated amputation. In Medicare Advantage (MA), the Health
Effectiveness Data and Information Set (HEDIS) Comprehensive Diabetes Care measure
does not evaluate foot care. Similarly, the 2023 and 2024 Core Set of Adult Health Care
Quality Measures for Medicaid includes four diabetes-related measures, but none that
address quality of care related to DFUs and amputations. Therefore, CMS should work with
