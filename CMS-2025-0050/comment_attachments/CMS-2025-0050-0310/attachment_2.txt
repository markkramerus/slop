As a partnership of over 1,000 seasoned experts who have supported more than 450 hospitals and
health systems in advancing data interoperability and digital health infrastructure, Healthcare IT

Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) and its partners at the Administration for Strategic
Preparedness and Response (ASPR) and the Office of the National Coordinator for Health
Information Technology (ONC). Our response addresses the recent Request for Information
(RFI) on beneficiary digital access and data interoperability.
1. Empowering Patients through Responsible Technology Adoption
We believe that modernizing digital access to healthcare data—especially through the adoption
of artificial intelligence (AI)-ready data formats, enhanced digital identity tools, and dynamic
patient sharing capabilities will empower Medicare beneficiaries to make smarter, safer, and
more personalized health decisions. Drawing on lessons from commercial healthcare, we
emphasize that responsible innovation hinges on interoperability, accessibility, and trust.
We recommend the following:
• Integrating no-cost digital identity platforms such as Login.gov and ID.me with Medicare
systems to ensure trusted, accessible credentialing for all beneficiaries.
• Issuing a Blue Button-enabled digital insurance card with optional photo ID to improve
usability and reduce fraud risk.
• Offering multi-format claims data access (including Fast Healthcare Interoperability
Resources [FHIR], Health Level Seven [HL7], PDF, Excel, and AI-structured formats) to
enhance usability for patients, caregivers, and digital tools.
• Developing a Pilot: AI-Powered Medicare Assistant Integrated via Blue Button 2.0 in a
FedRAMP-Protected Public Cloud and live Health System EHR
Designing all patient-accessible data to be AI-consumable, paves the way for voice assistants and
generative AI copilots that can explain benefits, navigate care options, and flag risks.
2. A Shared Vision for Infrastructure Progress
Healthcare IT Leaders and Sublimation Health bring a proven, scalable approach to building
health technology infrastructure. Our experts are helping leading hospitals and health systems:
1

• Build and scale Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources (FHIR)-based application
programming interfaces (APIs) and HL7 integration engines.

3. Collaborating with CMS, ONC, and ASPR to Cultivate the Digital Health Market
To realize CMS's goals, we propose a public-private collaboration model that:
• Pilots AI-integrated patient navigation tools using real Medicare data to assess usability,
equity, and impact on decision-making.
• Builds a verified, shareable ecosystem of providers, researchers, and proxies, enabling
beneficiaries to control their data in real time.
• Operationalizes data sharing safeguards akin to the protocols used by the National
Institutes of Health (NIH) and the TEFCA framework, ensuring trust and transparency.
4. Supporting the Transition to Value-Based Care through Health IT
The transition to value-based care represents a cornerstone of CMS's strategy to incentivize
improvements in health outcomes rather than increases in service volume. We believe the role of
technology is critical to this transformation. While significant progress has been made in health
IT adoption, opportunities remain to better align technology requirements with the needs of
providers participating in Alternative Payment Models (APMs) and other value-based care
programs. We support:
• Refining requirements for the use of health IT, certified under the ONC Health IT
Certification Program, including Certified Electronic Health Record Technology
(CEHRT).
These requirements must continue to enable value-based care and meet statutory requirements,
while also addressing key program objectives such as reducing provider burden. Properly
aligned, these requirements can support broader adoption of value-based care models, drive
innovation, and enhance patient choice and competition in the healthcare marketplace.
5. Pilot Concept: AI-Powered Medicare Assistant Integrated via Blue Button 2.0 in a
FedRAMP-Protected Public Cloud and live Health System EHR
To support CMS’s vision for expanded patient access, digital identity, and AI-driven
engagement, Healthcare IT Leaders and Sublimation Health propose a pilot initiative to
demonstrate a scalable, AI-enabled Medicare assistant for beneficiaries. This assistant would
operate within a FedRAMP-authorized public cloud environment and integrate directly into
an existing EHR platform and mobile app ecosystem of one of our current health system clients.
2

Core Capabilities:
• FHIR-based Blue Button 2.0 integration for real-time claims data ingestion and
longitudinal record assembly.
• Natural language interaction powered by fine-tuned generative AI models, capable of:
Explaining benefits and coverage in plain language.
o
Providing proactive reminders for preventive care.
o
Supporting medication management and encounter prep.
o
• Digital identity authentication through Login.gov or ID.me to align with CMS’s security
and fraud-prevention objectives.
• Multi-channel deployment: available to beneficiaries through their provider’s mobile app,
EHR portal, and potentially CMS.gov.
Goals of the Pilot:
1. Validate usability and adoption among Medicare patients, including older and lower
digital-literacy populations.
2. Assess impact on beneficiary comprehension of benefits, cost transparency, and
navigation behaviors.
3. Demonstrate technical feasibility of embedding Blue Button-derived intelligence
directly into provider and patient-facing tools.
Deployment Readiness: This pilot can be launched with minimal friction due to:
• An existing health system partner already using a Sublimation Health-supported, cloud-
hosted EHR environment.
• A pre-existing mobile platform that can host the assistant.
• Our engineering capability to map Blue Button claims into FHIR and AI-ready semantic
layers, securely processed within a FedRAMP-compliant AWS cloud enclave.
Future State: If successful, this pilot will serve as a model for national replication—enabling
CMS beneficiaries across the country to interact with a smart, secure, and accessible assistant
that extends CMS data utility far beyond passive viewing and into true digital engagement.
We also advocate for flexibility in technology standards, interoperability mandates, and
automation to reduce manual effort and administrative overhead, allowing providers to focus
more on outcomes than on documentation. This collaboration can ensure that infrastructure
investments translate directly into increased access, reduced disparities, and improved health
outcomes.
In Conclusion: We believe CMS is uniquely positioned to lead the future of patient-centric
health IT, and that with the support of capable partners like Healthcare IT Leaders and
Sublimation Health, this vision can become a reality. Together, we can create a digital
experience that is secure, human-centered, interoperable, and impactful for every American.
We welcome the opportunity to present a pilot framework, co-develop a roadmap, or respond to
additional requests for information or partnership.
