Subject: Public Comment on CMS RFI – Advancing a Seamless, Secure, and Patient-Centered
Digital Health Infrastructure
To Whom It May Concern,
On behalf of PreventScripts, a clinical-grade platform transforming chronic care prevention by
embedding scalable behavior change directly into primary care workflows, we commend CMS
and ONC for leading this critical initiative to modernize and humanize the nation’s digital health
infrastructure.
We appreciate the opportunity to provide input on this important RFI and offer the following
recommendations:
Drive the Development and Adoption of Digital Health Management and
Care Navigation Applications
● Promote digitally-enabled care models that offer between-visit health behavioral
support:
True prevention and long-term behavior change do not happen during 15-minute office
visits. CMS should prioritize models that provide consistent, digitally-driven
support—reinforcing lifestyle goals, monitoring progress, and addressing barriers in real
time through daily or weekly touchpoints.
● Expand access to tools that summarize and synthesize health data into
meaningful insights:
It’s not enough to collect patient data—it must be transformed into actionable
information. CMS should support tools that distill data from RPM devices, validated
assessments, and patient-reported outcomes into concise, useful summaries that
empower patients and guide clinical decisions.
● Champion solutions that drive behavior change and promote health equity:
Digital tools must be evidence-based, culturally appropriate, and accessible to seniors
and low-health-literacy populations. These innovations reduce disparities and enable
participation in prevention programs across communities.
Strengthen Interoperability and Secure Access to Health Data Through
Standards-Based Technologies
● Standardize data exchange using open APIs and protocols:
CMS should enforce universal support for FHIR APIs, TEFCA participation, and Direct
Messaging, thereby eliminating the need for costly custom EHR integrations and

enabling seamless collaboration across care teams and platforms.
● Support patient- and provider-centered data usability:
Interoperability must go beyond access to include understandable, structured data.
CMS should lead on setting standards for user-centered design that translate complex
health data into clear, useful formats.
● Promote integration of social determinants of health (SDOH) data:
To deliver whole-person care, digital infrastructure must support the exchange of SDOH
data in standardized formats, enabling providers to address root causes of chronic
disease and health disparities.
Identify and Dismantle Barriers to Seamless Exchange of Health
Information
● Confront vendor gatekeeping and fragmented interfaces:
Many EHR vendors maintain proprietary constraints that limit true interoperability. CMS
must strengthen compliance enforcement and require certified health IT to facilitate
open, low-cost integration.
● Advance a national patient identity framework:
Patient-matching failures continue to disrupt continuity of care. A unified,
privacy-protecting identifier would streamline data exchange and improve care
coordination across systems and platforms.
Reduce Administrative Burden While Advancing Value-Based,
Patient-Centered Care
● Simplify billing and documentation for digital services:
RPM and assessment codes like 99453, 99457, and 96160 are underused due to
complex documentation requirements. CMS should standardize billing criteria and
support broader adoption across payers to reduce friction and incentivize provider
participation in preventive models.
Address Patient Barriers to Participation and Outcomes
● Eliminate out-of-pocket costs that deter engagement:
Even modest copays can discourage Medicare beneficiaries from participating in

programs that improve outcomes. CMS should consider:
○ Waiving copays for preventive RPM and assessment services;
○ Bundling digital support into VBC contracts or capitation models;
○ Providing funding for RPM devices and broadband access for vulnerable
populations.
● Invest in digital literacy and onboarding support:
Many patients—particularly older adults—require hands-on assistance to engage with
digital tools. CMS should support care models that include technology education and
high-touch onboarding to ensure equitable access and sustained engagement.
Real-World Value in Action
PreventScripts has seen promising results through its digital prevention platform. In a clinic
serving rural communities in Tennesee, we observed on average sustained 1.5% reduction in
A1c in 3 months and 6.2mmHg reduction in blood pressure in 4 months. among patients using
high-touch RPM paired with behavior-change content. These interventions work—and they’re
most effective when grounded in seamless digital infrastructure and policy alignment.
Final Thought
CMS has a unique opportunity to reshape our nation’s healthcare infrastructure into one that not
only documents illness—but proactively builds health. Enhanced, digitally-enabled care models
that deliver between-visit support, track patient progress in real time, and integrate digestible,
actionable insights into workflows are the foundation for a prevention-first system.
PreventScripts stands ready to participate in future innovation models aligned with this vision
and appreciates CMS’s continued leadership in advancing digital health equity.
