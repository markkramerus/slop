To whom it may concern,
We appreciate the opportunity to contribute to the improvement of patient-centered healthcare in
the US via this response to the CMS RFI (CMS-0042-NC). We are excited that CMS is
considering the perspectives of patients and technology providers to compliment the voices of
payers and providers on these matters.
The FHIR Department is a group of 250+ industry experts and leaders specialized in FHIR
and/or policies that require FHIR. Our members represent a very diverse and passionate cross
section of participants in the health care and health tech industries. Together, the group has
prepared comments in response to the request for information from Health and Human Services
(HHS) including the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS) and the Office of the
National Coordinator (ONC). The breadth of perspectives held by the group allows us to
provide a uniquely broad set of feedback that we hope will be valuable in guiding CMS decision
making.
We agree with CMS leadership that with aggressive, informed and judicious action by the
federal government we can reduce barriers to data access and exchange, realize the potential
of recent innovations in healthcare that promote better health outcomes, and accelerate
progress towards a patient-centric learning health system. The tables that follow represent our
direct responses to the questions posed in the RFI.
Sincerely,
The FHIR Department
