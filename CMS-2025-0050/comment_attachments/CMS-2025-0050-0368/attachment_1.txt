RE: CMS-0042-NC RFI Response — Empowering Digital Health for Medicare Beneficiaries I’ve spent my entire career building care coordination services from the ground up. Through my work with Previva Health Group, our team engages over 5,000 Medicare patients every month across chronic care management, annual wellness visits, transitional care, and more. After years on the front lines—watching care coordinators struggle with burnout, inefficiencies, and outdated systems—I founded CareCo, an AI-powered co-pilot designed to transform the way care is delivered. CareCo doesn't replace the human—it empowers them. Our platform equips care coordinators with AI-generated Call Guides, real-time documentation, source-cited patient summaries, and automated post-call workflows. It solves three of the most pressing challenges facing CMS today: 1.Fraud: We’ve seen firsthand how some providers bill for care that never happens. CareCo is actively developing a generation-two Call Guide that will analyze and score every conversation for quality and patient outcomes, making low-value or fraudulent care impossible to scale. 2.Non-compliance: Many care teams deliver strong patient engagement but fail to meet CMS documentation standards. CareCo solves this by automating compliant notes, logging time, and generating auditable outputs—without burdening the care team. 3.Inefficiency: Legacy portals and fragmented tools cost time and quality. CareCo turns each call into a streamlined workflow—from call prep to documentation, task generation, provider messaging, and patient summaries—boosting productivity and outcomes. This RFI signals an important shift: CMS and ONC are not just focused on access—they’re ready to shape a smarter, more scalable digital infrastructure. That future depends on tools like CareCo that enable the success of value-based care, eliminate fraud, and reduce burnout, particularly for non-clinical frontline staff. In our attached submission, we’ve addressed questions across the Patient, Provider, Technology Vendor, and Value-Based Care sections. We’ve also included visuals and can provide video demonstrations of our AI-guided workflows upon request. Thank you for your leadership and vision. I look forward to contributing to CMS’s efforts and would welcome any opportunity to collaborate further.

Section 3: Providers PR-1 How can CMS encourage providers to adopt digital tools? CareCo reduces provider burden and improves compliance by: ●Handling documentation automatically ●Prepping every call ●Logging billable time in real time CMS can support this by: ●Creating reimbursement parity for augmented care teams ●Recognizing traceable, source-cited notes as compliant documentation PR-2 What obstacles exist in developing and adopting tech? ●Tools often layer onto existing work ●EHRs are closed or non-cooperative ●Documentation is a major time sink CareCo solves this by integrating with workflows, reducing redundancy, and ensuring all outputs are billable, compliant, and audit-ready.
PR-4 What changes would allow better third-party tools? ●Standardized appointment availability APIs ●Intake form interoperability ●Provider directory access CareCo could coordinate across systems more effectively with open access to these elements. PR-7 How can CMS increase data sharing and reduce burden? ●Support use of conversational data as source for documentation ●Allow non-EHR tools to participate in CEHRT extensions ●Incentivize output-based compliance (e.g., time-logged, source-cited) rather than input method Section 4: Value-Based Care Organizations VB-1 What incentives would drive ACO adoption of digital tools? ●Tech grants for ACOs deploying AI co-pilots ●Shared savings bonuses for using validated engagement tools ●Simplified compliance for traceable, AI-generated documentation CareCo gives ACOs the ability to deliver more care with less burnout.

CMS should incentivize open data models for outreach platforms.
