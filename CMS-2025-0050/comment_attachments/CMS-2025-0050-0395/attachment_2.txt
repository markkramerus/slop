Response to CMS RFI on Advancing the Digital Health Ecosystem
Certify appreciates the opportunity to respond to CMS’s Request for Information on advancing
digital health tools to improve care delivery, patient access, and interoperability across the US
healthcare system. Our mission is to build the infrastructure that will power provider data
management across the country, as we build toward a more efficient and patient-centered
healthcare ecosystem. We offer this response to highlight the foundational role of provider data
infrastructure in achieving these goals.
Certify is addressing CMS’s stated goals from the perspective of provider data management, at
the infrastructure level. We are building the nation’s modern provider data infrastructure - a
real-time, AI-driven platform that unifies data from primary sources, provider self-attestations,
third-party partners, payer networks, and delegated vendors. The platform is grounded in four
core pillars, that when laid as a national provider data infrastructure, enable a more functional,
future-ready healthcare ecosystem:
● Data-first architecture ensures that data is not an afterthought or a byproduct of
workflows. It is the foundation. This allows Certify to maintain high data fidelity across
every record, support robust data lineage and entity resolution, and enable real-time
synchronization between systems. It also makes it possible to support advanced use
cases like provider matching, accurate patient attribution, network adequacy validation,
and personalized provider search.
● Scalable infrastructure allows Certify to ingest, process, and distribute massive volumes
of provider data across stakeholders with different needs and access levels. Whether it’s
supporting a regional Medicaid plan or powering a national multi-tenant network, the
infrastructure can accommodate diverse operational and regulatory requirements. It also
allows for seamless cross-tenant intelligence so a provider update in one part of the
system can permeate to other plans, improving data quality everywhere else it’s
relevant.
● Configurability gives each tenant full control over their environment while still benefiting
from a shared national backbone. Data schemas, business rules, validation thresholds,
and agentic AI can be applied to the discrete and atomic data we store on our back end,
across countless use cases, to ensure our platform meets the specific needs of
individual clients. This flexibility supports a wide range of use cases, including
onboarding, credentialing, ongoing monitoring, enrollment management, delegated
roster management, all feeding into our living provider database that can power a

national provider directory.
● AI-powered automation enhances the efficiency, accuracy, and scalability of provider
data operations. Certify uses machine learning and rules-based systems to support
real-time entity resolution, survivorship logic, delta detection, and anomaly flagging. This
not only reduces manual effort but also accelerates workflows like roster reconciliation,
provider outreach, and ongoing data validation.
Together, these pillars enable Certify to deliver a continuously verified, interoperable source of
truth that can power a national provider directory. Care navigation and provider
search/scheduling apps can then be layered onto our infrastructure, ensuring patients have
visibility into which providers are in their network, cover the patients needed benefit, have
availability, are close to where they live, and are a culturally competent fit. The result is not just
cleaner data, but a healthcare system that is easier for patients to navigate, with reduced friction
and waste after the encounter, lowering overall cost, and improving the experience for patients,
providers, and payers alike - exactly the kind of foundation CMS is looking to build.
At Certify, we believe that provider data is the invisible backbone of the U.S. healthcare system.
It underpins everything from patient access and coverage verification to claims processing, care
coordination, and compliance. Yet despite its central importance, provider data remains deeply
fragmented, duplicative, and outdated. This is because no one has built an infrastructure that
spans the full provider data management life cycle - until now. Certify is filling that gap by
delivering a platform designed to unify, manage, and activate provider data at national scale.
Without this kind of infrastructure, fragmentation continues to limit the effectiveness of digital
health tools, hinder care navigation, impair access, and create unnecessary burden for payers,
providers, and patients alike.
Unlike point solutions and static data repositories, Certify functions as a national Master Data
Management (MDM) platform that understands and maintains the many-to-many relationships
between providers, locations, groups, and networks. Our multi-tenant architecture allows
stakeholders such as payers, health systems, state agencies, and delegated entities to operate
within their own private environments while benefiting from national-scale intelligence and
cross-tenant data sharing. When a provider record is updated in one tenant, that change is
reconciled at the master level through entity resolution and survivorship logic, and made
available to all other tenants who subscribe to that provider. This ensures that updates cascade
appropriately and with full data lineage, so that every data point is traceable, auditable, and
governed.
Importantly, keeping data current doesn’t require complexity. Our platform supports multiple
update pathways. Delegated entities can upload rosters that are automatically compared
against existing data, with delta files generated to identify and track all changes. Providers can
update their own records through self-service portals, which write changes directly to the
relevant tenant and then sync with the master record. No matter the source, updates are

processed at the root level and cascade only to the appropriate entities. This ensures data
integrity while avoiding overcorrection or blanket data overwrites.
This infrastructure directly supports CMS’s stated goals in this RFI:
Patient-Centered Access and Care Navigation
Certify ensures that accurate, granular provider data, such as participation status, location
affiliations, specialties, languages spoken, and cultural identifiers, is readily available. This
allows patients, including those with complex or rare conditions, to find the right provider for
their needs, within their network, and as close to home as possible.
Our platform can support data needed to power consumer-facing tools such as provider search
and scheduling applications. With our infrastructure in place, patients can easily find providers
based on insurance participation, specialty, practice location, or even language and cultural
background. That kind of transparency and precision is key to improving navigation and access
across the system, while also contributing to advancing health equity.
Data Exchange and Interoperability
Certify’s open APIs and interoperable design pave the way for seamless integration into claims
systems, provider scheduling tools, and patient-facing apps. Instead of requiring costly custom
connections or redundant workflows, our infrastructure provides a unified, plug-and-play
foundation that digital health tools can rely on. By enabling real-time exchange of verified
provider data, Certify helps address one of the most persistent blockers to interoperability and
ecosystem innovation. This aligns perfectly with our mission to lay the infrastructure that will
power the next generation of provider data products.
Enabling Value-Based Care
Accurate provider data is essential for patient attribution, performance measurement, and
proper reimbursement. Certify supports value-based care initiatives by reducing provider burden
and streamlining the administrative infrastructure that underpins these models. We also see a
compelling opportunity for payers and government programs to encourage providers to maintain
their information through incentive mechanisms. This could turn data maintenance into a
value-aligned behavior rather than a compliance task.
Transparency and Equity in Care
Certify supports the capture and surfacing of key provider attributes, including ethnicity,
language proficiency, and other cultural competencies. Patients deserve to choose care that
reflects not just clinical fit but cultural alignment. Our infrastructure helps ensure that information
is readily available and accurate across all touchpoints, making care more inclusive and
equitable for all.
Finally, by building a national, API-first provider infrastructure layer, Certify lays the groundwork
for others in the digital health ecosystem to innovate on top of it. AI agents and other smart tools
selected as part of this RFI will be able to leverage Certify’s granular, continuously updated data

to deliver more intelligent recommendations, automate administrative tasks, and improve user
experiences.
Certify is proud to be contributing to a future where every patients can see which providers are
in-network, at which locations they practice, what specialties they cover, and which populations
they serve. That clarity is essential to real access. And in many ways, that kind of national
visibility into care options is what it will take to truly Make America Healthy Again.
In summary, Certify is not only contributing to the national conversation about digital health, we
are actively building the foundation. Our infrastructure enables the workflows CMS seeks to
strengthen, from care navigation and patient access to value-based care and interoperability.
We look forward to collaborating with CMS and the broader ecosystem to make this vision a
reality.
