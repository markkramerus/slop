Introduction

Global eSolutions Group is committed to advancing healthcare technology innovation
while ensuring compliance with CMS priorities, including interoperability, privacy
protections, equitable patient access, and regulatory adaptability. As CMS builds a
comprehensive Health Tech Ecosystem, our expertise in ontology-driven data governance,
adaptive consent management, automated policy enforcement, and metadata tagging
positions us as a key collaborator in achieving CMS objectives.

Supporting CMS Health Tech Ecosystem Priorities

CMS aims to create an integrated, secure, and patient-centric healthcare data exchange
that aligns with evolving regulations and ensures transparent, interoperable
communication between providers, individuals, and healthcare organizations. Global
eSolutions Group provides cutting-edge solutions to support these goals.

1. Interoperability and Seamless Data Exchange

e Built on HL7 FHIR, C-CDA, V2.x, and OMOP standards, our framework ensures real-
time, secure, and accurate data sharing across EHRs, HIEs, QHINs, and third-party
applications.

e FHIR-based APIs support policy-driven automated data governance, simplifying
CMS’s efforts to improve data liquidity and accessibility.

e We align with CMS initiatives on longitudinal patient records, ensuring data
provenance tracking and transparent audit trails to improve data integrity and trust.

2. Privacy-Centric and Policy-Driven Data Governance

e Granular privacy controls allow patients to customize consent settings based on
security level, jurisdictional policies, and personal preferences.

e We enable metadata-driven classification, ensuring sensitive data (e.g., mental
health, reproductive health, substance use disorder, SDOH) is properly tagged and
restricted in compliance with HIPAA, 42 CFR Part 2, state regulations, GDPR, and
the American Privacy Rights Act of 2024 (APRA).


e Our policy automation eliminates manual updates, ensuring compliance with state-
specific laws, CMS regulations, and emerging privacy policies.

3. Equitable and Patient-Centric Data Access

e CMS prioritizes reducing disparities in health data accessibility, particularly for
underserved populations. Our secure consent management system fosters trust
and transparency, giving patients full control over their data-sharing preferences.

e Our approach ensures compliance with local and federal mandates, such as
California Consumer Privacy protections, reproductive healthcare legislation, and
SUD confidentiality regulations, strengthening CMS’s health equity initiatives.

4. Al-Driven Compliance and Policy Automation

e« CMS’s initiative to modernize regulatory oversight aligns with our Al-driven

e Our ontology-based policy framework proactively supports cybersecurity, auditing,
