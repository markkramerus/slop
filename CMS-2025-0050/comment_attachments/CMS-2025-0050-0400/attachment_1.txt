Dates and materials subject to change. 1 Executive summary for CMS RFI response Thank you for the opportunity to respond to the CMS Health Technology Ecosystem RFI. Sharecare is a digital health company with proven public-private partnerships delivering results across Medicare, Medicaid, ACA, and employer programs. We’re excited to help CMS accelerate modernization and bring connected care to more Americans. Intelligent navigation built for real-world use AskMD, our AI-driven health navigation platform, integrates real-time eligibility, provider matching, EHR connectivity, and value-based care alignment. Unlike point solutions or pilots, AskMD is live and deployed at scale through Sharecare’s enterprise infrastructure, reaching diverse populations across Medicaid, Medicare Advantage, ACA exchanges, and commercial networks. Scalable, interoperable, and grounded in real-world relationships Sharecare’s reach across U.S. markets enables scalable solutions that improve access, engagement, and outcomes. AskMD reflects this investment and is built on longstanding partnerships with health plans, government agencies, health systems, employers, and leading technology and cloud services and is shaped by millions of real-world interactions across Medicaid, Medicare, and commercial populations. Live demonstration: Georgia Georgia offers a real-time model for CMS to observe scalable public-private collaboration in action, serving diverse populations and funding models across the state, including: • CMS can see this model in action in Georgia, where Sharecare supports: • SHBP: One of the nation’s largest public employee groups • Georgia Access: Supporting QHP enrollment on the state exchange • Medicaid: Digital front door for the largest MCO in the state • Commercial and VBC networks: Concierge experience that integrates benefits, care access, and rewards Together, these deployments provide a testbed aligned with CMS’s vision for an integrated digital ecosystem, creating opportunities to validate provider directories, support real-time eligibility navigation, and personalize benefit delivery across coverage types. 
 Embedded research to evaluate impact A new partnership with the University of Georgia is evaluating AskMD’s impact on access, enrollment, and satisfaction for 1.5M exchange members. This academic study will measure cost, experience, and plan fit between members using AskMD and those navigating legacy systems. Proven reach and results Sharecare supports 9.6M eligible lives, including: • 2.25M+ Medicare Advantage members • 900K+ Medicaid members, many in rural areas • 450K+ federal employees (FEP) • 750K+ individuals via government contracts • 10K+ provider sites through ROI services In a Medicaid deployment, Sharecare achieved: • 49% engagement with value-added benefits • 29% use of AI chatbot for benefit/reward guidance • 19% use of geolocation tools for resources like SNAP • 26% rural user penetration, demonstrating equitable access Secure, scalable, and ready to grow AskMD is built on Sharecare’s CMS-certified infrastructure, including HIH and QCDR status. Our SaaS platform supports rapid deployment, cross-system interoperability, and configurable branding for Medicaid, exchange, and employer markets. It enables identity verification, real-time data exchange, and benefit navigation tailored to user needs. Aligned with value-based care goals: AskMD supports value-based care contracts by value-based care contracts by embedding risk stratification, real-time data inputs, and behavioral nudges that align with performance metrics. APIs and backend integrations support care team workflows, while reporting capabilities track progress against quality and cost-of-care goals. A platform ready for CMS partnership Our solution aligns directly with CMS’s modernization priorities: augmenting national provider directories, enabling identity verification, supporting Blue Button 2.0 integration, and strengthening value-based care enablement. This is not a concept or pilot in development. It is a live platform delivering measurable results.
