Context: Defining Payment Integrity and Its Role in CMS
Modernization
CMS defines payment integrity as the process of preventing, identifying, and correcting improper payments in
Medicare, Medicaid, and CHIP. These efforts are essential to maintaining sustainability and public trust in
federally funded healthcare programs. The Payment Integrity Information Act (PIIA) of 2019, along with
Executive Orders 14058 and 13520, reinforce the importance of reducing improper payments while also
minimizing administrative burden on providers and beneficiaries.
Improper payments in FY2024 exceeded $100 billion, representing a significant opportunity for CMS to adopt
private-sector innovations to address root causes and reduce waste. Effective payment integrity includes pre-
payment analytics, contractor coordination, data integration, and transparency into program operations. CMS
has emphasized in recent publications the need for "a seamless, transparent healthcare experience, enabled by
interoperable data systems"—a goal directly aligned with ClarisHealth’s platform, Pareo.
Private payers leveraging enterprise-wide PI programs typically achieve 5–10% of claims cost savings through
real-time audit management, medical record digitization, and centralized oversight. These platforms also align
with CMS’s Health Equity Framework by improving accuracy, reducing delays, and ensuring resources are
allocated fairly and efficiently across beneficiary populations.
