Subject: Request for Information; Health Technology Ecosystem
Dear Secretary Kennedy and Dr. Keane,
Thank you for the opportunity to respond to this Request for Information (RFI).
I am the primary editor of the FHIR US Core Implementation Guide and have served as a subject-matter
expert to the Argonaut Project since its inception. During this 60-day RFI period, I’ve also been on the
front lines of care, helping my son through an urgent appendectomy, assisting with a chronic condition
that returned from remission, and calling 911 for an elderly family member after a hard fall.
These experiences reaffirm the need to empower patients and families with digital tools that support
timely, informed decisions. I urge CMS and ASTP/ONC to focus on the following:
• Enable API Access to Diagnostic-Quality Images: Patients and providers should be able to access
imaging through secure APIs, not outdated CDs. My son can download a 75 GB Xbox game from
home; image size isn’t the issue.
• Enable Eligibility and Prior-Authorization Transparency: Patients and care teams need real-time
access to coverage and prior-auth status. Credit checks take seconds, yet our care was delayed
because no one could confirm what was covered or approved in the moment.
• Enable Digital Delivery of Emergency-Care Documentation: Data from 911 calls, urgent care, and
referrals should flow electronically to the patient and their care team. When a grandparent flies
home to Minnesota for a graduation celebration, they shouldn’t be expected to know what a Record
Locator Service is or wear out a fax machine trying to share 80+ years of medical history.
• Enable Full API-Based Electronic Health Information (EHI) Access: Patients should be able to
request and receive their complete health record in computable formats, through certified APIs,
without workarounds or delays. The AI revolution is here, and patients, not institutions, should
decide who can access and use their data to support innovation, care, and trust.
• Enable Health Systems to Self-Audit EHR Implementations: Real-world use often drifts from
certified design. CMS and ASTP/ONC should support tools that let health systems evaluate their own
implementations, such as Bulk FHIR export readiness, endpoint discoverability, and terminology
mapping coverage.

On Technical Standards and Implementation
I am intentionally not listing specific technical solutions here. Josh Mandel of SMART Health IT already
did an excellent job summarizing in his response. Instead, I urge CMS and ASTP/ONC to stay focused on
outcomes. Do not mandate specs that are not proven in the real world. Set clear, bold requirements,
like the 2015 “open API access” rule, and give the industry space to choose or build the best tools to
meet them. That simple requirement lit the fire for FHIR, and it's the kind of north star we need again.
