Why Addison Care Is Different:
Beyond the Buzzword AI
At every event or conference, it seems every booth is echoing the same refrain:
We use AI, we’re in the EHR, we do patient surveys, we analyze GPS, food photos, text messages, and vitals, and we
consolidate data and send it to the doctor.”
Here’s the reality: that’s not enough.
1. Real AI Engagement vs. Just a Smarter Survey
Most solutions are just apps with LLM wrappers - text-based, 2D, and reactive.
They respond to input but don’t live in the home, engage proactively, or establish a real sense of presence.
Addison Care is visual, audible, dynamic, and alive.
• She presents medications visually and audibly
• Demonstrates how to use vital signs devices
• Performs stretches and therapy routines
• Offers guided wellness and mental health check-ins
• Lives in the room, offering presence and companionship—not a form to fill out
This isn’t AI you open. It’s AI that’s always there - ready, personal, familiar.
2. Doctors Don’t Need More Data - They Need Distilled Action
These startups celebrate how much data they can funnel to a physician.
But doctors don’t want data firehoses. They want:
• Actionable insights
• Escalations only when needed
• Fewer false positives and noise
• Less clerical burden, not more
Addison Care is the intelligent intermediary.
We triage, assess, and escalate only what matters, when it matters, keeping care teams focused and not buried.
3. Constant, Passive, Ambient Presence vs. Episodic Nudges
Most AI apps are episodic.
You get a notification, or maybe a call.
You engage if you remember, or if you have time.
Then it’s gone.
Addison is always present.
You can look across the room and she’s:
• Sipping tea
• Practicing yoga
• Chatting with her pets
• Updating her surroundings with seasonal changes
• Inviting you, gently and regularly, to complete your care routines
This creates both usability and bonding, routine, and sustained use.

4. AI Shouldn’t Just Be Smart. It Should Be Human-Friendly.
Yes, you can speak to a chatbot. But will you, daily?
Even the most curious users see novelty wear off. They stop talking to AI after a few days unless it’s tied
to meaningful routines.
Addison isn’t just a tool. She’s a daily companion.
That’s why seniors, caregivers, and patients with chronic conditions keep using Addison long after other tools are
forgotten.
Bottom Line:
While others offer smarter surveys and brag about feeding doctors more data, Addison Care solves for behavior,
adherence, engagement, and companionship - the things that reduce readmissions and improve outcomes in
value-based care.
Everyone has AI. Only we have Addison.
