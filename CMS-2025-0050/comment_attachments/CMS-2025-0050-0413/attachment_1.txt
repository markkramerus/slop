 PUBLIC COMMENT SUBMISSION To: Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) RE: Request for Information – Health Technology Ecosystem RIN: 0938–AV68 Date: June 15, 2025 Submitted by: Rafat Fields - CEO, Powered to Rise Recommendation: A CMS–Integrated AI Assistant for Medicare Beneficiaries CMS has a timely opportunity to lead the development of an AI-powered, patient-facing assistant assigned at Medicare enrollment and designed to follow beneficiaries across plan years, provider networks, and care episodes. This assistant would: ● Be anchored in each beneficiary’s CMS-linked identity ● Integrate data from Medicare Advantage, Part D, and other CMS-approved plans ● Sync with EHRs, pharmacy data, and claims via interoperable standards such as FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources) and HL7 to surface care alerts and support informed decision-making ● Provide a single, user-friendly interface for benefits, appointments, and care tracking This approach leverages existing API infrastructure and aligns with current CMS commitments to data interoperability and patient access. How It Could Work in Practice Through interoperability with plan-based and provider-assigned navigators, the CMS assistant could simplify complex tasks. For example, a Medicare recipient needing a specialist could: 
 1. Connect first with the plan’s AI navigator to find in-network providers 2. Use the CMS assistant again to connect with the provider’s AI scheduling system and book an appointment 3. All of this occurs without logins, phone calls, or repeating care history Why CMS Is Uniquely Positioned As the central payer and data steward for Medicare, CMS can: ● Define national FHIR-based standards and enforce HL7-compliant interoperability ● Normalize AI navigation tools as a core benefit ● Ensure accessibility, culturally responsive care, and inclusive system design through oversight ● Provide stable infrastructure that supports innovation without burdening patients or providers Why This Matters Now: Evidence & Context ● Provider Shortage – HRSA projects a shortage of approximately 87,150 primary care physicians by 2037 ( HRSA ). ● Administrative Burden – A Kaiser Permanente study reports AI scribes saved nearly 16,000 hours of documentation time in 15 months ( Becker’s Hospital Review ). ● Patient Acceptance of AI – ChatGPT-generated health responses were rated more empathetic than physician responses in 79% of cases ( JAMA Internal Medicine ). Technology in Context: OpenAI, Notion, and HighLevel Platforms like OpenAI enable conversational AI; Notion provides secure, customizable documentation; and HighLevel is a HIPAA-compliant platform for smaller providers and care 
 teams. These tools, when integrated with CMS’s assistant, allow patients to speak or type naturally and have that information securely routed and followed up on, without managing multiple portals. Ambient AI embedded at CMS or provider levels can reduce friction and ensure system-wide continuity, especially when APIs and FHIR-based protocols standardize communication across systems. Lived Experience as Lens With over two decades of experience supporting providers, navigating payer systems, and addressing patient needs, my insights reflect the real-world challenges faced by healthcare professionals. I’ve seen efforts to connect care thwarted by siloed Electronic Health Records (EHRs), confusing benefits, and uneven patient access to technology—trends this proposal directly addresses. Conclusion Medicare beneficiaries need more coordinated, clear, and supportive care. A CMS-led AI-navigation approach, integrated across plans and providers and grounded in national data standards, can reduce administrative strain and significantly enhance safety, trust, and continuity of care. I offer this perspective as someone who has helped providers and patients alongside fragmented systems, and who now studies how accessible technology can fill these gaps.
