Initial Position on CMS Health Technology Ecosystem RFI
Introduction
Heudia was formed as a result of a Phase 2 Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) Contract with the
U.S. Department of Agriculture to address the problem of Childhood Obesity, leading to the
commercialization of AccessMeCare™. Our digital health platform, AccessMeCare™, is designed to
connect healthcare providers, community organizations, and patients through an interoperable, cloud-
based ecosystem.
By addressing barriers to care, streamlining data exchange, and empowering vulnerable populations,
AccessMeCare™ aligns with the CMS Health Technology Ecosystem RFI’s goals of enhancing
interoperability, leveraging emerging technologies, promoting health equity, improving data transparency,
and fostering stakeholder collaboration. This response outlines how AccessMeCare™ addresses the RFI’s
key questions and supports CMS’s vision for a modernized, patient-centered digital health infrastructure.
Since it’s commercial release, AccessMeCare™ has been deployed in over seven federally funded research
initiatives and four existing sites with more than 250 clinical and non-clinical network partners, and over
50,000 direct user encounters. AccessMeCare™ is a Health Technology leveraging advanced machine
learning, including graph theory, pathfinding algorithms, and predictive analytics, to streamline and
enhance patient journeys by addressing clinical and community-level factors that adversely affect patient
care transitions.
Heudia Health leverages advanced AI technologies to enhance care access, particularly for underserved
populations. The team develops platforms that address access and availability of care by identifying and
mitigating barriers such as geographic and socioeconomic challenges. Heudia excels in integrating
multimodal data, including voice, imagery, and electronic health records (EHRs), to create comprehensive
patient profiles. This enables precise population health management and reduces disparities in care
delivery. The team’s commitment to ethical AI is evident in bias mitigation strategies, use of diverse
training datasets, and explainable AI frameworks to ensure fairness and transparency.
The team’s leadership expertise in scaling technologies and fostering partnerships to deploy innovative
solutions in complex healthcare environments. Our team comprises seasoned professionals with extensive
experience in AI, healthcare, health system operations, geospatial technologies, and strategic
development, uniquely positioning us to execute this project with excellence.
Our response incorporates the collective wisdom we have gathered from all of our ecosystem partners
throughout this journey with key input from HSR.health. HSR.health is a leader in geospatial health
informatics, leveraging location-based data to address public health challenges and improve healthcare
outcomes by integrating environmental, socioeconomic, and healthcare access data to inform decision-
making and optimize care delivery.

Our response to this RFI is predicated on a long history of related work which started from a Phase 2 Small

1. System Interoperability
RFI Question: How can CMS integrate systems like the National Provider Identifier (NPI), Provider
Enrollment, Chain, and Ownership System (PECOS), and Medicare Care Compare with external data sources
(e.g., state licensing boards, wearable devices) to streamline provider data and support research? What
data elements are critical for a centralized health technology ecosystem?
Heudia’s Response: AccessMeCare™ is a scalable PaaS that facilitates seamless data exchange across
disparate healthcare and social service systems, addressing CMS’s interoperability goals. The platform
integrates independent programs, schools, universities, and community organizations into a unified
network, enabling real-time data sharing. Key features include:
• Standards-Based Integration: AccessMeCare™ supports Health Level Seven (HL7) standards and
Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources (FHIR) APIs, ensuring compatibility with CMS systems
like NPI, PECOS, and Medicare Care Compare. This allows for accurate provider data validation and
verification, critical for a potential National Directory of Healthcare Providers & Services (NDH).
• Data Elements: The platform captures essential data elements, including provider credentials (NPI,
licensing information), digital endpoints (secure messaging addresses), service locations, and
SDoH-related data (e.g., housing, food security). These align with CMS’s need for comprehensive
provider and patient data to support care coordination and research, such as the NIH-CMS autism
data platform.
• External Data Sources: AccessMeCare™ integrates with state licensing boards and community-
based organizations, pulling verified data into a centralized hub. For example, it can connect with
social service providers to share SDoH data, enhancing CMS’s ability to address environmental
stressors like poverty, as highlighted in studies on maternal and infant health outcomes.
• Verification Processes: The platform employs automated validation checks to ensure data accuracy
(e.g., confirming NPI against NPPES) and supports secure endpoint verification via encrypted
messaging, meeting CMS’s requirements for data integrity.
The inclusion of HSR.health’s GeoMD Platform provides spatial data infrastructure with the intent of
merging disparate data from multiple separate repositories into a single framework to support AI/ML-
based data analytics. Their pipelines can curate data from the various sources mentioned in the RFI as well
as additional data from authoritative federal (e.g., Census, CMS, health outcomes data) as well as state
and local sources of social, environmental, and health outcomes data, wearables data, and other data
sources as may be needed to support health risk analytics the HHS and its agencies may deem important
for protecting public health.
Further, Anna, HSR.health’s generative AI orchestrator can provide this data lake with a conversational API
that accepts natural language queries and returns explainable results. For example, this can enable HHS
and CMS to perform research queries such as “Which Medicare cardiologists practice in heat risk areas?
Anna and the GeoMD Platform integrating disparate data elements, enable GeoAI analytic capabilities,
along with an conversational layer allowing CMS to turn siloed provider files into a living, research-ready
health-technology ecosystem.

2. Technology Integration
RFI Question: What role can AI, APIs, and wearable technologies play in improving care delivery, prior
authorization, and research into chronic diseases? How can CMS mitigate risks like algorithmic bias or
privacy breaches?
Heudia’s Response: AccessMeCare™ leverages advanced technologies to enhance care delivery and
support CMS’s research objectives:
• APIs: The platform uses FHIR-based APIs to enable secure, real-time data exchange between
healthcare providers, social services, and patients. For example, APIs facilitate remote intake and
appointment scheduling, streamlining prior authorization processes by connecting payers and
providers directly.
• AI and Behavioral Nudges: AccessMeCare™ employs AI-driven algorithms to guide care seekers to
appropriate services based on their needs, incorporating behavioral nudges (e.g., reminders,
personalized recommendations) to improve engagement. This supports CMS’s focus on patient-
centered care and chronic disease management.
• Wearable Technologies: The platform can integrate data from wearable devices (e.g., glucose
monitors, fitness trackers) to provide real-time health insights, supporting research into chronic
conditions like diabetes or autism. For instance, wearable data can inform the NIH-CMS autism
data platform by tracking behavioral patterns or environmental stressors.
• Risk Mitigation: To address algorithmic bias, Heudia implements transparent AI models with
regular audits to ensure equitable outcomes, particularly for underserved populations. Privacy is
safeguarded through HIPAA-compliant encryption, role-based access controls, and secure cloud
infrastructure hosted on Amazon Web Services (AWS). Penetration testing and compliance with
NIST 800-53 standards further protect against breaches.
AccessMeCare™’s technology stack aligns with CMS’s vision for innovative tools that enhance care
coordination and research while prioritizing security and fairness.
This allows us to integrate 3rd party solution providers like HSR.health into our technology-driven
ecosystem to provide chronic disease care ecosystem partners can act on, not just study.
As an examples, Anna can pulls daily streams from connected medical devices wearables, e.g., heart-rate
patches, step counters, etc., and fuses them with GeoMD risk layers such as social and environmental
factors, care desert maps, and other risk factors. Anna can then push a “watch list” into an EHR identifying
those patients/ beneficiaries that are most likely to experience an adverse health event in the near term
and why. Every alert ships with an explainable feature list, and each model retrain runs through a parity
audit to confirm the same accuracy across race, sex, and dual-eligible groups.
This strategy also ensures that prior authorization is fair and nearly friction-free.

3. Health Equity
RFI Question: What strategies can ensure equitable access to health technologies for marginalized
populations? How can CMS address barriers like digital literacy and broadband access?
Heudia’s Response: AccessMeCare™ is designed to empower vulnerable populations, directly addressing
CMS’s health equity goals:
• Mobile Accessibility: The platform is accessible via a smartphone or tablet app, enabling low-
income and rural populations to connect with care providers and social services without requiring
advanced hardware. This reduces barriers for communities with limited broadband access.
• SDoH Focus: AccessMeCare™ integrates SDoH data to guide users to affordable care and
community resources (e.g., food banks, housing programs). For example, it has supported
maternal-fetal health programs, securing federal funding for six of eight clients to address poverty-
related stressors, as evidenced by studies showing the impact of environmental factors on infant
brain development.
• Digital Literacy: The platform features a user-friendly interface with multilingual support and
guided workflows to accommodate users with low digital literacy. Community health workers can
also use AccessMeCare™ to assist patients, bridging literacy gaps.
• Early Childhood Education: By connecting children to early education programs, AccessMeCare™
promotes long-term health equity, as education correlates with better health outcomes and
reduced inequities.
These features ensure that marginalized populations, including rural, low-income, and minority
communities, can access CMS-supported services equitably, aligning with the RFI’s emphasis on inclusivity.
The strength of AccessMeCare’s™ ecosystem architecture scales very rapidly to close care gaps across
rural, frontier, and tribal communities where Anna and the GeoMD Platform can show and allow users to
explore where physical and digital access gaps exist. Combining both the lack of brick-and-mortar medical
facilities as well as the infrastructure (broadband, computing resources) required to support telehealth
and digital health services along with data on chronic disease burden, can help target resource allocation
to where they will have the greatest overall impact on improving health outcomes.
For example, because the Care Desert Initiative includes income, age, as well as broadband and
infrastructure factors, CMS could pilot “device-plus-data” bundles (tablets + hot-spots) the same way it
reimburses durable medical equipment.

4. Data Transparency
RFI Question: How can CMS enhance public access to Medicare Advantage data, provider directories, and
quality metrics to empower beneficiaries and reduce fraud?
Heudia’s Response: AccessMeCare™ enhances data transparency and supports CMS’s transparency goals:
• Provider Directories: The platform maintains accurate, up-to-date provider directories by
integrating with NPI, PECOS, and state licensing data, reducing errors and ensuring beneficiaries
can locate appropriate providers.
• Quality Metrics: AccessMeCare™ enables providers to share quality metrics (e.g., patient
satisfaction, readmission rates) through its dashboard, which can be made publicly accessible via
CMS’s Medicare Care Compare. This empowers beneficiaries to make informed decisions.
• Medicare Advantage Data: By connecting payers and providers, AccessMeCare™ facilitates the
sharing of Medicare Advantage (MA) encounter data, supporting CMS’s goal of increasing MA data
transparency for public analysis and fraud detection.
• Public Access: The platform’s cloud-based architecture allows CMS to expose de-identified data
sets to researchers and the public, supporting initiatives like the NIH-CMS autism data platform
while maintaining HIPAA compliance.
By centralizing and validating data, AccessMeCare™ reduces fraud risks (e.g., ghost providers) and
enhances beneficiary access to transparent information, aligning with CMS’s objectives.
Through our partnership with HSR.health we can turn raw files into a public, map-based front door. The
GeoMD Platform geocodes every provider and plan record and layers it on an interactive map that any
beneficiary can filter by specialty, network status, star rating, travel time, and a host of other factors of
interest. Further, Anna allows a conversational interface to this data meaning no spreadsheets or PDFs are
needed, and more information is more readily accessible to all.
The GeoMD Platform and Anna offers a novel means of analyzing claims: geospatially. A provider billing
for home visits 300 miles apart on the same day can be flagged; a clinic suddenly performing telehealth
visits at 3 AM across time zones triggers an alert. These signals feed CMS fraud detection teams in near
real time. Further, the algorithm’s ability to detect fraud from simply unusual but legitimate activity will
improve over time.
The public map accepts user-reported errors (“This office moved”) that flow back through Anna for
verification — advancing the update cycle faster than annual plan submissions.
The result - patients, beneficiaries, and researchers get a live, visual directory they can trust, app
developers gain a clean API, and CMS gain geospatial fraud and data quality safeguards without adding
bureaucratic steps.

5. Stakeholder Collaboration
RFI Question: How can CMS partner with state agencies, private entities, or initiatives like the Office of the
National Coordinator for Health Information Technology (ONC) to build a robust ecosystem?
Heudia’s Response: Heudia’s collaborative approach positions AccessMeCare™ as a key partner for CMS:
• State and Local Partnerships: AccessMeCare™ has integrated with state agencies and regional
organizations, such as the Rappahannock Area EMS Council and RHP-NWAHEC, to deliver
coordinated care. These partnerships can support CMS’s integration with state licensing boards
and Medicaid programs.
• Private Sector: Heudia’s work with managed care organizations, hospitals, and community-based
organizations demonstrates its ability to bridge public and private sectors, aligning with CMS’s goal
of multi-stakeholder collaboration.
• ONC Alignment: AccessMeCare™ adheres to ONC’s interoperability standards (e.g., FHIR, USCDI),
ensuring compatibility with ONC’s Trusted Exchange Framework and Common Agreement
(TEFCA). This enables seamless data exchange with ONC-certified systems, supporting CMS’s
interoperability efforts.
• Federal Funding Success: Heudia’s track record of securing federal funding for SDoH programs
(e.g., maternal health initiatives) positions it as a reliable partner for CMS to implement grant-
funded technology projects.
AccessMeCare™’s interoperable, partnership-driven model fosters the collaborative ecosystem CMS
envisions, connecting federal, state, and private stakeholders to enhance care delivery.
Challenges and Mitigation Strategies
While AccessMeCare™ addresses the RFI’s objectives, potential challenges include:
• Scalability: Integrating with legacy CMS systems (e.g., PECOS) may require custom adapters.
Heudia mitigates this by offering modular APIs and dedicated integration support.
• Adoption: Providers may resist new platforms due to workflow changes. Heudia addresses this
through user training, intuitive design, and demonstrated ROI (e.g., reduced costs for hospitals
and payers).
• Data Privacy: Sharing SDoH and wearable data raises privacy concerns. AccessMeCare™’s HIPAA-
compliant infrastructure and consent management tools ensure compliance and user trust.
• Equity Gaps: Some rural areas lack smartphone access. Heudia partners with community
organizations to provide device access and in-person support.
These strategies ensure AccessMeCare™ can scale to meet CMS’s needs while addressing implementation
barriers.

What is Proposed – MERIT – A National Demonstration of AI-Powered Health Technology Ecosystem
Supporting Secretary Kennedy’s Vision to “Make America Healthy Again”
Project Overview
Heudia Health proposes a large-scale, national bipartisan demonstration of MERIT—the Model Evaluation
& Real-time Integration Tool—delivered through its AccessMeCare™ platform. MERIT is a real-time
performance monitoring and self-correction framework that ensures predictive AI models remain
transparent, equitable, and optimized across diverse populations and care settings. The platform directly
supports CMS’s RFI priorities to enhance interoperability, reduce health disparities, and foster patient-
centered innovation.
Demonstration Objectives
This proposed demonstration will quantify MERIT’s impact on:
• Reducing per capita healthcare expenses associated with uninsured and publicly insured
individuals over utilization of avoidable emergency department use and preventable hospital
admissions/readmissions;
• Improving uptake of primary and preventive care in Medicaid and Medicare populations;
• Expanding equitable access to care across geographies and populations.
Resources Required
To successfully implement this initiative under a federal grant or IDIQ contract, Heudia Health will require:
• Federal Support: Multi-year funding (3–5 years) to support platform expansion, data integration,
and model validation in partnership with CMS-regulated entities;
• Community Sites: Deployment across a minimum of 3-5 high-need regions associated with
approximately the same number or more congressional districts regions with support from local
hospitals, FQHCs, EMS agencies, and community-based organizations;
• Stakeholder Engagement: A national Community Advisory Board and bipartisan coalition of
federal, state, and local health care leaders to guide implementation and policy alignment.
Technology Alignment
AccessMeCare™ already meets HHS interoperability standards (FHIR, HL7), supports ONC’s TEFCA
framework, and is hosted on HIPAA- and NIST 800-53-compliant infrastructure. MERIT enhances these
capabilities with AI tools that detect model degradation, correct in real time, and map risk at the
population level.
Bipartisan Vision, Real-World Results
This project directly supports Secretary Kennedy’s call for scalable, evidence-based innovation that bridges
political divides through measurable improvements in cost, access, and quality. MERIT is built for
bipartisan success: focused on measurable ROI for payers and providers and ecosystem accountability in
how AccessMeCare™ shapes care delivery.
