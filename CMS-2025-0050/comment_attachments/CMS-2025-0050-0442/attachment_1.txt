Background Recent evidence from a systematic review of the evidence for the relationship between nurse staffing and certain patient outcomes, suggests important associations 1 . Most studies demonstrated that higher registered nurse (RN) staffing levels were associated with reduced patient mortality. Twelve analyses from seven studies, including three at low risk of bias, found significant associations between higher RN staffing and reduced in-hospital mortality in adult general populations. In adult intensive care and hospital settings, higher RN staffing was associated with statistically significant reductions in infections. Higher RN staffing was linked to several positive outcomes including reduced adverse events (death, cardiac arrest, unplanned ICU admission), shorter hospital stays (0.23 days reduction per additional RN hour per patient day), and lower readmission risk. The inpatient nursing labor market is rife with key issues including high rates of clinician burn-out, moral injury related to understaffing, and competition from other sectors that are increasingly hiring inpatient bedside nurses away. Artificial intelligence and an advanced hardware-software combination system operated by virtual inpatient nursing companies (e.g., Nexus Bedside) allows for the redesign of inpatient nursing workflows to reduce operating costs, improve quality patient outcomes, and improve workplace satisfaction among nursing staff. The model also allows for hospitals in low-nurse labor supply areas to access additional labor resources from other geographic areas in the United States. A hybrid-virtual inpatient nursing care model implemented at an IPPS hospital or Critical Access Hospital pairs remote virtual nurses with bedside nursing teams to address nursing shortages and support inexperienced staff. Virtual nurses, working from remote locations, handle documentation-intensive tasks like admissions, discharges, medication reconciliation, and continuous patient monitoring using AI alerts and early warning systems, while bedside nurses focus on hands-on patient care. This team-based approach, guided by high reliability organization principles, has demonstrated significant improvements in patient safety through early detection of deterioration and reduced readmissions, standardized quality processes, optimized workflow efficiency, and maintained workforce stability during the COVID-19 pandemic. The program allows all team members to work at the top of their licensure scope while providing mentorship to novice nurses and creating a sustainable model that enhances both clinical outcomes and operational effectiveness. We estimate that nursing represents 30% of hospital operating costs so CMS pays between $270 and $450 billion dollars per year on hospital nursing care alone per 2023 spending data. CMS payment policies and hospital regulations shape inpatient nursing care delivery models and modernization using technology is necessary. By supporting hospital adoption of the technology infrastructure for virtual nursing and enabling comprehensive and efficient outsourced management models under existing payment systems, the Inpatient Prospective Payment System (IPPS) and in payment to Critical Access Hospitals, CMS can improve the efficiency of inpatient care delivery and support rural hospital facilities in maintaining high standards of care while reducing operating expenditures. Definition and Scope of Hybrid-Virtual Nursing Digital Health Product Definition (PC-1a, PR-1): Hybrid-virtual inpatient nursing is a digital health technology-enabled solution that integrates remote nursing capabilities with bedside care delivery. This model employs registered nurses working from centralized virtual command centers who collaborate with on-site nursing teams to provide comprehensive patient care through audio-visual technology platforms embedded in patient rooms. The most advanced technologies incorporate artificial intelligence technologies integrated with the electronic health record and remote vital monitoring systems to automate repetitive nursing workflows, to catch potential clinical quality issues, and to enhance collaboration between the virtual and in-person nursing team as well as advanced practice nurses and physicians. This model of care may be operated by the hospital via vendor relationships with a technology company or it may be entirely outsourced to a company that manages hiring, training, staffing, and technology implementation. Core Technology Components: ● High-definition cameras and monitors in patient rooms ● Two-way audio communications technology between the patient, in-person care team, and off-site virtual nurse ● Secure, HIPAA-compliant communication platforms ● Integration with Electronic Health Records (EHR) systems ● Real-time access to patient data and monitoring systems ● Standardized workflow protocols for virtual and bedside nurse collaboration ● Artificial intelligence systems with both patient and nursing interfaces

The Nexus Bedside Hybrid-Virtual Inpatient Nursing Approach A Case Study of Mercy Hospital’s Virtual Nursing Model Mercy Virtual Nursing addresses the critical nursing shortage crisis through an innovative telehealth care delivery model. With nursing demand expected to increase by 21% by 2025, according to HHS, while 1 million nurses retire, Mercy created a 125,000 square foot Virtual Care Center that operates "like a 
hospital without beds. 5 " This facility employs over 330 virtual care professionals who use high-definition cameras, real-time vital sign monitoring, and integrated electronic medical records to provide remote nursing care across 5 states and more than 30 intensive care units. The virtual nurses collaborate with bedside teams to provide 24/7 coverage, data synthesis to identify patient deterioration trends, mentorship for novice nurses, and documentation support. This model is very similar to that operated by Nexus Bedside, but Nexus leverages artificial intelligence to further enhance workflows, reduce missed care opportunities, and to ensure extra monitoring of patient safety. The model has demonstrated significant clinical improvements, including reducing septic shock progression from 34% to 19% through early intervention based on automated data trending 6 . Virtual nurses maintain the critical nurse-patient relationship through video conferencing while providing expert assessment and care coordination. Additional technologies like mobile health devices enable continuous biometric monitoring of patients even after discharge, preventing readmissions and supporting care in patients' homes. The telestroke program brings specialist neurological care to community emergency rooms that lack onsite neurologists. Rather than replacing traditional nursing, this telehealth model integrates technology to enhance and extend nursing care, particularly benefiting rural and underserved areas. It allows experienced nurses to continue practicing despite physical limitations, provides cost-effective care delivery, and maintains the compassion and therapeutic relationships essential to nursing while improving patient outcomes and satisfaction. 2. Implementation Considerations for Different Hospital Types 3.1 IPPS Hospitals Implementation considerations vary significantly across different hospital types, with each requiring tailored approaches to maximize the benefits of virtual nursing programs. IPPS hospitals are particularly well-positioned to implement virtual nursing solutions due to several inherent advantages. Their larger patient volumes create sufficient scale to justify the substantial technology infrastructure investments required for these programs. These facilities typically have established IT support systems that facilitate smoother implementation processes, along with greater financial resources that enable comprehensive virtual nursing program development. Additionally, IPPS hospitals can leverage their size and resources to serve as virtual nursing hubs, extending support to smaller facilities in their networks. The implementation strategy for IPPS hospitals should focus on a phased rollout approach, beginning with specific units such as medical-surgical and telemetry departments where virtual nursing can demonstrate clear value. Integration with existing quality reporting requirements ensures that virtual nursing initiatives align with regulatory compliance and performance measurement systems. These larger hospitals can leverage economies of scale for technology procurement, reducing per-unit costs while ensuring robust system capabilities. Interoperability and Data Exchange Requirements (PR-5, PR-7) 4.1 Technical Standards Implementation Successful virtual nursing implementation requires robust interoperability and data exchange capabilities that ensure seamless integration with existing healthcare technology infrastructure. The foundation of these technical requirements centers on comprehensive FHIR API implementation that enables virtual nurses to function effectively within the broader care team ecosystem. The Patient Access API serves as a critical component, providing virtual nurses with comprehensive access to patient records necessary for informed clinical decision-making. The Standardized API for Patient and Population Services supports broader population health monitoring capabilities, allowing virtual nursing programs to contribute to quality improvement initiatives and outcome tracking. Provider Directory APIs facilitate essential communication pathways between virtual nurses and other healthcare providers, ensuring coordinated care delivery. SMART on FHIR integration enables virtual nursing applications to seamlessly integrate with existing EHR systems, maintaining workflow continuity and reducing technological barriers to adoption. Data integration requirements extend beyond basic record access to encompass real-time monitoring capabilities essential for effective virtual nursing practice. Virtual nurses need immediate access to vital signs and monitoring data to provide timely interventions and support. Integration with nurse call systems and patient communication platforms ensures that virtual nurses can respond promptly to patient needs and maintain effective communication channels. Seamless documentation workflows across virtual and bedside nursing teams prevent information gaps and support comprehensive patient care coordination, while quality metrics tracking and reporting capabilities enable continuous program evaluation and improvement. 4.2 Privacy and Security Considerations HIPAA Compliance: Privacy and security considerations form the cornerstone of virtual nursing program compliance and patient trust. HIPAA compliance requires secure video conferencing platforms with end-to-end encryption to protect patient communications and maintain confidentiality. Access controls must limit virtual nurse 
access to only their assigned patients, preventing unauthorized information exposure. Comprehensive audit trails for all virtual patient interactions ensure accountability and support compliance monitoring, while established patient consent processes for virtual nursing services maintain transparency and respect patient autonomy in care delivery decisions. 5. Rural Hospital Considerations (PR-1a, VB-10) 6.1 Critical Access Hospital Specific Benefits Rural hospitals, particularly Critical Access Hospitals, face unique operational challenges that make virtual nursing solutions especially valuable for maintaining quality care, access to nursing labor, and financial sustainability. These facilities must navigate the complex requirements of providing comprehensive healthcare services in resource-constrained environments while serving geographically dispersed populations in areas with limited nursing labor markets. Virtual nursing provides essential staffing flexibility that directly addresses the regulatory and practical challenges facing Critical Access Hospitals. These facilities must comply with CAH requirements for 24/7 emergency care availability, which can be difficult to maintain with limited nursing staff. Virtual nursing solutions enable appropriate RN coverage during periods when only Licensed Practical Nurses are physically present on-site, ensuring that patients receive the appropriate level of clinical oversight and intervention capabilities. Additionally, rural hospitals gain access to specialized nursing expertise in areas such as ICU care, cardiac monitoring, and oncology support that would otherwise be unavailable due to geographic isolation and limited resources. Virtual nurses that service these facilities may be located in a distant area, but can be licensed in the same state in which they are providing care to the patient. The financial sustainability benefits of virtual nursing align particularly well with the unique reimbursement structure of Critical Access Hospitals. The cost-based reimbursement model that provides 101% of reasonable costs creates a favorable environment for virtual nursing technology investments, as these expenses can be recovered through Medicare reimbursement. Virtual nursing reduces the need for expensive contingent labor from travel nurses or locum tenens arrangements for advanced practitioners. Perhaps most importantly, improved patient retention capabilities reduce revenue loss from transfers to 
distant facilities, allowing rural hospitals to maintain their patient populations and the associated reimbursement while providing higher-quality local care that better serves their communities. Within regions, hub-and-spoke models can be developed to allow for collaborative staffing arrangements with larger facilities. Groups of critical access hospitals can also share services to reduce the cost of implementation and to ensure the financial sustainability of these programs. In the age of artificial intelligence, these facilities also may lack the infrastructure to adopt the very technologies that may benefit them most. CMS, HRSA, and HHS should carefully examine and consider specific hybrid-virtual nursing programs for critical access hospitals via payment models driven by the Center for Medicare and Medicaid Innovation. 7. Value-Based Care Integration (VB-1, VB-3, VB-8) 7.1 Value-based Care General Alignment This model of care is well-aligned with value-based care and the Quadruple Aim in that it is designed to produce hard cost savings for facilities and produce better patient outcomes while supporting the nursing workforce. Research and Development Priorities (PC-7, TD-1) 8.1 Evidence Generation Needs The continued advancement of hybrid-virtual inpatient nursing requires focused research and development efforts that address both immediate implementation needs and future innovation opportunities. Evidence generation represents a critical priority for establishing the long-term viability and effectiveness of virtual nursing programs across diverse healthcare settings. CMS, HHS, and the National Institutes of Health should fund patient-centered outcomes research on quality outcomes from these programs as well as cost-savings and return on investment to CMS. Clinical outcomes research must examine the long-term impact of virtual nursing on patient safety metrics to establish definitive causal evidence of improved care quality. Cost-effectiveness studies comparing virtual nursing models to traditional staffing approaches will provide essential data for healthcare administrators and policymakers making investment decisions. Comprehensive patient satisfaction and experience measurement will ensure that virtual nursing enhances rather than diminishes the patient care experience, while research into nurse retention and job satisfaction outcomes will demonstrate the workforce benefits that can sustain these programs over time. Future innovation opportunities center on artificial intelligence integration that can dramatically enhance virtual nursing capabilities. Predictive analytics for patient monitoring will enable proactive interventions 
before clinical deterioration occurs, while automated alert systems can ensure rapid response to changing patient conditions. Natural language processing for documentation support can reduce administrative burden and improve accuracy, and machine learning applications can continuously optimize care protocols based on outcome data. Nexus Bedside’s application of artificial intelligence in this particular area is quite promising and is already implemented at facilities. Expanded virtual care models represent the natural evolution of virtual nursing beyond traditional hospital boundaries. Integration with hospital-at-home programs can extend virtual nursing support into patients' residences, while virtual specialist consultations can provide expert care access regardless of geographic limitations. Remote monitoring capabilities for post-discharge care can reduce readmissions and support care continuity, and comprehensive telehealth platform integration will create seamless care experiences that span multiple settings and provider types. Conclusion Hybrid-virtual nursing represents a transformative digital health solution that directly addresses the workforce challenges facing both IPPS hospitals and Critical Access Hospitals while improving patient care quality and operational efficiency. The technology aligns with CMS priorities for advancing interoperability, supporting value-based care, and leveraging digital health products to enhance Medicare beneficiary outcomes. We recommend that CMS consider virtual nursing as a priority area for policy development, quality measurement, and reimbursement clarification. The evidence demonstrates significant potential for improving patient care, supporting nursing workforce sustainability, and advancing the goals of a patient-centered digital health ecosystem.
