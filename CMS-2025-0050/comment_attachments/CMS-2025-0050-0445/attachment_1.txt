INTRODUCTION We appreciate the opportunity to submit this response to CMS and ASTP/ONC’s RFI on the future of the health technology ecosystem. This submission reflects a convergence of longstanding professional partnerships—spanning more than two decades—grounded in public service, innovation leadership, and shared values around data access, security, and public trust. Our multidisciplinary team—comprising experts from systems integration, open-source infrastructure, public-private innovation strategy, clinical informatics, and AI—brings proven experience in delivering secure, scalable, and patient-centric technology solutions across federal healthcare. We draw upon operational success across CMS, VA, DoD, HHS, and state Medicaid programs to provide pragmatic, standards-based innovation grounded in real-world implementation.
II. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY This collaborative submission outlines our strategy to: •Build a future-ready, interoperable digital health ecosystem centered on patients and aligned to Medicare; •Reduce reliance on proprietary systems through open standards, APIs, and modular hybrid-cloud architectures; •Advance AI-enabled analytics and workflow automation with explainability, security, and ethical guardrails, including clinical-grade AI validation, continuous model monitoring, and reinforcement learning approaches; •Enable secure federated digital identity and verification consistent with NIST 800-63-3, with emphasis on interoperability across payers and providers and identity assurance traceability; •Harmonize clinical and semantic data exchange using FHIR®, TEFCA (as a base layer), and trust frameworks—while acknowledging the need to go beyond TEFCA to realize real-time, patient-directed innovation. Additional emphasis includes: •Creation of CMS-curated Digital Health Product Trustmarks to foster confidence and innovation; •Greater interagency collaboration with NIH, VA, FDA, and ARPA-H to unify federal investments; •Application of CMS’s Health Equity Framework through multilingual, culturally sensitive, broadband-resilient, and mobile-first design, with a focus on rural and aging populations; •Implementation of outcome-driven KPIs to track adoption, quality improvements, and burden reduction. This proposal aligns with CMS’s Framework for Health Equity 2022–2032, particularly Goals 1, 3, and 5 related to expanding standardized data, aligning delivery ecosystems, and increasing culturally competent, person-centered access.
