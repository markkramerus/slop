The Non-Emergency Medical Transportation industry represents a significant opportunity for
Medicare and Medicare Advantage plans to enhance member outcomes and reduce long-term
costs. While NEMT is critical for connecting vulnerable populations—especially those in Special
Needs Plans—to essential care appointments, only 30% of MA plans currently offer this benefit
in 2025 according to the Kaiser Family Foundation. Without reliable transportation access,
members frequently miss preventive care visits, leading to delayed diagnoses, worsening
chronic conditions, and overall driving up healthcare costs through potentially avoidable
emergency interventions and hospitalization. Expanding NEMT coverage across Medicare and
Medicare Advantage could create a more sustainable healthcare delivery model while improving
member satisfaction and outcomes.
Investing in modern technology and streamlined systems is essential for improving the efficiency
and reliability of NEMT services. By prioritizing innovation, CMS, State Medicaid Agencies and
Managed Care Organizations can enhance the quality of care for vulnerable populations,
reduce inefficiencies, and create a more accessible healthcare system for all.
As the nation's first digitally integrated healthcare transportation platform, Kinetik has
demonstrated how technology can eliminate barriers to accessing care while creating
transparency and peace of mind for members, families, and healthcare stakeholders. Our
platform supports both transportation providers and health plans in enabling interoperable and
member-centric care delivery. Through our direct experience serving Medicaid, Medicare and
Medicare Advantage Special Needs plan members with diverse healthcare needs who rely on
NEMT services, we have witnessed how real-time communication, proactive care coordination,
and connected digital health solutions close critical gaps in access to care. In response to
CMS’s RFI (PC-1), we outline how digital health apps like Kinetik that enable non-emergency
medical transportation can help individuals and their loved ones manage health needs and
foster transparency and communication. We offer the following comments, drawn from our
real-world experience developing and integrating digital tools for members, caregivers, payers,
and providers.

PC-1. What health management or care navigation apps would help you understand and
manage your (or your loved ones) health needs, as well as the actions you should take?
Transportation is a critical component to accessing care for the Medicare population. For many
Medicare members, their ability to access needed care hinges on whether they have access to
transportation. That means CMS, State Medicaid Agencies, and Health Plans have a
responsibility to make requesting transportation as accessible and user-friendly as possible.
Kinetik recommends the following components to assist Medicare members, their families, and
loved ones in accessing transportation to connect to care.
Request and Manage NEMT Rides
○ Need: More flexible options for Members and their caregivers to request rides
and manage their NEMT benefit. Members and their caregivers are often
required to make a phone call to request a ride.
○ Solution: The Kinetik digital health app allows users to request NEMT rides
directly at their convenience (24/7) through a user-friendly interface. The app
verifies eligibility and schedules rides with preferred transportation providers.
Users are prompted to answer a series of questions that ensure the most
appropriate mode of transportation is sourced - this includes an opportunity to
add any special instructions, such as “member requires extra time to descend
stairs” or “needs wheelchair-accessible vehicle,” ensuring tailored
accommodations.
○ Impact: Kinetik meets members where they are by offering options for managing
their transportation benefit. This streamlines access to care, reduces missed
appointments, and enhances member autonomy, especially for those with
mobility or chronic health needs.
Real-Time Ride Status and Driver Tracking
○ Need: Uncertainty about transportation status or difficulties connecting with the
driver leads to frequent calls inquiring, “Where’s my ride?” This creates
administrative burdens and unnecessary member anxiety.
○ Solution: The Kinetik member app provides real-time updates on ride status (e.g.,
“Driver assigned,” “En route,” “Arrived”) and allows users to track their driver’s
location on a map, similar to ride-sharing platforms. This transparency reduces
uncertainty and empowers users to plan accordingly. For example, a member
could see that their driver is 10 minutes away and prepare to leave.
○ Impact: Decreases call volume to health plans, enhances the member
experience, and improves efficiency for NEMT providers.
a. What are the top things you would like to be able to do for your or your loved ones'
health that can be enabled by digital health products?

An issue that Kinetik has identified among key NEMT stakeholders, such as care coordinators,
family members, and loved ones, is the lack of visibility to ensure the member and/or loved one
has made their way to their medical appointment safely. In a technological world where
individuals are able to track their burrito being made, getting picked up, estimated time of arrival,
and where the driver is, there is no excuse as to why this same technology isn’t available for
Medicare members and their loved ones to track their medical ride. Kinetik has made this
possible with our fully digitized, interoperable closed-loop ecosystem, ensuring our Member App
is capable of delivering complete transparency to members and their loved ones. Empowering
members to take ownership of their healthcare journey with an intuitive tool offers peace of mind
to all including the members themselves, their care coordinators, family members, and loved
ones.
Safety and Communication with Care Coordinators and Loved Ones
○ Need: members and caregivers value safety, security, and the ability to keep
others informed during medical transportation.
○ Solution: The Kinetik platform enables users to notify care coordinators, family
members, or loved ones when they begin their ride, sharing details such as the
estimated arrival time. Authorized individuals can track the ride’s progress in
real-time via the app, ensuring peace of mind. For instance, a caregiver could
receive an alert when their loved one boards the ride and monitor their location
until they reach the medical facility.
○ Impact: Enhances safety, builds trust in NEMT services, and fosters collaboration
among members, caregivers, and care teams.

TD-13 What new opportunities and advancements could emerge with APIs providing
access to the entirety of a patient's electronic health information (EHI)?
Universal API access to complete member Electronic Health Information (EHI) would enable
comprehensive care coordination across the entire healthcare ecosystem. Currently, critical data
points are siloed - a member's transportation history, specialist visits, emergency room
encounters, and routine checkups exist in separate systems that don't communicate. With full

EHI access, every touchpoint in a member's journey could be digitized and connected
end-to-end.
It is a common occurrence for a hospital to pay for a taxi cab to discharge their member to free
up their bed to a new patient instead of waiting the typical 48-hour window of NEMT request and
ride fulfillment. Imagine if a discharge coordinator were able to request an on-demand NEMT
trip right from a member’s EHI and hospital workflow system. Not only would it save time and
resources on sourcing a ride, but it would log this data point in the EHI. Additionally, it could
develop algorithms to automatically prompt, or even schedule a trip for members if they do not
have their own transportation.
This interoperability would transform healthcare delivery by ensuring providers have complete
member context, reducing medical errors caused by incomplete information, and enabling
predictive analytics to identify care gaps before they become crises. For NEMT providers like
Kinetik, transportation patterns could reveal health trends, medication adherence issues, or
social determinants of health that impact member outcomes.
The resulting ecosystem optimization would free up resources currently wasted on manual data
entry and phone calls, allowing healthcare workers to focus on high-value member care
activities. This efficiency translates directly to improved health outcomes and reduced costs
across the system.
a. What are the primary obstacles to this?
The fundamental challenge is that the current system incentivizes data hoarding rather than
sharing. Major EHI vendors have built profitable business models around proprietary systems
that make integration deliberately expensive and complex. These companies may not see
immediate value in facilitating seamless data exchange, particularly with smaller healthcare
technology companies.
Financial barriers are significant, especially for healthcare startups and smaller organizations.
API integration costs, certification fees, and ongoing maintenance expenses can reach
hundreds of thousands of dollars annually. This creates a barrier to entry that favors established
players over innovative solutions.
Data integrity concerns compound these challenges. Too many healthcare processes still rely
on manual data transfer - members filling out forms in pen, faxing documents between
providers, or phone-based information sharing. Each human handoff introduces opportunities for
error, creating a "telephone game" effect with critical health information.
The irony is that members routinely share personal data for minimal benefits - discount
coupons, social media engagement, or convenience features - yet the healthcare system treats
their medical information as if safely and appropriately sharing it for better care coordination is
inherently problematic. Most members would gladly share their health data if it meant more
effective treatment and better health outcomes.

Breaking this cycle requires cooperation from major EHI vendors, regulatory alignment, and a
fundamental shift toward viewing comprehensive data sharing as essential infrastructure rather
than a competitive advantage. The stakes are too high to continue tolerating fragmented
systems. Policy must prioritize accurate information sharing and coordinated care to project
millions of lives.
