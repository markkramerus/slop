Azuba Corporation – RFI Response to CMS-0042-NC
Azuba respectfully submits the following detailed responses to CMS's Request for Information (CMS-
0042-NC), focusing on empowering Medicare beneficiaries through advanced digital health tools,
expanded interoperability, and real-time data exchange.
Executive Summary
In alignment with CMS’s bold vision and the urgency to think beyond legacy models, Azuba is
committed to helping CMS redefine the health IT landscape. This includes enabling a:
• Future where identity-proofed credentials allow seamless access across all health-related
apps and devices;
• System where data quality is objectively assessed at the source and clinical insights are
delivered to the point of care; and
• Transition from claims-based reimbursement to clinical FHIR® API-driven payments, which
removes redundant stakeholder infrastructure (e.g., Revenue Cycle Management) through
interoperable automation.
Azuba Corporation's Health Communication Platform addresses CMS’s RFI priorities by enabling
real-time, multilingual communications (in 86 languages) to lifetime clinical records and
configurable bi-directional communications for payers, providers, and beneficiaries. We support over
220 APIs, including all approved HL7® FHIR® APIs, to deliver seamless data exchange, personalized
member experiences, and streamlined workflows to any stakeholder.
Azuba Enables:
• Real-time, multilingual access to 90+% of U.S.-based longitudinal clinical records;
• TEFCA-aligned, standards-based interoperability across more than 500 EHR systems;
• Integration of structured and unstructured formats (PDF, FHIR, CDA, HL7v2, free text, etc.);
• Tens of thousands of compliant, well- governed communication workflows that support
billions of communications that improve care navigation, ensure regulatory compliance &
agency/brand governance and significantly enhance member experience; and
• Personalized outreach workflows for all CMS stakeholders: payers, providers, app developers,
ACOs, researchers, approved caregivers, data-exchange partners, etc.

By building a ready-to-pilot interoperable clinical data ecosystem that connects to more than 500

RFI Recommendations
Overall, Azuba’s comments include workflow descriptions, interoperability insights, and
recommendations aligned to CMS-0042-NC question codes. Our comments also cover critical areas
including TEFCA implementation, bulk data processing, identity management, and value-based
care enablement. This response highlights actionable feedback and practical workflows to inform
CMS’s digital health strategy.
PC-2
Azuba recommends CMS expand Blue Button 2.0 to include real-time clinical data from EHRs,
SDOH inputs, device-generated data, and language preference metadata. Azuba enables Medicare
beneficiaries to access a unified, lifetime clinical record – including claims, labs, imaging, encounter
notes, and ADT feeds – from across providers (500+ EMR systems) and payers. Informed by over 300
million transactions and built on FHIR® and TEFCA principles, Azuba’s platform supports structured
and unstructured formats, integrates 86 languages, and eliminates the need for multiple portals.
PC-9
The Blue Button 2.0 API should evolve to support use cases like preventive alerts, longitudinal care
summaries, and member education. Azuba suggests CMS includes additional endpoints for
structured problem lists, immunization history, genomics, care plans, and prescription fill history.
PC-14
Azuba supports preregistration workflows that leverage a Digital Insurance Card, USCDI data, and
federated digital identity to eliminate clipboard-driven intake. Our platform automates this process
across smartphones, web portals, and call centers – empowering members, reducing intake errors,
and improving administrative efficiency.
Azuba embeds secure digital identity into its health communication workflows and supports
integration with ID.me, Login.gov, and other NIST-compliant credential service providers (CSPs). We
recommend CMS implement a digital identity incentive model for Medicare Advantage (MA) plans
and TEFCA participants to increase adoption and reduce identity fragmentation across the
ecosystem. Additionally, CMS should launch a centralized portal where patients can manage and
revoke app authorizations linked to their verified identity credentials.
PR-8
Bulk FHIR® exports from EHRs can reduce provider burden by offloading quality measure
calculations to payers or certified platforms. Azuba processes bulk exports and supports
deduplication, gap closure tracking, and submission-ready metrics for CMS quality programs like
Stars, HEDIS, and MIPS. This helps providers reduce EHR time while maintaining reporting fidelity.
TD-5
CMS should champion a model akin to the internet's Domain Name System (DNS), where payer and
provider FHIR® API endpoints are registered, validated, and discoverable in real time. This DNS-style
registry would empower any certified app to dynamically locate and connect with participating
organizations – eliminating the need for proprietary integrations or bilateral agreements.
Azuba recommends that CMS maintain a national FHIR® endpoint directory, indexed by NPI, to
ensure consistency across app deployment, testing, and network access. This directory should
include:
• Endpoint URL
• Supported FHIR® version
• TEFCA/QHIN participation
• Credentialing level (IAL2/AAL2)
• Access type (patient/provider/payer)

This architecture would mirror SWIFT’s global financial messaging registry, offering an interoperable

PC-1
Azuba recommends CMS promote care navigation tools that support aging populations, non-
English speakers, and caregivers. Our platform assists members with understanding benefits,
completing forms, and preparing for appointments.
PC-10
Azuba supports TEFCA and currently operates with a TEFCA-aligned architecture. We recommend
CMS incentivize app developers to integrate with QHINs and request CMS define public reporting
metrics for TEFCA performance. Azuba recommends CMS fund onboarding grants for small vendors
or FQHCs.
VB-3
Essential Health Information Technology (HIT) capabilities for APMs (Alternative Payment Models)
include access to real-time longitudinal data, attribution visibility, remote engagement tools, and
configurable alerts. Azuba supports these through smart templates, role-based views, and real-time
ADT alerting.
CMS should recast CEHRT certification to focus less on static EHR features and more on FHIR® API
fidelity, reliability, and scope. Redefining certification to prioritize API maturity, longitudinal
completeness, and support for dynamic use cases (e.g., identity exchange, SDOH tagging, AI-model
optimization) will unlock innovation both inside and outside traditional EHR boundaries.
TD-8
Azuba recommends redefining certification to prioritize API performance, access to full longitudinal
data, and ability to integrate with TEFCA endpoints. Current gaps exist in certifying capabilities like
member language preference capture, SDOH tagging, and gap closure communication – all
supported within Azuba workflows. CMS should incorporate dynamic certification updates aligned
to evolving data exchange patterns.
Reimagining a Better Future
CMS should pilot a model where claims submission is eliminated entirely, replaced by real-time
FHIR®-based clinical encounter APIs. Azuba supports such a shift and has already deployed
infrastructure enabling structured clinical data capture from providers at the point of care. This can
be validated against CMS coverage criteria and trigger immediate adjudication, closing the
feedback loop in seconds, not weeks.
We further propose CMS fund proof-of-concept demonstrations (see below, “Available for
Demonstration from Azuba”) that show how real-time clinical data access – paired with secure
digital identity and consent – can radically simplify processes, eliminate fraud pathways, and support
quality-based care and eliminate redundant intermediaries.
Azuba believes this RFI represents a once-in-a-generation opportunity to move beyond piecemeal
digital reform. We support CMS in establishing a “good –better –best” glide path to national
interoperability that evolves from:
• Good = CCD/Direct exchange through Carequality;
• Better = TEFCA QHIN access supporting multiple use cases,; or
• Best = CMS-led, API-first, digitally verified, internet-driven architecture where individuals
are co-equal participants in care delivery, decision-making, and data sharing.
