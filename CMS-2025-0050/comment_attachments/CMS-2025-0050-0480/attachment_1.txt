 Public Comment Submission: CMS-0042-NC Submitted by: CaringWire, LLC Date: June 16, 2025 I. Executive Summary CaringWire operates SDOHConnect, a digital health navigation platform designed to identify health-related risks and connect individuals—especially Medicare and Medicaid beneficiaries—to personalized support services. Through direct integration with a national network of over 70,000 pharmacies, CaringWire delivers real-time Health Risk Assessments (HRAs) and Health-Related Social Needs (HRSN) screenings using trusted, community-based channels. Our system enables efficient patient engagement, interoperable data exchange, and scalable “closed-loop” navigation that tracks outcomes and informs value-based care strategies. II. Recommendations and Responses Empower Pharmacists for Broader Engagement Pharmacies are the most frequent and trusted access point for millions of beneficiaries. CMS should expand pharmacist engagement authority to include: ● Patient assessments (including HRAs and HRSN/SDOH screens) ● Benefit education, eligibility verification and navigation ● Digital enrollment support (e.g., medically tailored meals, transportation, preventive services) Formal recognition of pharmacists as care navigators would significantly advance reach, reduce disparities, and alleviate provider burden—particularly in rural and underserved communities. Recognize Infrastructure Like SDOHConnect as Standard CMS should consider platforms like SDOHConnect as national infrastructure for benefit delivery and patient engagement. These systems are optimized for: 
● High-touch engagement through trusted relationships ● Interoperability with health plans and providers ● Real-time identification of needs and timely connection to benefits ● Closed-loop tracking of whether services were accessed or barriers remain We recommend CMS include such capabilities in pilot programs, certification pathways, and APM operational standards to support health equity and value-based care goals. III. Alignment with CMS Questions PC-1 to PC-5 – Health Management Tools SDOHConnect equips patients to complete HRA and HRSN assessments and immediately access a curated “Member Action Plan” with local and national service options. The platform supports closed-loop referrals by tracking whether individuals acted on recommendations and whether gaps in care remain unresolved. This functionality—delivered through a patient’s trusted pharmacy—offers a more complete picture of patient needs and outcomes, which can inform both clinical and operational workflows. PC-8 to PC-9 – Data Access & Integration We support expanding the Blue Button 2.0 API to include patient-reported social needs and real-time engagement outcomes. Our platform’s ability to structure and share these insights in a standards-based way strengthens care coordination, risk stratification, and performance improvement. TD-1, TD-2, TD-4 – Innovation & Developer Enablement CaringWire encourages CMS to promote the use of open APIs, identity standards, and pharmacy-based data exchange to support national-scale digital engagement. Tools like ours are optimized for identifying at-risk individuals based on criteria such as plan eligibility, clinical conditions, demographics, and geography—making them essential infrastructure for population health. VB-1 to VB-4 – Value-Based Care Integration SDOHConnect supports early identification and navigation for patients who would otherwise face costly downstream complications. Our platform creates actionable data for ACOs and other APM participants, enabling proactive support and outcome documentation aligned with value-based metrics. 
IV. Closing We thank CMS and ASTP/ONC for taking a bold step toward building an equitable, patient-centered digital health ecosystem. CaringWire encourages the agency to: ● Broaden pharmacists’ role in digital care management ● Recognize platforms like SDOHConnect as scalable, compliant infrastructure ● Include “closed-loop” referral tracking and pharmacy-based engagement in future policy design We welcome the opportunity to share additional insights, results, and demonstrations to support CMS’s ongoing work.
