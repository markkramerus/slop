Recommendations For Impact
Increasing Access to Care
Providers face immediate barriers to policy innovations and cost-cutting in Medicaid and
Medicare due to outdated, consultant-built software and data systems. Providers, especially
those in rural areas, find it difficult to bridge the gap between outdated processes and
modern digital standards. Digital health products like the Healthy Together solution provide
a modernized approach that significantly reduces the time, cost, and resources that are
currently required by replacing labor-intensive manual processes. One of our clients
reported that a single new enrollment can require up to 800 keyboard clicks using their
existing Medicaid system. Patients are forced to wait in line to meet with a Medicaid
administrator in order to submit a benefit application. Once submitted, applicants wait days
or weeks to be enrolled in programs. Healthy Together systems are purpose-built to
automate manual processes, eliminate bottle-necks, and allow benefit applicants to apply
from the comfort of their homes, which saves providers time, money, and resources.

Improving Healthcare Outcomes
Healthy Together recommends the following to improve healthcare outcomes:
1. As state and territory agencies are already implementing Integrated Eligibility &
Enrollment with other HHS and FNS programs, CMS should seek OMB A-87 waivers for
any agency that wishes to integrate multiple programs into the state-run Medicaid
system. The OMB A-87 waiver will reduce administrative and accounting challenges by
allowing Medicaid agencies to cover the cost for smaller programs utilizing the IE&E
system without seeking reimbursement from those programs, expediting the
modernization process.
2. Cease funding Design, Develop & Implement (DD&I) models of legacy-style systems that
cannot be modernized to the new standards: modularity, cloud-native, composable
SaaS, event-based tracking, and polymorphic data models.
3. Provide state and territory agencies with a “Turbo-Tax” style system to facilitate and
automate Advance Planning Document (APD) creation. Create a case management
system that uses ML and AI to support CMS administrators in the APD review and
approval process. Re-vamp the T-MSIS reporting process by requiring MMIS/MES to
utilize event-based tracking. T-MSIS reporting is a key bottleneck for identifying
opportunities to improve health outcomes and finding fraud. T-MSIS data submitted by
state agencies is often incomplete, inaccurate, and not real-time. Some state agencies
have not automated T-MSIS report creation, creating a significant burden on
administrators to generate and transmit reports. By requiring event-based tracking in
Claims Systems, CMS can future-proof the T-MSIS system by ensuring that all data is
collected and ready for transmission and analysis at all times.
4. Create a system for ongoing management information system certification that uses
open APIs, data flows, and interfaces for state and territory agencies to automatically
maintain certification of their Enrollment & Eligibility and Medicaid Enterprise Systems.
State and territory IE&E and MMIS/MES are required to comply with ongoing
certification to receive funding for M&O. This process is cumbersome, time-consuming,
and expensive, and can be automated with modern tools for data extraction, testing and
automation. This system could be deployed as a standalone CMS system or as part of a
CMS-provided “base operating system” which would have certification tools and reports
pre-configured.
5. Explore ways these modern tools can be implemented to reduce cost and improve the
efficacy of Medicare and other CMS programs.
