Executive Summary
MediKarma appreciates the opportunity to submit this response to the Centers for
Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) Request for Information. Our response addresses
critical opportunities to enhance the nation's healthcare infrastructure by supporting
preventive, data-driven, AI-enabled solutions that empower patients, improve outcomes,
and reduce the burden of chronic disease.
MediKarma’s Agentic AI platform is designed to operationalize wellness as a default state,
not an exception, by integrating electronic medical records (EMRs), behavioral insights,
biometric data (generated by both consumer and medical grade wearable health devices),
and AI-driven coaching to proactively guide individuals toward healthier lifestyles. This
submission outlines how CMS can help unlock the full potential of these technologies
through targeted regulatory and policy support.
Background: The Case for Preventive, Proactive Care
The accelerating shift toward value-based care—further driven by the emergence of the
MAHA era, necessitates a new paradigm in healthcare delivery. Rather than treating illness
only after it manifests, the healthcare system must evolve to support ongoing, preventive
health maintenance.
MediKarma addresses this gap by providing individuals with real-time, personalized
insights to make healthier decisions every day. Our Agentic AI platform identifies early
warning signs and delivers behaviorally intelligent nudges to help users avoid or reverse
chronic conditions before they require clinical intervention.
MediKarma Platform Overview
MediKarma operates a business-to-business-to-consumer (B2B2C) platform that leverages

• Preventive Care Wellness Coaching, via Agent Jill
• Chronic Care Management, via Agent Jill
• Enterprise AI for Care Management, via Agent Jack

By fusing clinical, behavioral, and biometric data, our agents generate personalized care

Data Integration: Enabling Real-Time, Holistic Insights
A critical enabler of MediKarma’s approach is the seamless integration of EMR data with
behavioral, environmental, and biometric inputs (e.g., from wearables and remote
monitoring devices). Our platform constructs a dynamic and continuous health profile for
each user, which our AI agents use to guide and coach individuals in real time.
However, current industry practices often inhibit the free flow of health data. Wearables
and device manufacturers frequently restrict real-time third-party access, and data
standards vary across systems, creating friction that undermines innovation and patient
empowerment.
Transformative Impact of Real-Time Wellness Models
When integrated systems like MediKarma are deployed, the impact is substantial:
• Fewer medications and emergency interventions
• Increased patient autonomy and vitality
• Earlier detection and prevention of chronic disease
Such models support a future where chronic conditions like diabetes, hypertension, and
obesity are not only better managed, but also prevented or reversed through accessible,
scalable, AI-powered engagement.
Policy Recommendations to CMS
To fully realize the promise of platforms like MediKarma, we respectfully recommend the
following policy actions:
1. Ensure Bi-directional EMR Data Exchange
Establish policies that mandate real-time, bi-directional data exchange between EMRs and
person-centered wellness platforms—not solely among providers. This promotes
continuity of care and supports holistic, personalized wellness interventions.
2. Clarify and Support Non-Treatment Data Use
Clarify that frameworks such as CareQuality and TEFCA permit access to clinical data for
wellness and preventive purposes, provided appropriate patient consent is obtained. This
ensures consumer-facing platforms are not excluded from contributing to public health
goals.

3. Accelerate Nationwide Data Liquidity
Incentivize EHR vendors, especially smaller EHR vendors, to participate in TEFCA. Broader
inclusion ensures equitable access to clinical data and supports innovation across the
digital health ecosystem.
4. Ensure Access to Medical Device Data
Prohibit device manufacturers, such as continuous glucose monitor (CGM) companies,
from blocking real-time data access by third-party developers. Establish standards that
prioritize interoperability and real-time availability of patient-owned data.
Advancing Consumer Trust and Market Adoption
5. Establish a CMS/ONC Certification Program
Create a formal “good housekeeping” seal or certification for digital health products that
meet national standards for:
• Security and Privacy
• Interoperability and Accessibility
• This program should also clearly communicate patient benefits such as cost
reduction, proactive health reminders, and improved care coordination.
6. Expand Access via Standardized APIs
Mandate and support the use of standardized FHIR-based APIs for data exchange. CMS
should also promote implementation guidance and reduce barriers to entry for developers
seeking access to EHR data for consumer-authorized use cases.
Infrastructure and Incentives to Support Innovation
7. Develop a Trusted Digital Identity Framework
Support the creation of a standardized digital identity framework and approve vendors to
streamline identity verification. MediKarma currently employs rigorous identity verification
protocols but welcomes opportunities to enhance efficiency and reduce user friction.
8. Incentivize Consumer and Provider Participation
Introduce financial and operational incentives that promote engagement with digital health
tools:
• Provide regulatory flexibility to APM and Medicare Advantage participants that
deploy certified technologies

• Offer bonuses to ACOs and MSSPs that deliver digital care navigation and wellness
coaching tools
• Support PMPM payments tied to consumer engagement and measurable health
outcomes
• Expand technical assistance to under-resourced providers
• Offer legal clarity on the safe and appropriate use of digital health tools to reduce
provider liability concerns
