TD-16. Point-to-point health information exchange models offer technical simplicity and fast implementation by creating direct, bilateral connections between healthcare entities. While this approach works well for immediate, small-scale connectivity, it becomes unsustainable at scale— requiring thousands of individual integrations as participants grow. Each connection demands custom interfaces, security protocols, and data mappings, placing a significant operational burden on organizations. In contrast, shared network infrastructures—such as Health Information Exchanges (HIEs) and TEFCA—enable scalability by allowing organizations to connect once and exchange data with all participants, dramatically reducing integration complexity and cost. These models benefit from network effects, increasing adoption and data availability as participation grows, and they allow for shared cost structures that make advanced capabilities more accessible across resource-constrained settings. While point-to-point models may offer faster response times due to direct routing, their scalability and interoperability challenges are significant—particularly in enterprise environments with multiple EHR vendors.
