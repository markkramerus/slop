 1 | Copyright © 2025 Rhapsody CMS-0042-NC Request for Information: Health Technology Ecosystem Introduction to Rhapsody Health’s RFI Response Rhapsody Health appreciates the opportunity to respond to the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) and the Office of the National Coordinator for Health Information Technology (ONC) on shaping the future of the national health technology ecosystem. As a global leader in healthcare data interoperability and digital enablement, we are uniquely positioned to support CMS’s strategic vision of a connected, equitable, and patient-empowered healthcare system. With over 1,700 healthcare organizations relying on our platform across 17 countries—including health systems, payers, government agencies, public health networks, and digital health innovators—Rhapsody offers deep technical and operational expertise in the movement, normalization, governance, and secure exchange of healthcare data. Our technologies serve as the connective tissue behind thousands of clinical and administrative workflows every day, facilitating critical functions such as patient identity resolution, claims exchange, remote monitoring integration, and real-time clinical decision support. We bring to this RFI a track record of: • Proven performance in national and regional interoperability initiatives, including HIEs, TEFCA-aligned networks, and FHIR API implementations. • Leadership in semantic normalization and terminology services, ensuring data retains its meaning and utility across systems. • Advanced EMPI capabilities, supporting accurate patient and provider identity resolution—essential for longitudinal records and digital identity frameworks. • Support for modern interoperability standards, including HL7 v2, FHIR R4, CDA, and X12, alongside emerging paradigms like Bulk FHIR, SMART on FHIR, and CDS Hooks. Beyond compliance, Rhapsody is committed to enabling a new generation of digital health applications—those that empower patients, ease provider burden, enhance care navigation, and drive value-based outcomes. Our perspective is informed not only by our technology, but by our role as an ecosystem integrator: bridging gaps between stakeholders, systems, and data silos to realize CMS’s vision of a learning health system. We are excited to share insights, capabilities, and ideas across all relevant domains of this RFI and stand ready to support CMS in building a resilient, interoperable, and patient-centered health technology future.
