To Whom It May Concern:
Memorial Healthcare System is pleased to offer this letter of support regarding the CMS RFI titled Advancing a
Patient-Centric Digital Health Ecosystem (CMS-3421-NC), and specifically to endorse the concepts and
direction represented in the response submitted by Remote Care Partners (dba HealthyConnect).
Over the past 16 months, Memorial has partnered with HealthyConnect to deploy remote patient monitoring
(RPM) technology, biometric equipment, and coordinated outreach services to high-risk patients under a
capitated Medicare Advantage contract. While this is not a randomized or controlled study, the impact has
been substantial. We have seen impressive reductions in unnecessary utilization, increased engagement in
primary care, and measurable improvement in medication adherence and biometric control for this high-risk
population.
Based on these encouraging outcomes, we are actively exploring broader deployment of this infrastructure—
including Hospital at Home initiatives, Medicaid populations, transitional care, community-based discharges,
and our CIN-affiliated provider network. These efforts are being undertaken in collaboration with our partners
at Telemetrix and Remote Care Partners, in which Memorial via its’ respective fund called Florida Community
Health Network is an investor in Telemetrix only.
While the full stack of AI-driven capabilities continues to evolve, the underlying approach of combining
intelligent outreach, remote monitoring, and structured care planning represents the type of systemic
improvement CMS and HHS should champion. We support the policy recommendations outlined in the
submitted RFI response and encourage continued leadership by the Department in defining standards that
promote real-time coordination, data usability, and scalable engagement.
