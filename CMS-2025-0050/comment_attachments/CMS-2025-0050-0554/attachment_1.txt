Re: Request for Information; Health Technology Ecosystem (CMS -0042-NC) Dear Dr. Oz : Abbott welcomes the opportunity to comment on the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) and Office of the National Coordinator for Health Information Technology (ONC) request for information (RFI) on the Health Technology Ecosystem. Abbott is committed to helping people live their best possible life through the power of health. For more than 135 years, we’ve brought new products and technologies to the world – in diagnostics, medical devices, nutrition, and branded generic pharmaceuticals – that create more possibilities for more people at all stages of life. Today, 114,000 Abbott employees are working to help people live not just longer, but better, in the more than 160 countries we serve. As a leading medical technology and nutrition manufacturer, we seek to ensure that CMS policies promote Medicare beneficiary access to high-quality healthcare innovations that address unmet medical needs and improve health outcomes . We therefore commend CMS’s intention expressed in the RFI to “reduce barriers to data access and exchange, realize the potential of recent innovations in healthcare that promote better health outcomes, and accelerate progress towards a patient -centric learning health system. ” Our specific comments follow. The code descriptor, like the national coverage determination (NCD) , for G0248 includes the language “face-to -face” when describing the training. In 2025, face-to -face can be achieved by video technology. This mechanism of providing remote training was not available when CMS last revised the NCD in 2008. Technology has evolved such that face-to -face services can be furnished in- person or virtually. Furthermore, CMS has defined the term “face- to-face” to be an in-person or virtual encounter in other circumstances. 1 We believe CMS has the authority to permit these services to be furnished in- person or virtually to Medicare patients -- without the addition to the Medicare telehealth list. Specifically, we ask that the Agency permit the services described by G0248 to be furnished in -person or virtually in light of the inherently remote nature of home PT/INR monitoring . CMS has acknowledged that other inherently remote services may be furnished virtually or in- person without requiring such services to be added to the telehealth list .2 In addition, as noted above, there is no language in the code descriptor or in the NCD itself that requires this service (described by G0248) be furnished in -person; rather, the service must only be furnished face -to- face. PR-7. What strategies can CMS implement to support providers in making high- quality, timely, and comprehensive healthcare data available for interoperability in the digital product ecosystem? • We are particularly interested in how CMS uses electronic clinical quality measures (eCQMs) to enhance the healthcare experience in Medicare quality programs. CMS has noted that “[b] y utilizing electronic data captured during the routine process of clinical care, the eCQM has become a critical component of the quality reporting framework. ”3 We believe that CMS can use eCGMs to advance its goal of improving the availability of high-quality, timely, and comprehensive data available in the health information ecosystem, while promoting a separate Administration priority of incorporating nutrition as a key component of care quality . 4 Specifically, CMS has adopted the Malnutrition Care Score (MCS) eCQM into the Hospital Inpatient Quality Reporting (IQR) Program . The MCS eCQM assesses adults admitted to inpatient hospital services who received care appropriate to their level of malnutrition risk and malnutrition diagnosis. While hospitals may currently self -select the MCS eCQM to meet the eCQM reporting requirement, we encourage the agency to take the next step to enhance patient nutritional care by making the MCS a mandatory measure for not only the I QR program , but value-based care programs as well.
