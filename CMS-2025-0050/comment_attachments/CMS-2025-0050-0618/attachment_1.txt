Public Comment on CMS-2025-0050-0031 Dear CMS Team, As CMS explores modernization of the health technology ecosystem, we encourage the agency to prioritize digital innovation and standardization within the Independent Dispute Resolution (IDR) process under the No Surprises Act (NSA). The NSA's IDR framework is essential in protecting patients and resolving out-of-network payment disputes. However, the process remains overly manual and fragmented. By streamlining the IDR system with modern technology, CMS can: • Reduce administrative burden on providers, payers, and IDR entities • Improve compliance through standardized digital workflows • Enhance transparency and auditability of determinations • Facilitate real-time tracking of status, timelines, and fee processing • Improve access to structured data for oversight and analysis We recommend CMS: 1. Invest in a centralized IDR platform with secure bi-directional APIs that all parties (payers, providers, IDR Entities) can integrate with. 2. Adopt national standards for data exchange in IDR submissions, leveraging FHIR or similar protocols. 3. Enable real-time dashboards and status alerts to support compliance with statutory timelines. 4. Pilot AI-driven triage tools for dispute eligibility, batching validation, and decision support—without compromising neutrality. 5. Streamline the IDR Entity approval process to add capacity to the pool of IDREs possibly through a tech-enabled certification and monitoring process—ensuring timely onboarding of qualified entities while maintaining integrity and oversight. These improvements would not only increase efficiency but would also better align the IDR process with CMS’s broader goals for interoperability, equity, and transparency. Thank you for considering these suggestions as part of CMS’s ongoing tech innovation efforts. Sincerely, Michael Izhaky CEO & Founder, Qualtrate
