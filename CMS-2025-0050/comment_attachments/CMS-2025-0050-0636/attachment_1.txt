Overview
To deliver on its mandate to modernize Medicare, reduce waste, and improve patient
outcomes, CMS must ground innovation and operations/program execution in real-time
patient and beneficiary experience data - ideally using AI and technology that can
integrate and analyze feedback and experience data with meta-data and operational
systems, and route those relevant insights for action (not just raw data) across CMS for
agency wide improvements.
Similar to the practices and technology stack that the leading Fortune 500’s - including
healthcare Payers use - by capturing, routing, and surfacing voice-of-the-customer data
from Medicare Contact Centers (eg. 1-800-Medicare and web chats) or consolidated call
center efforts, Healthcare.gov, and other digital or journey touchpoints (most of which
exist at CMS but are siloed), CMS can be pro-active in driving more targeted
improvements, reduce operational waste, and elevate cross-agency performance.
Dr. Oz shared his Medicare enrollment experience and tips navigating with the public last
week. As he looks to improve that experience for the millions of eligible Americans and
others looking for a better healthcare journey, imagine if he, and his entire team across
CMS could receive continuous and personalized insights - understanding what actions
to take that are most effective for innovation/enrollment and to more effectively drive
down costs - across different patient journeys and omni-channel programs. This could
be to answer questions, improve, or get notified on a specific contact center team who
are not performing well, actions to reduce costs within or across Medicare Administrative
Contractors (MAC), nuances of what issues are specific to a jurisdiction or State along the
medicare journey, alerts on specific topics like fraud/waste/abuse, digital products not
working, repeat calling, etc.), or how effective a product or healthcare innovation launch
is going?
There are millions of experience data points existing within CMS currently and
continuously increasing via its contact centers - specifically in the National Data

Warehouse (NDW) and via digital/meta-data collected through other disparate systems-
that is siloed and held within a few programs/groups and contractors.
Moreover, the capacity to automate not only the insights from the calls to improve call
center operations (e.g. reduce call time, improve first call resolution, improve agent
retention - which amounts to tens to hundreds of millions of dollars saved each year)
most contracts and programs running the contact centers are not leverage experience
data in a way that is streamlining operations, saving money or reducing overhead.
As we have seen from the best and most complex companies and agencies, access to
and insights from customer experience data is not optional—it is mission-critical. And
using technology that can integrate, analyze at scale, and route dynamically in near-real
time so the whole of the CMS has relevant data to take the most effective and efficient
action. Without it, CMS risks designing systems in the dark, misallocating funds, and
leaving people behind, especially the most vulnerable. Data-informed CX is essential to
making Medicare more accessible, equitable, and trusted.

Current Challenges and Risk for CMS
The amazing plans that CMS has for major innovation, reducing waste, and drastically saving
costs and its stakeholders - will always be at risk of program failures, enrollment challenges,
rising servicing costs, and inefficient actions due if they do not have true insights from the voice
of the people and lack of ability to democratize insights across the agency (which leads to
lagging decision/improvements or rapid actions that cause major problems or costs due to a
siloed or nonexistent risk mitigation).
1. Reduced Enrollment Effectiveness
● Ineffective outreach and communication: Without insights into what information confuses
or engages customers, CMS may continue to produce materials or digital experiences
that are difficult to understand or poorly targeted.
● Missed enrollment opportunities: Without real-time data on drop-off points in the
enrollment funnel, CMS cannot identify where or why users abandon the process,
resulting in fewer enrollments.
2. Poor Customer Experience and Satisfaction

● Increased frustration: Without feedback from touchpoints (calls, web sessions, chatbot
use, etc.), CMS may be unaware of usability issues or barriers—such as confusing
eligibility steps, long hold times, or inaccessible digital platforms.
● Trust erosion: Negative experiences that go unaddressed can erode public trust in CMS
and government healthcare programs.
3. Government Blind Spots in Health Access
● One-size-fits-all from Washington: Without clear, localized data, federal agencies like
CMS risk creating bloated, top-down programs that overlook the real needs of seniors
and working Americans—especially in rural, low-income, or non-English-speaking
communities.
● Missed voices, wasted dollars: When bureaucracy ignores input from real people—such
as the disabled, immigrants, or those navigating the system without help—policies end
up wasting taxpayer money on generic solutions that don’t work on the ground.
4. Operational Inefficiencies and Waste
● Misallocation of resources: Without knowing which aspects of the enrollment journey
work or fail, CMS may overinvest in ineffective tools or underinvest in high-impact
services like live support or culturally competent outreach.
● Duplicative efforts: Lack of CX data can lead to siloed initiatives that don’t coordinate or
learn from each other, increasing administrative burden.
5. Stagnation in Innovation
● Limited data for policy pilots: Innovation requires evidence. Without robust user journey
data, CMS cannot evaluate or scale pilot programs (e.g., AI-driven chatbots, simplified
applications, or personalized plan recommendations).
● Slow responsiveness to change: CMS may not detect emerging user needs or behavior
shifts in time to adapt, such as increased mobile use or shifting perceptions of Medicare
Advantage.
6. Compliance and Oversight Gaps

● Failure to meet federal mandates: Agencies are increasingly required to measure and
improve customer experience (e.g., Executive Order 14058 on Transforming Federal
Customer Experience). Lack of CX data puts CMS at risk of non-compliance.
● Inability to demonstrate value: Without data showing improved experiences and
outcomes, CMS may face difficulty justifying budgets or policy proposals to stakeholders,
including Congress.
Solution
By leveraging a FedRamp High CX platform (with Speech Analytics that connects directly
with Digital feedback/insights capabilities and AI that routes insights across the
organization from top-bottom) CMS can rapidly convert voice-of-customer data (e.g.
100% visibility of calls, digital behavior,) into agency-wide action—unlocking: more
accurate understanding of progress in healthcare innovation/pilots/new programs,
patient engagement and issues in enrollment, and operational efficiency and operational
waste.
Leveraging the audio-files and associated meta data from calls from 1-800-Medicare
(located in the National Data Warehouse) can enable faster, more transparent routing of
feedback from call centers, HealthCare.gov, and post-service surveys directly to the
correct CMS teams and systems, Medallia allows CMS to:
● Improve patient engagement by acting on what matters most to
beneficiaries—reducing friction, confusion, and frustration;
● Enhance usability of CMS digital services by identifying the highest-impact
improvements across self-service journeys;
● Advance risk stratification by surfacing hidden signals of need or vulnerability
across demographic and regional lines;
● Accelerate care coordination by giving all departments and contractors access to
real-time experience trends and sentiment;
● Reduce costs and save time across all divisions—from policy to 100% transparency
across contact centers (including MAC oversight) to digital operations.

Contact Center and Quality Assurance Specific Solutions and Benefits
Example from a current agency using this method and technology for
multi-million dollar cost savings and time-savings
A current Federal agency has used audio files and meta-data (call records located in their cloud)
to not only realize the program, policity, and digital communication benefits across multiple
program and cross-sector objectives, but also better manage its contact centers and possible
consolidation.

Through this approach this agency has been able to set up a scalable solution to providing
vendor oversight of its most expensive line items – contact centers and servicing. With the
implementation of the Medallia Speech Tool, there are four areas where this solution is making it
possible to achieve these outcomes. Without this type of tool, these savings and improved
customer experience would not be possible at scale.
1. Self-service Drivers
An agent handling a phone call from a customer is the most expensive way a
contact center can provide service. Agency has developed a few self-serve
options but not all were effectively promoted or used, and others could be added.
These efforts and data associated are also siloed amongst vendors. Using the
Speech and Voice Analytics tool, Agency is able to pinpoint how many customers
are calling when they could have used an existing self-serve option. Armed with
this information, they will be able to assertively guide and direct more of their
callers to use self-serve options and thereby reduce the Agency’s overall call
volume (across its many contact centers). Private sector companies use this to
great effect and have experienced anywhere from 8% to 20% conversion of
callers to self-service users.
2. One-call Resolution Rate
While the Agency does not publicly publish a one-call Resolution benchmark,
best-in-class industry standards for our type of business ranges from 70% - 85%.
Given this Agency’s high abandon rate it is estimated that it could be anywhere
between 40% to 55% meaning as much as 60% of this Agency’s call volume could
be from repeat callers who have not been able to reach an agent or have gotten
inaccurate information when they did reach one. Reducing repeat callers has the
potential to significantly reduce call volumes and dramatically cut costs – in the
millions of dollars. Implementing this tool will make possible establishing a
One-call Resolution Rate benchmark and provide the insight on ways to
consistently improve it – without this type of tool it will not be possible.
3. Efficiency Savings
Based on feedback from the private and government sectors, users of this
technology have realized efficiency gains anywhere from 10% to 30%. Even using
the low end of this scale allows this Agency to perform more in depth and
significantly more effective QA without having to hire new personnel. These
efficiencies will also position this Agency to control the growth of staffing as the
customer base grows in the future.
4. Cost Avoidance

This Agency’s only remotely feasible alternative to investing in this type of
technical solution is to hire more people to achieve a fraction of the benefit. In
determining the most viable paths forward, increasing staffing was studied and
through it the Agency determined that it could potentially increase its sampling
rate of monitored calls to 2-3% from a current baseline of 0.5% but only by hiring 11
new contractor staff that would cost ~$9 million a year. This annual cost will be
avoided and hence saved with implementation of this tool while gaining the
benefit of capturing and transcribing 100% of recorded calls without having to hire
additional contractors or employees.
5. Vendor Downsizing or Elimination
The implementation of this type of tool will arm this Agency with a tremendous
amount of data and the AI that can be used to truly understand the volumes, the
nature, and types of contacts enabling them to organize its contact center work
more efficiently while maintaining effectiveness. With this tool, this Agency will be
able to identify opportunities to streamline and automate several workflows where
having 10+ contact centers may not be necessary and could possibly be reduced.
This will greatly simplify this Agency’s contractor footprint representing potentially
significant cost savings to the organization.
IV. Recommendation: Invest in Unified, Intelligent Feedback Infrastructure
We recommend CMS adopt a FedRamp High Customer Experience solution (with speech
analytics capabilities) as a central experience intelligence platform, integrated across
departments and systems, to:
● Use advanced CX technology to analyze all audio files/meta-data from the
National Data Warehouse, whereby it would automatically route and provide
role-based insights for improvements (by the job to be done across CMS’s
divisions, leadership, call centers, digital offices, ombudsman, etc) - for large scale
cost savings and innovation.
● Accelerate innovation cycles with voice-of-the-customer data;
● Reduce operational and contractor costs through targeted action;
● Route experience insights directly to accountable POCs for timely follow-up;
● Build a modern, interoperable technology foundation for future CMS programs.

This approach makes cost-effective innovation repeatable, measurable, and
scalable—anchored in the real experiences of Medicare beneficiaries.
