Connecting Providers Across the Ecosystem
Universal connectivity among all healthcare providers delivers substantial financial
benefits, generating billions in direct cost savings and unlocking additional value through
improved provider satisfaction, enhanced patient outcomes, and operational
efficiencies. Beyond these tangible gains, it paves the way for transformative digital innovation—
enabling the U.S. healthcare system to overcome foundational challenges and adopt advanced
care delivery models. These models integrate artificial intelligence, virtual care, and digital health
solutions, marking a pivotal shift toward a more connected, efficient, and patient-centered future.
Most healthcare professionals outside of health systems and physicians are not connected today. This is
for many reasons, including lack of incentives and requirements to connect.

Why Include Physical Therapy
Physical therapy is fundamental to overall healthcare and
value-based care because it provides non-invasive, cost-
effective interventions that prevent expensive surgeries,
reduce opioid dependence, and restore functional
independence. Early physical therapy intervention can
reduce total healthcare costs by 20-50% for musculoskeletal
conditions while significantly decreasing hospital
readmissions and recovery times. For chronic diseases like
diabetes, heart disease, and COPD, physical therapy
improves cardiovascular fitness and prevents costly
complications like falls and fractures, particularly in aging
populations.
From a value-based perspective, physical therapy addresses
both treatment and prevention by maintaining patient
mobility, reducing disability, improving workplace

Why Include Dental Providers
Dental care is fundamental to overall healthcare and value-
based care because oral health serves as both a gateway and
a mirror to systemic health conditions. Poor dental health is
strongly linked to serious chronic diseases including diabetes,
cardiovascular disease, stroke, and respiratory infections,
with periodontal disease acting as both a risk factor and
complicator for these conditions. For patients with diabetes,
untreated gum disease can make blood sugar control
significantly more difficult, while cardiovascular patients face
increased risk of heart complications from oral bacteria
entering the bloodstream.
Dental professionals often serve as the first line of detection
for serious conditions like diabetes, cardiovascular disease,
osteoporosis, and autoimmune disorders through oral
manifestations, but without interoperability, these critical
findings may never reach the patient's primary care physician

Why Include Behavioral Providers
Behavioral care is fundamental to overall healthcare and
value-based care because mental health conditions affect
over 50% of Americans and significantly worsen medical
outcome, depression doubles heart disease risk, anxiety
increases emergency visits by 300%, and untreated mental
health drives billions in preventable hospitalizations.
From a value-based perspective, behavioral health
integration delivers exceptional ROI: every dollar invested
saves $4 in reduced medical costs, while integrated models
can reduce total healthcare costs by 20-30% through
improved medication adherence and better chronic disease
management. Mental health is also the leading cause of
disability, with depression alone costing over $200 billion
annually in lost productivity. When properly integrated,
behavioral care enables early intervention, reduces costly
crisis situations, improves patient engagement across all
