Introduction
Thank you for the opportunity to respond and provide commentary on the Health Technology Ecosystem
RFI (CMS-0042-NC).
Fourier Health, Inc. (“Fourier”) is a clinician-in-the-loop medical record summarization platform that
transforms unstructured data into structured, interoperable information for physicians, care teams, and
value-based care organizations. Our platform ingests multi-plex unstructured health data, such as faxes,
PDFs, HL7 feeds, HIE-data, and scanned records, etc. and delivers structured summaries tailored to the
clinical priorities of different specialties.
Our mission is to provide every clinician instantly with a full-story of a patient’s medical history,
prior to the provider-patient interaction, so that a patient can receive the best care possible.
As a platform that synthesizes vast quantities of multiplex healthcare data, we are delighted to offer our
perspectives from our experience as a technology platform working with health systems, providers, and
value-based care organizations.
Technology Vendors, Data Providers, and Networks (TD-1, TD-2, TD4-5, TD-19)
Our suggestions to stimulate developer interest in building digital health products include expanding data
access and encouraging use of open, standards-based APIs.
TD-1: Steps CMS Can Take to Stimulate Developer Interest
• Provide Sample Synthetic Data Sets: CMS should publish high-fidelity, synthetically generated
FHIR bundles that mirror real-world Medicare data to reduce onboarding friction for small
developers.

• Establish a Certification Sandbox: A lightweight, no-cost sandbox tied to ONC Health IT
Certification would allow vendors to verify conformance of new APIs and workflows before
production rollout.
• Expand Claims Data Scope: Making Medicare Part C and Part D Explanation-of-Benefits (EOB)
data and prior authorization metadata available through Blue Button 2.0 would unlock richer
longitudinal views for beneficiaries and caregivers.
TD-2: Additional CMS Data Valuable via APIs
• Home Health, Hospice, and DME Claims (HHA, HH PPS, DMEPOS): These data sets are critical
for accurate risk stratification of frail populations.
• Expand Diagnostic Data Scope: Another example, such as LOINC panels could be expanded to
include actual testing results and these data feeds are presently absent from Blue Button 2.0.
TD-4: Encouraging Use of Open, Standards-Based APIs
We support all forms of standardized-APIs, especially when it comes to improvement of clinical outcomes.
The Fourier platform is creating the “ground truth” for every patient prior to the patient stepping into the
room, thus, we encourage CMS to continue to provide and reward polices that advance open information
sharing, including support for actions towards nationwide provider FHIR endpoint directories (TD-5) and
universal patient identifiers (PC-14).
TD-5: Nationwide Directory of FHIR Endpoints
Fourier supports the creation of a nationwide directory. CMS and ONC should jointly maintain an
authoritative directory, leveraging the National Plan & Provider Enumeration System (NPPES) as the
source of truth for National Provider Identifiers and associating each NPI with validated FHIR endpoints
and OAuth scopes.
TD-19: Clinician-Supervised AI Pathway
ONC should create a certification sub-category for clinician-supervised summarization and documentation
tools. Criteria should verify things such as: (a) persistent linkage of every extracted datum to its source
location and (b) a human approval workflow prior to EHR write-back.
Potential Safe-Harbor Alignment: CMS could pair this pathway with a voluntary “Safe Harbor
Clinician-Supervised AI” designation (as an example), akin to sandbox proposals in other submissions,
signaling that provenance-rich platforms meet program-integrity expectations.
Additionally, there could be considerations to also tie TEFCA or FHIR data-access incentives to
downstream usability, not just transmission compliance. There is alignment here with also TD-4 (practical
barriers to data sharing, above) and TD-13 (opportunities providing full HER downstream).
Provider Modernization (PR-2, PR-3)
We are delighted to provide additional thoughts and comments to improve and modernize provider
workflow, along with address our perspectives on what present obstacles that stifle innovation in clinical
applications may be, which include:
• No Standardized Write/Admin APIs: Scheduling, intake auto-population, and provider-directory
endpoints hide behind proprietary throttles, especially given lack of competitive EHR-ecosystem.

• Security Rhetoric as Barriers for Interoperability: Vendors cite “HIPAA risk” to deny write
access even when regulations encourage openness.
• Lack of Reimbursement: CMS can encourage appropriate reimbursement that would incentivize
provider organizations to more readily adopt AI-enabled technologies, especially those that
compensate physician or qualified health-professional time spent reviewing, validating, and acting
on outputs from certified clinician-supervised AI tools.
• Fragmented Identity Management: Providers must juggle multiple credentials and IDs for each
patient or vendor, impeding seamless data pulls.
Value-Based Care Organizations (VB-1-4)
Having a comprehensive patient summary at the point-of-care is incredibly useful for all value-based models
and care delivery. These could be more deeply incentivized across all MSSP, ACO REACH, and MA
programs, as there is alignment with VB-1 (comprehensive patient understanding could encourage more
appropriate financial incentives) and VB-3 (essential health IT capability for value-based arrangements).
A foundational understanding of a patient should be an infrastructure element for any entity operating under
risk. More importantly, it would offer CMS an accurate picture of each patient mitigating potential
downstream financial waste and abuse.
VB-3: Essential Health IT Capabilities for Value-Based Arrangements
• Automated Longitudinal Summaries: With clinician-in-the-loop supervision and attribution to raw
data feeds.
• Prospective Risk Adjustment: Derived from medical records (rather than just claims data) and
integrated into encounter preparation.
• Real-Time HIE Query & Reconciliation: To close care gaps ahead of visits.
VB-4: Essential Data Types for Value-Based Arrangements
• Successful VBC arrangements require timely access to progress notes, imaging narratives,
device observations, and mortality data in addition to claims and lab results.
Conclusion
Fourier provides a platform technology that can help CMS tackle several components and verticals to
improve data usability, quality, and clinical outcomes. We are aware that we are not an encompassing
solution for everything outlined in this RFI. We remain deeply committed to a vision of ensuring all clinical
providers walk into each patient interaction with a “comprehensive and use-case specific chart for every
patient.” Thus, we support initiatives to expand data interoperability, encourage data standardization,
oversight for responsible AI, reduce administrative burden, and enable appropriate resource allocation.
Thank you for considering our comments and feedback. We welcome any questions and look forward to
opportunity to collaborate.
