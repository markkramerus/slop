Digital Health in Medicare and Medicaid Services

In Summary

Why is all of this important?

While modern FHIR and initiatives like TEFCA are making strikes in data availability, HIPAA needs to
evolve to better support patient health.

Regulations are struggling to keep pace with technology. Laboratories present an opportunity to
empower both patients and providers to better manage chronic diseases through actionable
information. Current regulations create roadblocks for laboratories to build a painless billing
experience for patients while providing actionable recommendations to patients and providers.
Interoperability technologies continue to improve data availability, but current rules make it difficult
for laboratories to access the same information as providers. Technologies—like Al-—have the
potential to help patients understand their results, but restrictions prevent its use as a powerful and
empowering tool.

To truly enhance patient care, we must incorporate technology use cases to address payment,
operational, and educational needs. We welcome any opportunity to collaborate on changing
existing regulations or drafting new programs to promote improvements to the US healthcare IT
ecosystem.
