Organization Overview
CCMCN/CHPA is a unified care network focused on improving patient outcomes through
coordinated care management, value-based care, quality improvement, and health technology
integration. We work across Colorado's diverse healthcare landscape to connect providers,
payers, and patients through innovative care coordination platforms and data sharing initiatives.
CCMCN started as a network of 19 Community Health Centers that was founded in 1994 to
respond proactively to the advent of mandatory Medicaid managed care, and evolved to a

multi-faceted organization focusing on managed and accountable care, health information

our new organization strengthens Colorado's community health network through value-based

Key RFI Responses
PR-7: Supporting High-Quality Data Availability
CCMCN/CHPA's Care Coordination Data Challenges:
● Fragmented EHR systems across 200+ provider partners
● Inconsistent data quality and completeness across care settings
● Limited real-time data sharing capabilities
● Complex care transitions requiring manual coordination
● Duplicate data entry burdens on both providers and patients
● Lack of standardized data exchange with non-healthcare entities (justice system,
community-based organizations, other HHS programs)
Strategies for CMS Implementation:
● Standardize care coordination data elements across all provider types
● Incentivize real-time data sharing through quality bonus payments
● Establish interoperability requirements for care management platforms
● Create data quality metrics tied to provider network participation
● Mandate data exchange capabilities with non-healthcare entities including justice
systems, CBOs, and HHS programs
● Ensure claims data is readily available under HIPAA for care coordination
purposes
● Reduce duplicate data entry through automated population of forms across
systems
Burden Mitigation Approaches:
● Automated data extraction from existing EHR workflows
● Shared care coordination platforms reducing duplicate data entry
● Integration with existing quality reporting requirements

TD-6: TEFCA Unique Interoperability Functions
CCMCN/CHPA's TEFCA Implementation Planning: We see TEFCA as essential for our care
coordination mission:
Unique Functions:
● Standardized patient matching across disparate health systems
● Secure care coordination messaging between unaffiliated providers
● Clinical data sharing for care transitions and referrals
● Quality measure data aggregation across multiple networks
● Integration with social services and community organization data systems
● Cross-sector data sharing for comprehensive care coordination
Current Limitations:
● Complex onboarding process for smaller provider practices
● Limited behavioral health provider participation
● Inconsistent implementation across Colorado health systems
● Cost barriers for rural and safety net providers
● Lack of funding mechanisms for HIE participation and maintenance
Recommended Changes:
● Simplified participation pathway for care coordination organizations
● Dedicated funding for rural provider TEFCA implementation
● Behavioral health-specific data sharing protocols
● Integration with existing state health information exchanges
● Government funding for HIEs with provider payment for participation
● Mandatory participation requirements with adequate financial support
