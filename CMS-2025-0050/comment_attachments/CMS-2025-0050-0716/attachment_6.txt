Artificial intelligence (AI), especially generative AI, is already a powerful tool in healthcare, offering amazing potential to responsibly
enhance patient care by improving care outcomes and patient experiences, reducing healthcare provider burnout by simplifying
administrative tasks, and helping to lower the total cost of care.
One of the most helpful ways to see the value of AI in healthcare is to view the question through the lens of the “quadruple aim”
framework. Built on the Institute for Healthcare Improvement’s “triple aim,” a widely accepted compass to optimize health system
performance, the quadruple aim focuses on four key areas where health systems need to be improved, across all of which AI is already
providing, and will continue to provide, value:
• Enhancing population health.
• Improving patient experience, satisfaction, and health outcomes.
• Augmenting clinician and healthcare team experience and satisfaction by reducing administrative burdens.
• Lowering overall costs of healthcare.
The health sector presents a key opportunity for the Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE) to support the President’s DOGE
Agenda by modernizing safe and effective federal technology and software to enhance government efficiency and productivity. In an effort
to address and appropriately manage increasing healthcare costs and provider burden, CHI offers the following use cases and
recommended actions:
