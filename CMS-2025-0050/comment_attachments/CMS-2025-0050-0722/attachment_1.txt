Modernization in Healthcare A vision for improving service delivery while cutting costs | FEDERAL CMS has an opportunity to improve healthcare service delivery, enhance interoperability, and streamline change management processes, ultimately leading to better patient outcomes and reduced costs within the U.S. healthcare system. Multi-channel data collection, agency wide reporting, and AI-powered change management will support CMS's modernization efforts and foster a patient-centered, technology-enabled healthcare ecosystem. 
A Modern Approach to Service Delivery The Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS), the world's premier insurance payor, operates on an unparalleled scale. As the largest processor of healthcare claims globally, CMS is the "big engine" of the healthcare system. With more than 6,000 employees overseeing 1.5 million healthcare providers—every clinician in the country is registered in Medicare. Major payors, hospitals, and health systems leverage the Qualtrics team to understand and optimize the full patient journey across all touchpoints, including CMS regulatory surveys. Similarly, major payors utilize the Qualtrics team to enhance the beneficiary experience at every interaction, including CAHPS and Star Ratings. Despite CMS's immense scale and impact overshadowing these private sector organizations, it currently relies on fragmented, siloed experience management solutions at the office, group, or division level. Like innovative providers and payers, CMS can leverage this future-focused, experience-driven data modernization approach to unify operational and experiential data across Medicare, Medicaid, and ACA programs—enabling real-time insights into beneficiary needs, provider performance, and service gaps. By embedding intelligent feedback loops and automation into its digital infrastructure, CMS can accelerate responsiveness, improve health equity outcomes, and drive continuous improvement aligned with its modernization roadmap. Interoperability Across Your Technology Ecosystem Beyond patient and beneficiary experiences, the Qualtrics team can also gather critical feedback on internal operations, including developer tools, interoperability initiatives, new software implementations, existing software utilization, usage patterns, and training and enablement programs. Additionally, the Qualtrics team boasts robust interoperability with major systems like EHRs, Salesforce, and ServiceNow, along with over 100 other out-of-the-box connectors, ensuring seamless integration within CMS's existing amalgamation of technological tools. Drive Successful Change Management To advance CMS’ data and systems modernization, we propose a Phase 2 initiative following the June 16th Healthcare Ecosystem RFI deadline, anchored in a change management approach that integrates agile principles, customer experience (CX), and design thinking. This phase would focus on conﬁdential engagement with key stakeholders—including RFI respondents, CMS employees, contractors, providers, Medicare Administrative Contractors (MACs), and Quality Improvement Organizations (QIOs)—to gather actionable insights that were limited in the public Phase 1 response. Using secure, role-speciﬁc surveys, we will capture the lived experiences, unmet needs, and systemic pain points of those embedded in CMS programs. This feedback becomes the foundation for iterative, stakeholder-informed change, in which agile methods allow for rapid testing and reﬁnement, design thinking surfaces human-centered solutions, and CX principles ensure measurable improvements in both internal and external service delivery. This integrated change management framework not only accelerates modernization efforts but also increases adoption, reduces resistance, and aligns CMS’s transformation with real-world user needs. Our team is fully equipped to launch this effort without additional investment, seamlessly exporting data into CMS’ data lake or visualizing it through cloud-based dashboards. The insights gained will guide responsive policymaking, optimize workﬂows, and drive a more adaptive and equitable healthcare ecosystem. 2
