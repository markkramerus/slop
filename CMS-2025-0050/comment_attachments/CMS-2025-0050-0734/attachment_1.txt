Dear Administrator Oz and ASTP‑ONC Colleagues,
Bayesian Health is a U.S.‑founded clinical‑AI company spun out of Johns Hopkins in 2018 and backed by
mission‑driven investors (a16z, Obvious Ventures, LifeForce Capital). Our adaptive AI platform already
helps frontline clinicians spot high‑risk patients earlier across sepsis, escalation of care, pressure injury
and readmissions, reducing risk‑adjusted sepsis mortality 18.7 % in a 750,000 patient, multi‑site study¹
while shortening length‑of‑stay and ICU utilization.
Yet the promise of AI is throttled by a chokepoint: large EHR vendors who were entrenched under the
HITECH Act and now act as data and innovation gatekeepers. Their market power lets them:
● Enable standards that lead to 6‑to‑18‑month queues for standard integrations — stalling
deployments that could save Medicare lives and cost now.
● Bypass FDA oversight for their own AI “apps” by classifying them as ONC‑certified modules,
while requiring outside innovators to run the full regulatory gauntlet—an uneven playing field that
suppresses competition and choice.
● Lock data behind proprietary formats and deny modern streaming or webhook access, keeping
hospitals on brittle nightly batch feeds and costly interface‑engineer labor.
The result is de‑facto regulatory capture: rules written to digitize records now inhibit innovation and
increase total cost of care. Medicare beneficiaries pay the price in missed early treatments, longer stays,
and avoidable mortality.

Context: Why Action Is Urgent
● Sepsis is the #1 inpatient cause of death and most expensive condition. Earlier AI‑triggered
care saves thousands of Medicare lives⁶.
● Workforce shortages will add $170 B in costs by 2027; nurses are the least experienced on
record⁷. Automation and timely decision‑support are critical.
● Evidence is mature. Multi‑site Bayesian deployment cut sepsis mortality 18.7 %; ICU RCT
showed −20 % LOS and −58 % mortality vs usual care⁴; for patients with septic shock, every hour
of delayed antibiotic administration increases mortality by **8 %**⁹.

Detailed Policy Requests
I. Provider & Technology/Data‑Vendor Lane
● PR‑1 — Quality‑Linked Payment for Validated AI
Ask: Create an NTAP‑style add‑on tied to peer‑reviewed outcome data.
Evidence: Bayesian reduced sepsis mortality by 18.7% while reducing total cost of care
(Nature Medicine 2022)¹.
● PR‑2 — Interoperability Guardrails to Break Regulatory Capture
Ask: Condition future ONC certification on: (a) streaming FHIR APIs; (b) zero‑cost access to
synthetic‑data sandboxes; (c) non‑discriminatory pricing for production APIs.
Rationale: Restores competitive neutrality and accelerates Medicare‑benefiting tools.
● PR‑3 — Modern Web‑Based EMR & Web‑Hooks Standard
Ask: Endorse React/Vue‑based EMRs exposing REST/GraphQL APIs and outbound web‑hooks
for CDS.
Rationale: Aligns with CI/CD best practices, slashes manual test cycles.
II. Value‑Based Care Lane
● VB‑1 — AI Response Metrics in HVBP
Ask: Add “risk‑adjusted sepsis mortality” and “AI‑verified time‑to‑treatment < 1 hr” as
quality measures.
Evidence: ML alerts cut LOS −2.7 days and mortality −58 %⁴; each hour of antibiotic delay
increases mortality 8 % in septic shock⁹.
Conclusion
Accelerating responsible AI adoption is no longer optional — it is essential to sustain access, quality, and
equity for Medicare beneficiaries while increasing the return on our tax dollars, however, regulatory
capture of the HITECH act is stymieing growth in this important area.
Bayesian Health welcomes further dialogue to demonstrate our platform and/or partner on
next‑generation standards.
