I serve as the primary caregiver for my brother, my mother, and my father, each presenting unique

My Brother: Navigating Non-Verbal Autism, Epilepsy, and Medication Side Effects
My brother is a mute, Level 3 autistic individual with intellectual and developmental delays, and he
experiences grand mal epilepsy. He requires almost around-the-clock care. Two of his medications
exacerbate his autism-related behavioral issues and negatively impact his sleep quality.
I've observed a cyclical pattern in his behavioral issues.
(PC-1, PC-1.a) To better understand and potentially manage these medication side effects, it would be
incredibly helpful to have a health management application that helps me systematically track:
● Mood fluctuations
● Dietary intake
● Physical activity levels
● Sleep quality
● Medication timings
(PC-2.b) Furthermore, connecting this behavioral and lifestyle data with his regular blood test results
(necessitated by his medications) and his medical records would provide crucial insights into his overall
well-being. This integrated approach is vital, especially given his inability to communicate his needs or
feelings verbally.
My Mother: Overcoming Language Barriers in Healthcare
My 70-year-old mother's inability to speak English creates significant obstacles in navigating the
healthcare system. My father's English proficiency isn't sufficient to advocate for her. While translators are
an option, the slow translation process consumes valuable appointment time, leaving physicians with less
time to address her needs.
Before each appointment, I dedicate about 45 minutes to gather and translate her health information
from Korean to English, then organize it for effective communication with the doctor. During
appointments, I'm constantly translating for my mother while simultaneously trying to comprehend the
doctor's instructions. I record appointments because the intensity of translating often leads me to miss
crucial information. Later, I use AI to summarize these recordings.
(PC-1) It would be tremendously beneficial to have a digital solution that could translate for her in real time
so her interactions with the health system could be more helpful to her.

My Father: Addressing Inaccurate Reporting and Non-Compliance
My father is moderately fluent in English, but his understanding is just poor enough that he doesn't realize
what information he's missing or misinterpreting. Consequently, the information he provides to physicians
about his health is often inaccurate.
Unlike my mother, he frequently exhibits non-compliance with care plans. He often misrepresents his
exercise habits, medication adherence, and how he's feeling physically (always claiming to feel "great").
This tendency to present himself in a positive light to his doctor, even with inaccurate information, leads to
ineffective care plans. This inevitably forces me to contact the doctor via the EHR to request revisions.
These revised plans are often misinterpreted by support staff who don't know my father, requiring me to
attend subsequent appointments to ensure the information is accurate and appropriate.
(PC-1) I would greatly benefit from a tool that allows my insights and knowledge as a caregiver to be
incorporated into my father's doctor visits without my physical presence, reducing the need for multiple
appointments. Additionally, a product that tracks his physician-relevant information would be invaluable,
eliminating his need to relay potentially inaccurate details and mitigating any embarrassment he might feel
regarding his non-compliance.

Spouse of Nuna Employee (Anonymous)
I’ve been a professional care advocate for over 3 decades. I primarily assist families with elderly parents
who are experiencing care episodes and work to ensure care continuity and coordinate after discharge. In
my experience, there are some key pain points that are potentially addressable with digital health
solutions.
The hospital discharge process is poorly coordinated with family members
● The timing of discharge, and the supporting patient discharge criteria is not clearly communicated
during an admission, frequently leading to an unexpected and chaotic discharge process for
patients and families and a poor post-acute experience
● Personalized patient discharge instructions are not clearly communicated to patients or caregivers,
leaving families with unclear next steps for care.
● The process for assessing the patient’s home environment, specifically identifying new patient
accessibility needs that require home modifications, is often left to the patient’s family with little
guidance
(PC-1) It would be helpful to have a digital tool that would electronically communicate discharge
information to patient’s families, including
● Clinical Discharge criteria and current status vs criteria
● Anticipated timing of discharge
● Personalized, continually updated discharge instructions - based on patients specific post
discharge environment (home or Assisted Living or SNF) and patient’s clinical history and
preferenced
Medication reconciliation is uncoordinated and poorly managed post-discharge
Reconciliation of patients “pre hospitalization” medications with “post discharge” medications often
requires scheduling of a post hospitalization PCP visit which can take days to schedule and create a
burden to patient and caregivers. It is often difficult to physically get the patient to an appointment merely
to adjust medications.
(PC-1) It would be helpful to have clear medication instructions, both post discharge medications and pre
discharge medications, as well as remote monitoring of patient after discharge to avoid an unnecessary
“face to face” visit or ED visit.
It is difficult to get detailed information about the patient’s hospital stay post discharge
After discharge, It is extremely difficult for patients to get information regarding their hospital stay. It’s a
cumbersome process and “it is like you never existed.”
(PC-1) It would be helpful to have a tool that gives patients and care-givers easy access to their hospital
records, before discharge, accompanied by an easy-to-understand summary of what occurred during the
hospitalization, including clear and concise follow up instructions regarding medications, future clinical
needs, therapy notes (while hospitalized, etc).
