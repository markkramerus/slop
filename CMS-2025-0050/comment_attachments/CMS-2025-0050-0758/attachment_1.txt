Our submission is focused on patient digital tools and a scalable database to help operationalize MAHA and empower Medicare beneficiaries (with their caregivers) to achieve their evidence-based health and well-being goals. To help patients focus on and be accountable for health goals and lifestyle and well-being behaviors that prevent and treat chronic diseases, we propose a patient health/well-being record (and platform) that shares data with, while being separate from, EHRs which continue as medical records and platforms that support physician/provider teams and care. A Medicare patient health and well-being record would support patient privacy and enable patients to focus on their roles in improving health and well-being supported by Medicare services: 1. Support patient engagement in Medicare services that empower patients to achieve their health and well-being goals, including CCM, Health and Well-being Coaching (HWC); RPM, MDPP, Shared Medical Visits; 2. Provide digital coaching/self-coaching tools and educational resources for lifestyle medicine and well-being to prevent and treat chronic diseases; 3. Populate a Medicare patient database that tracks Medicare services, Medicare claims, patient data and outcomes, in order to evaluate and improve cost effectiveness and quality of Medicare services that support patients in achieving their health and well-being goals.
6/16/25 3Existing digital health technology platforms (such as those currently in use by INTERVENT and Avidon in collaboration with Wellcoaches) can be enhanced by industry following CMS guidelines, similar to EHR standards. Industry or CMS or both together could design, develop, and oversee a national, secure and interoperable database for all Medicare patient data, which we discuss below. The proven digital health technology platform developed by INTERVENT and Avidon in collaboration with Wellcoaches has been widely deployed in multi-center NIH/PCORI-funded clinical trials involving patients (many are 65 or older) from 140 medical centers/healthcare systems. The platform is being deployed at an early stage in medical practices, supporting NBHWC-certified coaches delivering Medicare- reimbursed CCM and RPM services in accordance with regulatory requirements, and supports group coaching programs (similar to MDPP). For additional details, we included a book chapter with our submission: Gordon NF, et al. Using digital health technology to promote cardiovascular disease risk reduction in secondary prevention. What features are most important to make digital health products accessible and easy to use for Medicare beneficiaries and caregivers, particularly those with limited prior experience using digital tools and services? We recommend that digital tools for Medicare beneficiaries and caregivers are designed with the following features: 1.Simple, Intuitive Interface a. Large, high-contrast text and icons b. Minimal navigation steps with clear call-to-action buttons c. Streamlined dashboards that avoid information overload 2.Guided Onboarding and Tutorials a. Step-by-step walkthroughs upon first login b. Tooltips and help prompts to explain features in real time c. Optional video or audio instructions to support different learning styles 3.Mobile and Tablet Optimization
