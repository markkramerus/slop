Ellipsis Health ("Ellipsis") appreciates this opportunity to respond to the Health Technology

care, and rising chronic disease.
Ellipsis’ solution, “Sage,” is an empathetic, AI-powered care management agent. Sage extends

addresses current systemic weaknesses by:
● Automating administrative tasks: Freeing clinicians for higher-value patient care.

2. Support Providers and Payors in Reducing Clinical and Administrative
Burdens (PR)
Challenge: Today's healthcare system is characterized by fragmented patient journeys, limited
access to primary and specialty care, and obstacles to promoting healthy lifestyles. Economic
2

realities and clinician shortages lead to thin margins and staff burnout, exacerbated by
time-consuming administrative tasks and disparate data systems.
Ellipsis Solution: Sage Streamlines Operations & Extends Care Capacity Ellipsis Health’s
Sage acts as an AI care manager, coalescing disparate information to provide a seamless,
integrated care coordination experience. Sage streamlines healthcare operations by automating
routine administrative tasks such as appointment scheduling, reminders, medical record
management, billing, and preliminary health assessments. This significantly reduces the
workload on human staff, allowing clinicians to practice at the top of their license. Sage operates
24/7, checking physical and behavioral health, discussing medication, managing symptoms,
providing next steps, and documenting interactions. In emergencies, Sage can escalate calls to
live nurses or emergency lines. (PR-2; PC-1).
Sage can handle a myriad of diverse tasks seamlessly, including verifying identity, introducing
the care management program, checking insurance and employer eligibility, as well as
programmatic appropriateness, capturing recent labs and meds, checking the ability to wear a
fitness tracker, scheduling a lab appointment, and scheduling onboarding visits all while keeping
the patient engaged. In an internal study conducted in partnership with a specialty care
management company, Sage was able to complete a typical patient onboarding workflow taking
an average member outreach specialist six weeks to fill a clinical staff caseload, in one week
given Sage’s ability to simultaneously conduct almost unlimited inbound and outbound calls to
enroll members. Sage also was able to complete all onboarding tasks during an enrollment call
at a rate that was 39% higher than the average teammate. Lastly, Sage was able to complete
clinical intake calls as part of this study in less than 20 minutes on average compared to an
average call taking 32 minutes before the partnership between Ellipsis Health and the specialty
care management company. Public studies confirm similar outcomes - one study indicated that
AI chatbots can cut consultation wait times by 15% and handle up to 80% of routine queries,
with one orthopedic practice saving over 95 hours of phone time by offloading inquiries to an AI
chatbot.1 This efficiency is critical for financially and operationally strained environments. (PR-2).
Recommended Actions to Reduce Burden and Foster AI Adoption:
● Establish AI-Focused Reimbursement Codes: CMS should create new
reimbursement codes or modify existing ones (e.g., Chronic Care Management (CCM),
Transitional Care Management (TCM), Principal Care Management (PCM), Remote
Patient Monitoring (RPM) to specifically cover AI-enhanced care management services.
This ensures the financial viability of providers adopting these solutions (PR-1a; PR-4).
● Mitigate Regulatory Burdens for Medicare Advantage (MA) Plans: CMS should
clarify and mitigate any regulatory obstacles that prevent MA plans from reimbursing AI
care management services, encouraging broader adoption. (PR-1a).
● Develop Medicaid Guidance: CMS should issue comprehensive guidance to state
Medicaid agencies on how to best leverage, reimburse, and ensure the safety of AI care
management solutions, supporting access for low-income beneficiaries. (PR-1a).

3. Expanding Access & Empowering Patients through Personalized
Prevention (PC)
Challenge: Healthcare access remains uneven, particularly for high-need groups and those in
rural areas. Many Medicare beneficiaries lack experience with digital tools, and existing
solutions often fail to meet patients where they are, hindering engagement with preventative
care. Chronic diseases pose a significant health burden in the U.S., affecting 60% of Americans
with at least one condition and 40% with multiple conditions, as reported by the CDC. These
conditions, encompassing heart disease, cancer, diabetes, and obesity, are major contributors
to mortality and morbidity. (PC-1; PC-6; PC-8).
Ellipsis Solution: Sage Delivers Accessible, Empathic, Preventative Support Sage extends
the reach, scale, and scope of healthcare service providers and delivers a more patient-centric,
empathetic experience that meets patients where they are. Sage fundamentally alters patient
access and responsiveness. Functioning 24/7 with rapid response times, Sage interacts with
patients via traditional phone calls, making it accessible even for those without broadband or
digital literacy. It provides support in common US languages (English, Spanish, and more) and
integrates regional dialects for maximum engagement. Sage’s empathetic, multimodal
conversational capabilities foster trust, making patients comfortable discussing their health.
(PC-6; PR-1).
Sage focuses on preventative care and patient self-management. It leverages key patient data,
including medical history, symptoms, and lifestyle information, to provide personalized health
navigation and chronic care management services. This enables tailored advice and
interventions, critical for effective chronic disease management and promotion of healthy
behaviors. Sage actively encourages behavioral modifications, supports self-management of
diverse health conditions, and offers ongoing guidance, such as promoting adherence to dietary,
exercise, or medication regimens through targeted behavioral nudges. Sage also helps patients
by surfacing relevant healthcare data, scheduling medical and lab appointments, and translating
health insurance benefits into easy-to-understand options for services.
As the Wall Street Journal recently noted, Sage is tailored for patients with specific diseases
such as diabetes and cancer. The capability of Sage to deliver personalized care and behavioral
prompts empowers patients in their health journey, fostering a patient-centric model wherein
individuals receive consistent support for the proactive management of their own health. This
4

continuous support can lead to more sustained improvements in health outcomes compared to
episodic interventions. For example, a study indicates that 61% of patients reported AI chatbots
aided symptom self-management, and patients found conversational AI engaging for
self-management support.2 (PC-1; PC-8).
Studies confirm AI care managers improve patient engagement (up to 30% increase) and care
plan adherence (as high as 97%)3. Patients report enhanced clarity. A study found that patients
reported greater clarity of information (3.73 vs. 3.62 out of 4, p<0.05) and overall satisfaction
(4.58 vs. 4.42 out of 5, p < 0.05) with AI-assisted conversations compared to standard care,
while maintaining equivalent levels of trust and perceived empathy.4 Furthermore, AI-based
healthcare chatbots enhance condition management, promote healthy behaviors, and support
mental health, increasing medication adherence by 15%.5 Sage’s continuous support leads to
more sustained health improvements and can significantly reduce hospital readmissions (by up
to 25% in some studies)6, shifting care from reactive to proactive. (PC-1; PC-5).
Recommended Actions to Expand Access and Empower Patients:

re-evaluations. A framework similar to the Transitional Coverage of Emerging
Technologies (TCET) for FDA-approved breakthrough devices would be highly
advantageous. (PC-5a; TD-1).
● Launch Nationwide Awareness and Education Initiatives: Implement programs to
raise awareness and provide education on credible digital health solutions for Medicare
and Medicaid beneficiaries and caregivers, focusing on rural and underserved areas.
This will build trust and encourage adoption. (PC-5).
● Incentivize Preventative AI: Explore incentives or bonus programs within existing CMS
models (e.g., Medicare Shared Savings Program, CMMI models) for providers and
ACOs that effectively deploy AI-driven preventative outreach and self-management
tools, linking their use to improved health outcomes and cost savings. (VB-1).
● Support a Unique Patient Identifier: CMS should support the creation and adoption of
a universal patient identifier. A UPI would be transformative by providing everyone with

4. Accelerating Interoperability (TD)
Challenge: The US healthcare system’s historical fragmentation and data siloing hinder quality,
access, and cost efficiency. Proprietary legacy systems create barriers to AI integration and
patients’ ability to access their own health data. The "last mile" implementation problem,
including mismatched data standards, impedes the realization of AI’s benefits. (TD-7; TD-13;
TD-19).
Ellipsis Solution: Sage Leverages Interoperability for Coordinated Care. While Sage does
not require comprehensive EHR data to be effective (only basic demographics are necessary), it
generates structured data that can be seamlessly shared with providers and integrated into
EHRs. Sage’s efficacy is enhanced by accessing a broader range of patient data for care
navigation and management. (TD-4; PR-3).
Ellipsis advocates for regulatory standards that mandate EHR manufacturers and payers to
adopt FHIR-based APIs and to advance bulk FHIR API capabilities, enabling efficient sharing of
a patient's comprehensive health record. This will improve Sage’s ability to integrate into EHRs,
access more patient data, and provide personalized services. (TD-10; TD-15; PA-5).
Recommended Actions to Accelerate Interoperability:
● Enforce and Expand FHIR-Based API Mandates: CMS and ONC must ensure the
timely implementation and rigorous enforcement of existing regulations requiring
FHIR-based API adoption. Build on these regulations to require widespread FHIR API
adoption across all sectors of the healthcare system (e.g., laboratories, imaging centers,
pharmacies). (TD-4; PA-2).
● Advance Full Electronic Health Information (EHI) Exchange: Accelerate efforts to
make all aspects of a patient’s health information interoperable, including clinical notes,
free text, health insurance information, emergency contacts, social determinants of
health (SDOH), problem and medication lists, laboratory and diagnostic results, care
events and encounters, and upcoming and missed appointments. This includes
supporting bulk FHIR APIs for cohort-based patient outreach. (TD-13; TD-15).
● Leverage EHR Certification for Standards Adoption: Continue to leverage the ONC
Health IT Certification Program to drive the adoption of USCDI and FHIR standards,
dismantling barriers erected by legacy EHR manufacturers. This should include
tightening certification criteria to ensure APIs cover "all aspects of the patient's chart,"
6

including unstructured data, possibly through emerging text or image exchange
standards. (TD-2, TD-7; TD-8; TD-9, PC-9).
● Support a Nationwide Provider Directory and TEFCA: Establish a standardized,
nationwide provider directory of FHIR endpoints to streamline connections between
systems. Continue building on existing efforts to implement the Trusted Exchange
Framework and Common Agreement (TEFCA) to address longstanding interoperability
and data exchange challenges, and provide support to independent healthcare providers
for connecting to HIEs and TEFCA. (TD-5; PC-10; PR-6).
● Enhance Healthcare Price Transparency: Continue building on existing efforts to
advance healthcare price transparency to support digital patient navigation solutions like
Sage, enabling patients to make informed, affordable healthcare choices. (TD-19).
● Broaden Data-Sharing Mandates for APM Entities: HHS should consider broadening
data-sharing mandates to cover APM entities. For example, when a beneficiary in an
ACO is admitted to any hospital, ensure the ACO's care team is notified (perhaps via the
QHIN/TEFCA network or other intermediaries). This would empower AI agents or other
tools to react promptly, such as calling the patient post-discharge, regardless of
hospitalization location. (VB-8; PR-7).
5. Integrating Behavioral Health and Driving Value-Based Care Success
(VB)
Challenge: The US faces a significant behavioral health crisis, with mental health inequities
contributing substantially to avoidable healthcare expenditures, projected to reach $1.3 trillion
by 2040.7 Workforce shortages impede access to behavioral health services, and co-morbid
mental/physical conditions amplify healthcare costs. Value-Based Care (VBC) organizations
often struggle with care gap closure and leveraging new technologies effectively to manage
populations and outcomes. (VB-2; VB-3; VB-4).
Ellipsis Solution: Sage Proactively Addresses Behavioral Health & Optimizes VBC Ellipsis
has developed a proprietary vocal biomarker integrated into Sage’s patient interactions. This
technology detects the severity of depression and anxiety symptoms through semantic and
acoustic analysis of spoken language, proactively identifying mental health needs. In a research
study in press at JMIR AI (included as an attachment), Ellipsis’ vocal biomarker demonstrated
high accuracy in predicting depression severity across more than 2,086 recordings of case
management calls with verbally administered PHQ-9 surveys of Highmark Blue Cross Blue
Shield members. As part of the study, the recordings were divided into a Development set
(n=1336) and Blind set (n=671) and PHQ-8 scores were provided for the Development set for
vocal biomarker refinement while PHQ-8 scores from the Blind set were withheld until after the
vocal biomarker model severity output was reported. The Concordance Correlation Coefficient
(CCC) for the test of the vocal biomarker model on the Development set was 0.57 and for the
Blind set 0.54, while the Mean Absolute Error (MAE) for the Development set was 3.91 and for
the Blind set was 4.06, demonstrating strong model performance. Overall, the findings suggest

For Value-Based Care, Sage provides a scalable, cost-effective solution for patient-centered,
longitudinal care management crucial for reducing unnecessary emergency department (ED)
visits and inpatient admissions. Sage’s capabilities include:
● Data Aggregation: This utilizes AI to consolidate diverse datasets (claims, clinical,
demographic, behavioral, socioeconomic, patient-reported) into a readily consumable
format for providers.
● Dynamic Care Planning: Enhances the frequency and quality of patient interactions,
generating concise summaries and recommending actions based on clinical protocols,
thereby augmenting the care team.
● Care Gap Closure: Scales patient outreach and facilitates empathetic interactions to
systematically address care gaps, leading to enhanced performance on provider quality
measures, as well as Stars and HEDIS metrics. (VB-3; VB-11).
Recommended Actions to Integrate Behavioral Health and Incentivize VBC Success:
● Reimbursement for AI-Enabled Behavioral Health: CMS should develop payment
codes for behavioral health-related screening, diagnosis, and general integration
services provided by AI care managers, including those using vocal biomarker
technology. These codes should be billable alongside existing care management and
remote patient monitoring codes. (VB-13).
● Incentivize Vocal Biomarkers in Value-Based Care: Explore how incentives for vocal
biomarker technology can support the success of value-based care initiatives, and
examine scalable value-based payment mechanisms for this technology. (VB-13).
● Expand Contracting Opportunities for Digital AI Tools: Expand contracting
opportunities for digital AI tools to support participants across CMS value-based care
programs. Standardize and implement incentives for digital health adoption across CMMI
models. (VB-1; VB-7).
● Pilot AI in Value-Based Care Models: CMMI should pilot incentives for Accountable
Care Organizations (ACOs) and other Alternative Payment Models (APMs) to leverage
AI care managers, potentially designating preferred vendors or directly procuring
services to leverage CMS’s scale. CMS could also facilitate bulk procurement or
deployment of proven AI solutions for smaller ACOs who might not otherwise afford
them, similar to how CMS has offered subsidized tools in the past. (VB-1).

● Promote Coordination of Physical and Mental Health: Develop policy to explicitly
support and incentivize the adoption of effective biomarker technologies, particularly
among participants in value-based care models, to advance primary care-behavioral
health integration. Coordinate regulations across health care agencies (CMS,
ONC/ASTP, FDA) to facilitate access to proven biomarker technologies. (VB-2; VB-8).
● Allow Substitution of CEHRT Functions with Certified AI: CMS could allow
substituting certain CEHRT functions with certified AI capabilities. If an APM can
demonstrate that a certified AI agent performs a function (e.g., patient education, care
planning documentation) as effectively as a CEHRT module, that should count toward
meeting technology use requirements, creating an alternative compliance pathway for
innovative non-EHR solutions. (VB-8).
● Provide Performance Evaluation Credit for Digital Health Engagement: CMS should
consider incentivizing the adoption of digital health coaching among ACOs and other
APM participants and subsequently evaluate how digital health coaching impacts patient
outcomes and programmatic spending in MSSP and other programs. For example, CMS
could provide a bonus for ACOs which have a higher percentage of their patients
engaging with digital health coaching and more closely evaluate how this engagement
impacts quality scores and total cost of care. (VB-1).
6. Conclusion
Ellipsis Health applauds CMS’s goal of accelerating the adoption of safe and proven health
technologies. The substantial increase in healthcare-focused AI adoption, with over 70% of
healthcare organizations incorporating them and market valuations anticipated to reach $10.26
billion by 2034, underscores a compelling value proposition driven by systemic pressures: rising
chronic diseases, an aging demographic, and healthcare workforce shortages. AI care
management models, such as Sage, provide scalable digital solutions to mitigate these
pressures, optimizing healthcare professionals' time allocation and alleviating burnout. This
enhances quality and is essential to sustain and elevate care delivery.
Ellipsis Health strongly encourages CMS to assume leadership in facilitating trustworthy,
empathetic AI care management agents capable of enhancing prevention and mitigating the
burden of chronic disease. Sage is today demonstrating positive outcomes for Medicare
beneficiaries and their care teams. Through clear regulatory support, targeted incentives, and
accelerated interoperability, CMS can catalyze the future of AI-powered chronic care
management that improves quality and patient experience while reducing costs.
