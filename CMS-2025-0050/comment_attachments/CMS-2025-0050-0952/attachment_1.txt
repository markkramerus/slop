 Health Technology Ecosystem RFI Unlocking Blue Button 2.0's Hidden Potential Through Better Discoverability Questions addressed: TD–1. What short term (in the next 2 years) and longer-term steps can CMS take to stimulate developer interest in building digital health products for Medicare beneﬁciaries and caregivers? Understanding of the problem space: Hospitals and clinics across America are paying thousands of dollars monthly for medication history data that CMS already provides for free. A typical hospital might spend $50,000 annually on SureScripts or similar services to check if patients are taking their prescribed medications—unaware that Blue Button 2.0 oﬀers the same Medicare Part D data at no cost. To compound this, commercial services often miss prescriptions from small pharmacies that can't aﬀord to participate, leaving gaps in medication histories. This is more of a marketing problem to solve than a technology one. Blue Button 2.0 has operated quietly since 2018, serving over 60 million Medicare beneﬁciaries' claims data through a modern API. Yet many EHR vendors, health systems, and developers don't understand what data it contains or how to use it. The result? We think medication adherence checks go unmade, drug interactions get missed, and providers make decisions without complete information— while some pay premium prices for commercial alternatives. Solution(s): CMS should consider actively promoting Blue Button 2.0's capabilities in a broad reaching manner. Start with targeted outreach to EHR vendors like Epic, Cerner, and athenahealth, showing them exactly how to replace expensive third-party medication history services with free Blue Button data. CMS could create compelling demos that show real medication reconciliation workﬂows, complete with code samples to implement. We think Blue Button's online presence could transform from a technical documentation site to a vibrant marketplace, one that features success stories from organizations already using the data. It could house interactive demos where developers can see actual medication histories, claims patterns, and coverage information. Most importantly, it can compare BB 2.0 comprehensive Medicare data against commercial alternatives, highlighting its coverage and cost. CMS should also consider launching an awareness campaign through channels healthcare IT professionals actually use. If it doesn’t already, it should sponsor sessions at HIMSS and Health 2.0 conferences, publish articles in JAMIA and Healthcare IT News showing concrete ROI from switching to Blue Button, and create a partnership with medical societies to reach practicing physicians who can encourage their IT departments to investigate free alternatives. Providers would catch more drug interactions and identify medication non-adherence faster. Emergency departments would also be able access complete medication lists for safer, quicker decisions. For CMS, better discoverability creates network eﬀects—each EHR integration reaches thousands of providers, attracting more developers and accelerating adoption.
