Challenges to digital identity implementation:
Interoperability Issues: Lack of standardization across digital identity solutions (e.g., CLEAR, Login.gov, ID.me) can create integration challenges, which can be addressed
by instituting a common standard (e.g., WC3, OpenID, FIDO).
User Adoption: Complex identity verification processes can deter users, especially those with limited technical literacy.
Privacy Concerns: Centralized identity systems may raise concerns about data breaches or misuse of personal information. This risk can be mitigated by using
decentralized digital identity (DID) systems that manage data ownership and access rights using blockchain and privacy-enhancing technologies (PET).

Benefits of digital identity implementation:

° Enhanced Security: Digital identity credentials reduce fraud and unauthorized access to public benefit systems by ensuring robust identity verification.

° Streamlined Access & Exchange: Patients, providers, and other stakeholders can securely access and share data across systems in accordance with strict data ownership
and governance controls, improving efficiency and auditability.

° Patient Empowerment: Patients gain greater control over their data and can use a single identity to access eligible services.

How requiring digital identity credentials would impact cybersecurity and data exchange:
° Cybersecurity: Stronger identity verification reduces the risk of unauthorized access and fraud. However, centralized credential systems are high-value targets for

cyberattacks, so systems with robust security measures (e.g., blockchain-backed verification), are essential.
Data Exchange:

Positive: Digital identity credentials enable secure, standardized authentication across systems, improving interoperability and trust.
. Negative: Implementation costs and technical barriers may limit adoption, particularly for smaller organizations.

Impact of requiring use of the OpenID Connect identity protocol:
° Adoption: OpenID Connect is widely adopted, so private sector companies will likely be able to continue working with CMS with minimal or no re-development. This
would streamline implementation, reduce technical complexity, and foster broader participation.

Built for Auth/Auth: While OpenID Connect is ideal for user authentication and authorization, it is not designed as a representation of a person’s comprehensive digital
identity (e.g., a full digital twin or personal knowledge graph). Blockchain-based solutions like LifeGraph can augment OpenID to authenticate identity based ona

person’s full digital persona, not just a username/password combination. This can significantly enhance security while maintaining data privacy.
